全文摘要
最佳版本14 票兒童心臟瓣膜置換的根本死結，是植入的瓣膜不會跟著孩子一起長大，孩子必須反覆開胸手術換更大顆的瓣膜。作者用細胞自己長出來的膠原蛋白，做成三根管子拼成的瓣膜（tri-tube valve），把它縫進幼羔羊的肺動脈裡。追蹤一年的結果顯示，這顆瓣膜會被宿主細胞重新長滿、會跟著羊一起變大、也幾乎沒有出現傳統瓣膜常見的鈣化變硬。

兒童心臟瓣膜置換的常見麻煩，在於植入進去的瓣膜不會跟著孩子一起長大。目前的動物組織瓣膜被化學藥劑固定過後，組織等於是死的，沒辦法生長、還容易鈣化變硬；機械瓣膜則是固定尺寸的硬零件。孩子心臟長大但瓣膜不會跟著長大，於是必須反覆執行開胸手術換更大顆的瓣膜，有些孩子要動五次以上。

為了解決這個死結，作者改用細胞自己長出來的活材料。他們把產生膠原蛋白的細胞 (fibroblast) 包進管狀凝膠模具裡培養兩個月，這些細胞會在管子裡慢慢編織出一根排列整齊的緻密膠原蛋白小管，也就是細胞外基質 (ECM)。之後再用清潔劑把細胞全部洗掉，只留下這層空的膠原蛋白骨架。這樣做的好處是，植入後病人自己的細胞會爬上去把骨架重新長滿，既不會被免疫系統排斥，這個骨架也能在實驗室冷藏保存至少半年備用。

為了用這種小管做出一顆瓣膜，作者一路改了兩代設計。前一版叫「管中管」(tube-in-tube)，把一根膠原蛋白小管塞進另一根大管裡、再用三條縫線抓出三片瓣膜形狀（請看論文 Fig. 1D），但缺點是三個瓣膜接合點 (commissure) 完全靠縫線承受瓣膜關閉時往下拉的力，縫線拉久了內管會從縫線處慢慢滑下去，瓣膜高度變短而漏血。新版「三管狀」(tri-tube) 改成三根膠原蛋白小管平行併排圍成圓環、相鄰處互相縫合，每根管子的下半部各自封口、自然形成一片瓣膜（請看 Fig. 1C）。這樣下拉力改由三根管子的膠原蛋白本體直接承擔，縫線不再是主要受力者，接合點就不會被拉開。

新版設計畫好後，真正的考驗才開始。作者把這顆三管狀瓣膜（第一代，Gen 1）縫進四隻幼羔羊心臟的肺動脈位置，每隔幾週用超音波追蹤三個指標：瓣膜的內徑變化、有沒有血液倒流、以及血流被擋住時的壓力差。瓣膜剛縫進去時直徑 19 mm，理想情況是隨羔羊長大、被撐大到約 25 mm 的成年羊肺動脈尺寸。但實驗發現，第一代四隻羊有兩隻在 12 和 20 週就出狀況：瓣膜直徑突然暴衝、三片葉片的接合點被拉開、血液開始倒流，只好提前取出。原因是瓣膜外圍的管壁長得比裡面的三片葉片快太多，葉片大小跟不上，三片葉片就沒辦法在中央碰在一起完全閉合，血液於是從中間縫隙倒流回心室。

為了拖慢外圍管壁的成長速度，讓葉片有時間跟上，第二代 (Gen 2) 在三管狀瓣膜外面再多包一層膠原蛋白管當「袖套」(sleeve)，順便給接合點多一層保險。Gen 2 三隻羊裡有兩隻順利撐到目標的 52 週：瓣膜直徑穩定在約 25 mm、只有輕微的血液回流、血流被擋住時的壓力差也低於 5 mmHg。第三隻撐到 44 週都還很好，只是到了 52 週直徑超標長到 27 mm，回流加重到中度，顯示袖套還沒完全壓制根部長太快的問題。

取出瓣膜切片觀察，外圍管壁長滿了宿主細胞，葉片表面也漸漸被細胞覆蓋、甚至從葉片底部往上長出一層正常的血管內襯細胞，代表原本空的膠原蛋白骨架真的被宿主住了進去。鈣化是傳統動物組織瓣膜變硬失效的主因，作者用染色檢查鈣的沉積，發現 Gen 2 瓣膜葉片只有零星細小點狀的微鈣化，沒有大塊鈣化。對照組的牛瓣膜在同樣的羔羊模型裡植入才 11 週就嚴重鈣化變厚而失效，換算下來這顆瓣膜的鈣化程度比商用牛瓣膜低了約 33 倍。

這項研究只在七隻幼羔羊身上追蹤大約一年，樣本量很小。瓣膜放在壓力較低的肺動脈那一側，壓力高出許多的主動脈位置能不能撐住，這個實驗回答不了。整段追蹤期間羊每天都打抗凝血劑來避免血栓，現實中要多久才能安全停掉抗凝血藥物，目前還不知道。

把結論外推到真正的兒童身上時，還有兩條不確定性要記住。羔羊肺動脈在這一年內長大的速度遠快於兒童，所以第一代後期看到的「根部長太快」失效模式，搬到真正的兒童身上時間軸會拉長許多，未必那麼快出問題。另外，葉片內部細胞長進的速度比表面慢，更久之後是否能完全長滿目前還無法回答。

兒童心臟瓣膜置換的死結在於植入的瓣膜不會長大。現有的兩類瓣膜都不行：動物組織瓣膜被化學藥劑泡過、「定型」後失去生長能力，還容易鈣化變硬；機械瓣膜本身是金屬零件，更不可能長大。但孩子的心臟自己會長大、肺動脈的口徑也會跟著變寬：從出生時直徑約 12 mm 長到青春期約 24 mm，瓣膜若卡在原本尺寸，血流就被一個太小的孔擋住，逼孩子在成長期反覆開胸換更大顆的瓣膜，有時要動五次以上，成年後改裝機械瓣膜還必須終生服用抗凝血藥。

作者用實驗室培養的細胞長出一種可植入的活組織支架。他們把產生膠原蛋白的細胞 (fibroblast) 包在管狀凝膠裡，灌進一根直徑 16 mm 的玻璃管模具，培養約八週後細胞會在管壁長出緻密、排列整齊的膠原蛋白支架材料 (ECM)。接著用清潔劑把細胞洗光、只留支架——細胞身上的「身分標記」會被病人的免疫系統辨認為外來物，洗掉細胞就避開排斥，骨架還能冷藏半年以上備用。接著進入組裝：先把三根這種小管並排圍成一圈，相鄰處用可被身體分解的縫線縫起來；再把每根管子下半段封口、各自折成一片葉片。三根管子就湊出一顆有三片葉片的瓣膜 (第一代 Gen 1，結構請看 Fig. 1A、B、C)。

其實這個三管狀並非作者的第一次嘗試，前一版「管中管」(tube-in-tube，請看 Fig. 1D) 失敗了，才逼著重新設計。前一版把一根小管塞進一根大管，用三條縱向縫線抓出三片葉片，三個葉片邊角接點 (commissure) 完全靠縫線承擔關閉時往下拉的力。長期下來縫線會把內管慢慢拉鬆、葉片變短，血就從中間倒流。新版「三管狀」把這份下拉力分散到三根管子的「整片管壁」上承擔，不再壓在幾條縫線上。電腦力學模擬證實，新設計的邊角不再出現縫線位置的局部高應力 (Fig. 1H、I)，邊角因此穩定許多。

作者把直徑 19 mm 的瓣膜縫進羔羊心臟的肺動脈位置，每隔幾週用超音波量瓣膜內徑、血液被擋住的壓力差與血液逆流多寡。判斷成功的關鍵指標是直徑：成年羊的肺動脈大約 25 mm，瓣膜若能撐到約 25 mm 就算「跟著長大」。這裡要先記住，瓣膜其實分兩塊——外圍那層管壁 (root，像瓣膜的「外框」) 和裡面三片可開合的葉片，兩者得同步長大才能繼續密合。但第一代四隻羊中，有兩隻很早就出問題：植入後 12 週和 20 週瓣膜內徑暴衝、三個葉片邊角被拉開、血液開始逆流，只好提前取出。另外兩隻撐到 28 週和 52 週才出現相同問題。核心原因正是外圍管壁長得比裡面的三片葉片快太多，葉片就蓋不住變大的中央截面，血液於是逆流。

為了壓制外圍管壁長太快，作者推出第二代：在三管狀瓣膜外面再多包一層膠原蛋白小管做成的袖套 (sleeve)。這層袖套像一條彈性束帶，把外圍管壁的生長速度「綁慢」一點，讓裡面的三片葉片有時間跟上。結果三隻第二代羊中，有兩隻在 52 週時瓣膜直徑穩定停在約 25 mm、血液被擋住的壓力差小於 5 mmHg、只有極輕微的血液逆流。第三隻撐到 44 週才出現中度逆流，且同樣是瓣膜過大撐到 27 mm。整體來看，第二代的逆流嚴重度比第一代明顯下降。

取出後切片觀察，外圍管壁裡面已經長滿宿主細胞，密度接近真血管壁；三片葉片表面也覆上一層宿主細胞，並從邊緣慢慢長出血管內表面的細胞。葉片內部細胞還少 (約原生的三成)，這也呼應了為何葉片長得比外圍慢。鈣化方面，新瓣膜在一年的時間裡只看到零星幾點細小鈣化，幾乎找不到結塊。再用化學定量比較，新瓣膜葉片的鈣含量比同樣模型測到的牛瓣膜葉片低約 2.6 倍，外圍管壁更低了 33 倍。對照之下，臨床用的牛瓣膜在同一隻羊身上 11 週就因鈣化堵塞、心臟衰竭而死。

本研究的樣本太少：第一代只 4 隻、第二代只 3 隻，統計上力道有限。瓣膜被植入在壓力較低的肺動脈那一側，壓力高出許多的主動脈位置能不能撐住，這個實驗回答不了。所有羊全程每天打兩次抗凝血藥 (肝素)，未來臨床上能不能停藥而瓣膜仍安全運作，仍未知。另外，葉片內部細胞密度仍只有正常的三成，長期能否完全填滿、把支架徹底改造為自身組織，目前無法回答。還要注意動物模型本身的偏差：羔羊肺動脈在一年內長大的幅度遠大於兒童 (兒童要十多年才完成同樣成長)，第一代後期那種根部暴衝失效，搬到兒童身上時間軸會拉長許多，未必那麼快出問題。

兒童心臟瓣膜置換的麻煩在於植入進去的瓣膜必須隨身體長大，但現有的動物組織或機械瓣膜都做不到——前者經化學固定後形同死組織、不會長大又易鈣化，後者是固定尺寸的硬零件，孩子只好反覆執行開胸手術換瓣膜。為了解決這個問題，作者讓產生膠原蛋白的細胞 (fibroblast) 在管狀模具裡慢慢長出一層緻密的膠原蛋白支架材料 (細胞外基質 ECM)，再用清潔劑把細胞洗掉、只留下這層支架。這等於做出一個現成、不會被免疫系統排斥、還能長期保存的「骨架」，等病人自己的細胞日後把它重新長滿。

作者用三根這種膠原蛋白小管平行併在一起圍成一圈、彼此相鄰處縫起來，每根管子下半部各自封口形成一片瓣膜，組成第一代「三管狀」瓣膜 (tri-tube，請看 Fig. 1C)。為什麼要這樣設計？前一版叫「管中管」(tube-in-tube，請看 Fig. 1D)，瓣膜邊角接合點 (commissure) 完全靠縫線抓住，縫線被拉久了，內管就會從縫線處慢慢滑下去，瓣膜變短而漏血。三管狀改讓膠原蛋白本體直接承擔這個下拉力，縫線不再是主要受力者，邊角接合點因此穩固許多。

作者把第一代瓣膜縫進四隻幼羔羊心臟的肺動脈位置追蹤，期望瓣膜能隨羔羊長大從 19 mm 撐到約 25 mm 的成年正常尺寸。結果其中兩隻在 12 週與 20 週時瓣膜外圍管壁突然暴衝長大，但裡面的三片葉片沒跟上。三片葉片於是沒辦法在血管中央碰在一起，心臟放鬆時血液從中間縫隙倒流回心室 (血液回流 regurgitation)，只好提前取出瓣膜。另外兩隻撐到 28 週和 52 週才出現相同問題。

為了解決外圍管壁長太快的問題，作者再剖開一根同樣的膠原蛋白小管包在三管狀外面當「袖套 (sleeve)」，做成第二代瓣膜。袖套讓管壁變厚，物理上拖慢管壁長大的速度，給葉片時間跟上。結果三隻羔羊全部撐到 52 週才取出，瓣膜內徑都穩穩停在約 25 mm 的正常尺寸；其中兩隻只有極輕微的血液回流，血液被瓣膜擋住產生的壓力差 (pressure gradient) 也小於 5 mmHg。第三隻在 52 週時內徑長到 27 mm、出現中度回流。

取出瓣膜時，三片葉片表面都已經被羔羊自己的細胞重新長滿，包括血管內皮細胞與支撐用的間質細胞，葉片仍然又薄又柔軟。常見導致瓣膜失效的鈣化 (瓣膜組織堆積鈣鹽變硬而受損) 只看到稀疏小點：葉片鈣含量是同模型裡傳統牛或豬人工瓣膜的 1/2.6、外圍管壁更只有 1/33。

不過實驗仍有限制。樣本很少，第一代四隻、第二代三隻；瓣膜也只放在壓力較低的肺動脈那一側，壓力高出許多的主動脈位置能不能撐住，這個實驗回答不了。另外羔羊肺動脈八個月就長大 4 mm，比兒童肺動脈十六年才長大 12 mm 快非常多，第一代後期那種「管壁長太快把瓣膜邊角拉開」的失效模式，在生長慢得多的兒童身上影響可能輕微許多。

作者先把從羊皮膚取得、會分泌膠原蛋白的 ovine dermal fibroblast (ODF) 泡進「會凝結成果凍的蛋白液」(fibrinogen + thrombin) 裡，灌進一根像甜甜圈剖面的玻璃模具（內徑 16 mm、管壁厚 4 mm），液體凝成果凍後細胞就被卡在管壁中。fibrin 是血液凝結時形成的網狀蛋白，撞傷後結痂的主成分；在這裡它只是一個臨時鷹架 (sacrificial scaffold)，先把細胞固定在 3D 位置。接著泡在培養液裡 8 週，讓細胞慢慢吃掉果凍、邊吐出自己的膠原蛋白填回去，最終整層果凍被替換成緻密膠原蛋白管壁。果凍貼在玻璃模具內外壁上，沿管子長軸會被細胞拉短、沿圓周卻被模具卡住不能縮；細胞和膠原被這個張力差往圓周方向拉直，最後管壁纖維沿圓周一圈一圈整齊排列 (circumferential alignment)，跟原生肺動脈瓣葉的纖維走向一致。

8 週後管子先用兩種洗碗精等級的清潔劑泡掉細胞 (SDS 6 小時、Triton X-100 30 分鐘)，再用 DNase 把殘餘 DNA 切碎沖走，這套手法叫脫細胞 (decellularization，標準流程見論文 Methods)。一定要洗的原因是細胞表面有「身分標籤」，植入別人體內會被免疫系統攻擊；洗掉細胞只留膠原骨架，就變成可長期庫存的現成同種異體支架 (off-the-shelf allograft)。作者用三色染料 (Trichrome) 染切片驗證：管壁上看不到黑色細胞核，殘餘 fibrin 紅色只剩管腔內側一薄層。再引用同實驗室前作 (Meier et al. 2014) 證明這款脫細胞管子可冷藏 6 個月不失去力學性質，醫院不必臨時培養 8 週。

要證明這管子有資格當瓣膜原料，作者把脫好細胞的管子和原生羊肺動脈瓣葉都切成 2x10 mm 條帶，夾在拉伸機兩端慢慢拉到斷 (tensile strain-to-failure testing)，記錄拉斷瞬間撐住的最高應力 (UTS) 跟材料抵抗拉伸的硬度 (modulus)。結果工程管 UTS 約 2 MPa、modulus 約 5 MPa，跟原生瓣葉幾乎重合；對照組 Hancock 豬瓣葉撐到 14 MPa、硬度 21 MPa 硬得多。縫線承載力部分用標準縫線拉脫力測試 (suture pull-out force testing，依 ISO 7198) 測得工程管 92 公克力，原生肺動脈 244 公克力——雖只有原生的 38%，但遠高於瓣膜開合時加在縫線上的力，足以承擔植入後到細胞長進來之前的空窗。

光說力學數字對得上還不夠，作者再做三道成分功課：先用一種能間接秤膠原蛋白的化學反應 (hydroxyproline assay，原始流程見 Stegemann & Stalder 1967) 抓特有胺基酸算膠原量；再用染色反應 (ninhydrin assay) 算總蛋白；再用能同時點名上百種蛋白的質譜分析 (LC-SRM proteomics，原始流程見 Hill et al. 2015) 比較四批管子的 ECM 蛋白組成。結果總纖維膠原量批次間有差，但每一種具體類型 (type I、III、V) 的比例沒差，其他 ECM 蛋白家族也一致——批次間是量的多寡差一點，成分配方沒飄移。

這套平台仍有限制：縫線承載力只達原生肺動脈的四成左右；管子比原生瓣葉厚一倍 (靠 modulus 一致來維持柔軟、保留結構安全餘量)；總膠原量在不同批次間會變動。後續植入失敗的兩顆 Gen 1 瓣膜剛好出自同一批，但 proteomics 找不出該批跟其他批的成分差異，所以差異是量而非種類；作者也承認 proteomics library 沒涵蓋所有 ECM 成分與細胞釋出的生長因子，不能完全排除批次效應。

次佳版本10 票

作者先把產生膠原蛋白的細胞 (ovine dermal fibroblast, ODF) 跟血液裡會凝結的蛋白 (fibrinogen) 一起灌進一根直徑 16 mm 的玻璃管，再加入催化酵素 (thrombin)，幾分鐘內整管凝成一根包著細胞的果凍狀小管 (fibrin gel)。這根 fibrin gel 不是長期容器、而是一條會被細胞自己拆掉的「臨時鷹架」：它把細胞撐成圓管形狀、讓細胞抓著它出力，細胞才被誘導沿圓周方向織出新的膠原蛋白。前 2 週讓凝膠自由沿管軸縮短，細胞被擠得更緊；接下來 7 週把管子套在玻璃軸上不讓它再收縮，細胞只能往管壁方向繼續織膠原，最後 fibrin 幾乎全被取代成緻密、循圓周方向排列的膠原層 (ECM)。為什麼非要「沿圓周排好隊」？因為原生肺動脈瓣的小薄片，圓周方向特別硬、特別耐拉——這正是瓣膜關閉時血壓往下壓、把瓣膜往外撐開的方向；把膠原最強的那條軸對準血壓最大的方向，瓣膜才撐得住。

8 週後管壁長滿了細胞與膠原，但活細胞不能就這樣植入別隻羊：細胞表面有一套「身分標記」會被別人的免疫系統認出是外來貨，輕則發炎重則整塊被排斥。作者用兩種清潔劑接力把細胞洗光：強力版 (SDS) 像強效洗碗精把細胞膜拆爛、把細胞裡的蛋白變性溶掉；溫和版 (Triton X-100) 接力沖殘骸和油脂；膠原蛋白本身耐這兩種清潔劑、留在原位不被沖掉，這套組合俗稱 detergent decellularization。最後再用一種把 DNA 切碎的酵素 (DNase) 把殘留 DNA 也清掉。為什麼非洗到一根 DNA 都不剩？因為殘餘 DNA 一樣會被免疫系統認得出來。作者用會把 collagen 染藍綠、其他蛋白染紅、細胞核染黑的三色染料 (Trichrome) 確認結果：藍綠膠原帶整齊沿圓周排好，紅色 fibrin 殘渣只剩管內壁一小條，整張看不到完整黑色細胞核。剩下這層空骨架就是免疫上中性、還能在冷藏室存半年以上的備品。

光看顯微鏡還不夠，作者得證明這根管子的「強度跟剛性」夠當瓣膜用。他們把材料切成大約 2 mm × 10 mm 的長條，夾上拉力機 (uniaxial tensile strain-to-failure testing) 沿圓周和沿軸向各拉一次：紀錄越拉越長到斷掉的「應力-應變曲線」，取斜率算剛性 (modulus)、取斷掉前的最高應力算 ultimate tensile stress (UTS)。圓周方向的 modulus 約 5 MPa，跟原生羔羊肺動脈瓣的小薄片差不多；牛瓣膜 ~10 MPa、豬瓣膜 ~21 MPa 都明顯硬得多，UTS 也是同樣的趨勢。為什麼「跟原生瓣膜同等級」這件事重要？因為瓣膜開合像門板，門板太硬就推不開、血流會被擋住、收縮時的壓力差會飆高。豬瓣膜剛性是 ECM 管的 4 倍，作者後面在 pulse duplicator 量到 ECM 瓣膜的壓力差只有豬瓣膜的三分之一，正好印證這條因果。

力學過關只是一半，作者還想知道這根管子的成分清單裡到底有什麼。膠原蛋白只是 ECM 大家族裡最主要的一群，瓣膜要正常運作其實還靠許多其他成分。他們用一套會「點名」約 250 種細胞外基質蛋白的質譜學流程 (LC-SRM proteomics，protocol 見 ref 34-36)，把每根管子打成胜肽、用標準分子當磅秤秤每種蛋白多少；再把蛋白分成七大功能類別，比較四批不同時期培養的管子。結果七大類裡只有「主結構膠原」總量在四批之間有差，但拆到 I 型、III 型、V 型這些細項，每批比例一樣；其他六大類比例在四批之間都穩定。這代表 ECM 管不是單一蛋白組成，而是 native 瓣膜該有的成分大致都齊了。作者其實是用 proteomics 反查 Gen 1 為什麼有兩隻 12 週、20 週就失效——把那批管子的蛋白組成跟其他批比，看不出特別之處，因此 ECM 組成不太能解釋失效，原因得到別處去找。

ECM 管能不能被「縫起來」做成瓣膜？作者切下 1×1 cm 的小片，在離邊 2 mm 處穿一條細縫線 (7-0 prolene loop)，再把線拉到斷掉 (suture pullout force test，依 ISO 7198 vascular graft 規範)，量「線從材料裡被扯出來前能撐多大力」。ECM 管能撐到 92 ± 13 gram-force 才被縫線扯破，原生羊肺動脈則撐到 244 ± 31。雖然只有原生的四成上下，但已經足以承受縫線把三根管子拼成瓣膜、再縫進肺動脈時不會撕裂——這條為後面所有瓣膜設計都用「縫」的組裝方式提供物理基礎。

主題二：為了解決前作 tube-in-tube 因 commissure suture 應力集中而 leaflet 變短的失效模式，作者改用 3 根 ECM tube 圍成閉環、底部各自封口形成 leaflet，讓 diastolic load 改由 bulk matrix 承擔，ANSYS 力學模擬與 pulse duplicator 測試確認 commissure 應力集中消失、EOA 0.97 cm² 且 ΔP 僅 2.5 mmHg。

最佳版本10 票

想理解新設計為什麼贏，得先看前一版怎麼壞的。前一版「管中管」(tube-in-tube) 把一根膠原管塞進另一根、用三條縱向縫線把內外管縫起來；這三條縫線本身就定義三片瓣葉的尖角接點 (commissure)。瓣膜關閉時血液把內管往心室方向往下拉，這個下拉力全壓在縫線上。想像用釘子把一塊布釘在牆上、從下方拉布：拉力會集中在釘子那幾個點上，布旁邊只承受很小的力。縫線就是那些釘子。縫線拉久了內管會從縫線處慢慢滑下去、葉片變短，三片葉子蓋不住中央，血就從中央縫隙倒流——前作報告 Reimer et al. 2017 觀察到 12-22 週就出現這種模式。

新版「三管狀」(tri-tube) 把三根脫好細胞的 16 mm 膠原管平行併排圍成一圈，相鄰處各用一條縱向縫線縫起來 (請看 Fig. 1A、B、C)。再把每根管子下半段橫向縫起來封口，封口處的弧形就自然成為一片瓣葉，三根管子各貢獻一片。關鍵差別是 commissure 不再「由縫線定義」、而是「由三根管子的整片管壁互相靠著」構成。新版瓣膜關閉時的下拉力直接被三根管子整片連續的膠原管壁吸收掉，就像把同樣的拉力分散到三大塊布的整片表面，而不是集中在幾個釘子上。縫線在新版裡只是讓三根管子保持相對位置的輔助，等細胞長進來把三根管子之間黏死後，縫線是否完全分解就不重要了。

作者用一種能在電腦上把連續體切成幾萬個小四面體、再一個個算應力的工程軟體 (ANSYS finite element model)，把瓣膜先擬合成一個能撐拉伸的彈性材料模型，再在電腦裡施加舒張壓往瓣葉下方推，看每個位置承受多少應力，畫成彩色應力分布圖。比較兩版時：前一版在縫線位置出現亮紅色的應力高峰，新版同樣位置只剩連續一片低應力 (請看 Fig. 1H vs 1I)。並非「commissure 應力為零」——新版 commissure 那一帶仍是整個瓣膜應力較高的區域，但這個應力是連續分布在膠原本體上的，而不是集中在幾個縫線點上。為了驗證模擬不只是電腦數字，作者另外做加速疲勞測試 (durability test)，把瓣膜放在 10 Hz、10 mmHg 舒張壓的脈動機 (Bose Durapulse) 裡跑了 1350 萬次心跳循環：commissure 完全沒有可見損傷，反倒是瓣葉底部封口縫線處出現拉脫。

為了量新版瓣膜實際開合表現，作者用一種能在實驗室裡模擬心跳循環的脈動測試平台 (pulse duplicator，原始系統見 Reimer et al. 2015)：把瓣膜縫進矽膠套、放進透明腔室，幫浦以兒童心跳大小 (4 公升/分鐘) 推水通過瓣膜，量瓣膜前後壓力差 (ΔP)、瓣膜真正能通血的開口面積 (effective orifice area, EOA) 與舒張時倒流的比例 (regurgitation fraction)。新版的 EOA 0.97 cm² 比商用牛瓣 0.74、豬瓣 0.71 都大；ΔP 只有 2.5 mmHg、比商用瓣低約一半 (牛 4.6、豬 4.5)；倒流比例 8.1% 跟牛瓣 7.8% 差不多。三項裡兩項贏對照組，第三項持平。

最有力的證據在動物身上。作者用一種統計「能存活多久」的標準畫法 (Kaplan-Meier survival curve)，把每隻羊「植入後到瓣膜出現中度逆流」的時間畫成一條階梯下降曲線；新設計 7 隻、前作 8 隻。比較兩條曲線的對數秩檢定 (Log-rank Mantel-Cox test) 顯示新設計顯著延後失效 (P=0.0024)、失效風險只有舊設計的 12.5% (請看 Fig. S5)。新設計確實把前作的 commissure 失效模式打掉。

兩個限制要記住。第一，ANSYS 為了能算出數字必須簡化：把材料假設成各方向硬度一致 (isotropic)，但實際膠原管沿圓周硬、沿軸向軟，這個方向差被抹平了；模型也只取瓣膜的 1/3 (axisymmetric sub-domain) 來算。第二，pulse duplicator 在室溫、用生理食鹽水、只試了一個流量、只測 n=2 顆 Gen 1，第二代 Gen 2 完全沒在這個平台上測過。所以「新版血流動力優於商用瓣」是限定 Gen 1、限定條件下的結論。

次佳版本9 票

tri-tube 的設計是被前一版逼出來的，所以要先講前一版怎麼壞。前一版叫 tube-in-tube：把一根小管塞進一根大管，用三條等距的縱向縫線把兩根管綁住 (請看 Fig. 1D)，這三條縫線同時就是三片瓣葉相接的接合點 (commissure)。問題在於瓣膜閉合時血液從上方往下推內管，這整股下拉力 (diastolic load) 全部壓在這三條縫線上。縫線跟膠原蛋白接觸只是幾個小點，整股拉力被擠進一個極小的截面積，這個小區域承受的單位面積壓力遠高於周圍的膠原蛋白本體（即「應力集中」）。長期反覆受高應力會把組織擠出、把縫線一點一點從膠原裡拉鬆——前一版瓣膜在 12-22 週後拔出來時葉片明顯變短，原本縫線標記的接合點高度比最終瓣葉頂端還高出好幾毫米，直接證實縫線位置確實隨時間下滑。葉片高度因此縮短、血從中央縫隙倒流。

為了把下拉力從縫線挪開，作者改成三管狀：把三根直徑 16 mm 的小管並排站起來，兩兩相接的那條軸線用會被身體分解的縫線從上縫到下，把三根管子綁成一個閉環。接著把每根管子的下半段沿底部封口，這個封口讓每根管的下半截往內折塌成一片可閉合的瓣葉，三根管就湊出三片瓣葉。新版的接合點落在「相鄰兩管接觸的那條軸線」上，瓣膜閉合時瓣葉把三根管壁從中央往外撐開，這股下拉力是透過整片膠原蛋白本體 (bulk matrix) 傳到下游，不再像前一版那樣壓在縫線上。縫線只負責把三管綁成一圈、保持結構，不再是主要受力者。為什麼挑會被分解的縫線？因為作者要的是「縫線撐到宿主細胞爬進來、用新分泌的膠原蛋白把三管自然黏在一起」這個過渡，等接縫變成組織自己的縫合、縫線就完成階段性任務被身體分解掉；如果用永久不分解的縫線，反而會永遠留下一個應力集中點。

光靠設計推理還不夠，作者用電腦模擬證明應力真的轉移了。這套「把材料切成上萬個小三角形再算每個位置受力」的方法叫有限元素分析 (finite element analysis, FEA)，本論文用 ANSYS Workbench 跑：先把前一節原料管的應力應變曲線擬合成一條材料硬度公式 (Mooney-Rivlin 超彈性模型)，再對瓣膜施加一條逐漸升高的逆向背壓模擬瓣膜閉合那一刻，並用一個避免瓣葉互相穿透的接觸算法去算每一點的應力。輸出就是 Fig. 1H、I 的應力色階圖：Fig. 1I 的 tube-in-tube 在縫線位置看得到明顯的局部紅斑，應力集中在那幾個縫線小點上；Fig. 1H 的 tri-tube 雖然接合點仍有應力，但這片應力是「連續沿整片膠原蛋白本體分布」、不再壓在某個點縫線上。要老實說，這套模擬有多處簡化（把材料當作方向都一樣、縫線當作幾個點、只算閉合靜態），所以它只能拿來比較兩款設計誰的接合點更穩，不能拿來預測絕對應力數值。

電腦模擬說設計合理，下一步是真的把瓣膜放進「在桌面上模擬心跳水流」的測試台。這套設備叫 pulse duplicator (protocol 見 Cattermole et al. 2017)：含波形產生器、儲液槽、可控壓力腔、避免水回流的單向機械瓣、以及量水流方向的電磁流量計。作者用約 4.0 LPM 的兒童心輸出量、室溫的生理鹽水，把 Gen 1 瓣膜放進去跑至少 10 次心動週期，記錄三個指標：血流真正能用的開口面積 (EOA)、血流被瓣膜擋住前後的壓力差 (systolic ΔP)、瓣膜關閉時血液往回漏的比例 (regurgitation fraction)。比較對象是臨床用的 Contegra 牛瓣膜跟 Hancock 豬瓣膜。結果：Gen 1 的開口面積 0.97 cm² 比牛瓣膜 0.74、豬瓣膜 0.71 都大；壓力差 2.5 mmHg 比牛 4.6、豬 4.5 都小；回流比例 8.1% 介於兩者之間。為什麼「開口大、壓力差小」很重要？因為瓣膜本來就是給血流通過用的，開口越大、血流越順，心臟為了把血推過去要做的功就越少；兒童心臟還在發育，壓力負擔少一點意義很大。

這套證據鏈仍要承認限制：ANSYS 模擬經過多處簡化，不能拿來預測絕對應力數值；pulse duplicator 只測了 2 顆 Gen 1 瓣膜、單一流量、室溫生理鹽水（不是體溫血液），統計力有限；Gen 2 完全沒做 pulse duplicator 測試；耐久測試在 920 萬到 1350 萬次循環之間出現底部封口縫線被扯出，作者解釋是因為非無菌測試環境有黴菌污染，但這個失效模式仍要在更乾淨的測試系統再驗。

主題三：作者把 7 顆 19 mm tri-tube valve 縫進 4 隻 Gen 1 加 3 隻 Gen 2 羔羊的 pulmonary artery，每 2 週用超音波追 valve diameter、EOA、systolic ΔP、regurgitation 與 insufficiency index 達 52 週,Kaplan-Meier 顯示 tri-tube 整體 freedom from moderate regurgitation 顯著優於 tube-in-tube（P=0.0024）、Gen 2 insufficiency index 又比 Gen 1 低（P=0.015），且全面勝過同模型的 Hancock porcine valve。

最佳版本9 票

這個模塊先講作者怎麼把瓣膜放到羊身上、追多久。羊心臟尺寸跟人差不多，所以瓣膜臨床前常用羊測試；作者特別挑 4 個月、約 28 公斤的幼羔羊，因為這個年紀肺動脈會在一年內從約 19 mm 長到約 25 mm，等於把「瓣膜能不能跟著長大」這個問題壓縮成一年內看完。位置選肺動脈是因為它是兒童先天性心臟病最常需要重建的低壓側 (~15-25 mmHg)，主動脈那一側壓力高達 80-120 mmHg 是另一個量級，這次不挑戰。手術從左側肋骨間切開進胸，先剪掉原本的肺動脈瓣葉與一小段肺動脈，再用會被身體分解的縫線 (Maxon 5-0) 把三管狀瓣膜接回原位：先縫遠端 (distal anastomosis)、再縫近端 (proximal anastomosis)，途中放血進去排空氣才關胸。術後每隻羊每天打兩次肝素 (1500 IU) 避免血栓堵住瓣膜。

追蹤怎麼做？作者用透過胸壁打超音波 (transthoracic echocardiography) 在植入 1 週、1 個月、之後每兩個月量五個指標：瓣膜直徑、瓣膜開到最大時血流能通過的有效截面積 (EOA)、血流穿過瓣膜時兩側的壓力差 (systolic ΔP)、心臟輸出量、漏血程度，以及綜合的「漏血嚴重指數」(insufficiency index)，它把超音波看到的回流噴流寬度、深度、與血管寬度比值壓成一個數字，越高就越漏，可在同一時間點直接比較兩組瓣膜。一旦漏血升到中度就取出瓣膜結束追蹤。

Gen 1 共 4 隻：兩隻在 12 週和 20 週瓣膜直徑暴衝、邊角被拉開、漏血加劇，被迫提前取出；另兩隻撐到 28 週和 52 週後也因瓣膜直徑長過 24-25 mm 預期值、漏血加重而被取出。失效的幾何機制是：瓣葉的「閉合疊蓋長度」(coaptation length) 是固定的，根部 (root) 一直長大、三片瓣葉就追不上、沒辦法在中央碰在一起，於是中央出現縫隙、血從那裡漏回去。Gen 2 共 3 隻：兩隻撐到 52 週，瓣膜直徑穩定在約 25 mm、跨瓣膜壓力差小於 5 mmHg、只有輕微漏血；第三隻撐到 44 週都正常，但 52 週時瓣膜直徑暴衝到 27 mm、漏血升到中度，跟 Gen 1 那兩隻晚期失效的羊類似。

要回答「tri-tube 真的比 tube-in-tube 好嗎？」需要存活曲線檢定。Kaplan-Meier 是一條「到現在還沒壞的瓣膜比例」隨時間往下掉的曲線；想看兩種設計差不差，就用 log-rank Mantel-Cox 這個專門比兩條存活曲線的檢定。把新版 tri-tube 全 7 隻和舊版 tube-in-tube 比，P = 0.0024 (機率小到不可能是巧合)，新版顯著比舊版撐得久。Gen 1 跟 Gen 2 兩組組內比則因為樣本太少 (4 vs 3 隻) 沒有顯著差 (P > 0.05)，但用插管當下的漏血指數比 Gen 1 平均 3.5、Gen 2 平均 2.2，這個差是顯著的 (P = 0.015)——因為漏血指數是直接在取出當下量到的單一數字，可以直接做 t 檢定比大小，不受存活時間分布影響，敏感度比 Kaplan-Meier 高。

跟同模型的商用豬瓣對照差距有多大？商用豬瓣 (Hancock) 6 隻 20 週時 EOA 只有 1.14 cm²、跨瓣膜壓力差高達 18 mmHg。同期 Gen 1 EOA 已 2.84、ΔP 5；Gen 2 EOA 2.05、ΔP 5；到 52 週 Gen 2 EOA 4.2、ΔP 2.7 (Table 1)。新版瓣膜的開口比豬瓣大 2-4 倍、心臟出力少推 3-7 倍，差距非常顯著。

這套 in vivo 證據鏈的限制有四：第一，Gen 1 4 隻、Gen 2 3 隻，樣本量太小，統計可信度有限。第二，只測壓力較低的肺動脈，壓力高出 4-5 倍的主動脈位置撐不撐得住沒測。第三，全程每天打兩次抗凝血藥 (肝素)，能不能停藥仍未知。第四，羔羊肺動脈八個月就長大 4 mm，兒童肺動脈十六年才長大 12 mm，所以 Gen 1 後期那種「根部長太快撐爆瓣膜」的失效模式，在兒童身上時間軸會拉長，未必那麼快發生。

次佳版本7 票

新瓣膜要證明能跟著身體長大，就得放進活體追蹤。作者選羔羊 pulmonary artery 是因為羊心臟跟兒童差不多大，是 FDA 認可的瓣膜臨床前測試標準模型；化學固定的動物瓣膜在人體要好幾年才鈣化，但在羊體內幾個月就會失效，這讓研究能在合理時間內看出失效模式；pulmonary 是右心側壓力較低，先在這裡看能不能撐住生長比較保守。手術上，羔羊先麻醉，從左側肋骨間切開進入胸腔 (left lateral thoracotomy)，切掉原生肺動脈瓣的葉片、再剪掉一段肺動脈，把 19 mm 新瓣膜用可被身體分解的縫線 (Maxon 5-0) 縫進去取代。可分解縫線是關鍵——縫線會在幾個月內被身體吸收，期間宿主組織已經沿著縫線位置長進去，縫線消失後接口就變成宿主自己的活組織，可以順著血管一起變寬；永久縫線則會把接口卡死，瓣膜長大時接口被縫線「圈住」拉不開。

作者每 2 週讓羊隔著胸壁做一次超音波 (transthoracic echocardiography)，從畫面與都卜勒血流量五個指標：瓣膜直徑 (valve diameter) 追蹤瓣膜會不會跟著羊長大；有效開口面積 (EOA) 與收縮期壓力差 (systolic ΔP) 看瓣膜開啟順不順；逆流嚴重度 (regurgitation grade) 看每次閉合漏多少；閉合不嚴指數 (insufficiency index) 是「逆流截面積除以 EOA」的比值，反映閉合相對嚴重度。瓣膜植入時羔羊大約 4 個月大、肺動脈內徑 19 mm；長到 12 個月時肺動脈會變到 23~25 mm (Gottlieb 2013)。所以「瓣膜跟著長大」的具體目標就是 52 週時直徑撐到約 25 mm，而且還要繼續密合。

Gen 1 四隻羊裡有兩隻 (PACV 1、PACV 2) 在 12、20 週瓣膜直徑暴衝、commissure 被拉開、開始中度逆流，只好提前犧牲取出。另外兩隻 (PACV 3、PACV 4) 撐到 28 週、52 週才出現同樣問題，分別在 36 與 62 週犧牲——共同點是失效都跟瓣膜直徑暴衝超過 25 mm 同步。Gen 2 三隻羊裡有兩隻 (PACV 5、PACV 6) 順利撐到 52 週：直徑穩穩停在 25 mm、壓力差小於 5 mmHg、只有極輕微逆流。第三隻 (PACV 7) 44 週前都很好，但 52 週時直徑暴衝到 27 mm、出現中度逆流，跟 Gen 1 那種「直徑長太快」的失效模式同樣是 commissure 被撐開。

作者用兩種方式量化新版的整體優勢。第一種是 Kaplan-Meier 曲線，把「從植入到第一次出現中度逆流」這段時間畫成階梯圖，橫軸是週數、縱軸是「還沒漏到中度」的比例。比較兩條曲線用對數秩檢定 (log-rank Mantel-Cox test)：tri-tube 整體 (Gen 1 + Gen 2) vs 前一版 tube-in-tube 算出 P=0.0024 (兩條一致的機率只有 0.24%)，證明 tri-tube 整體真的撐得久。但同樣方法用在 Gen 1 vs Gen 2 卻沒有顯著差異——cohort 只有 4 vs 3 隻、Gen 1 變異又大。第二種是閉合不嚴指數 (insufficiency index)：每隻羊在犧牲那一刻量到的連續數字，作者用 t-test 比較平均：Gen 1 = 3.5 ± 0.4、Gen 2 = 2.2 ± 0.8，P=0.015。對小樣本而言，「比較連續數字」比「比較事件時間」更容易抓到差異。

作者把同樣 lamb model 之前測過的 Hancock 豬瓣膜數據 (n=6，Carney 2017 已發表) 直接拿來對比，不再殺新的羊。Table 1 顯示 20 週時豬瓣膜的有效開口面積 1.14 cm²、收縮期壓力差 18 mmHg；Gen 2 是 2.05 cm² 與 5 mmHg——Gen 2 開口將近兩倍大、壓力差只有約四分之一，全面壓制豬瓣膜。商用牛瓣膜 (Contegra) 的對照則只植入 1 隻當參照組，但這隻羊 11 週時就因為瓣膜鈣化堵塞、右心衰竭而死亡，實驗提前結束——換句話說，新瓣膜撐到 52 週的對照組，傳統牛瓣膜根本撐不到。

這套追蹤證據鏈的限制要說清楚。樣本太少：Gen 1 共 4 隻、Gen 2 共 3 隻，作者自己承認無法在兩代之間下統計結論。只測壓力較低的肺動脈側，主動脈那種高壓位置撐不撐得住、這套實驗回答不了。7 隻羊全程都打抗凝血劑 (heparin)，未來臨床上能不能停藥、瓣膜表面是否真的長出夠完整的內皮層擋住血栓，都還無法判斷。物種差也要記住——羔羊 8 個月肺動脈就長 4 mm，兒童要 16 年才長 12 mm，前者快多了。Gen 1 看到的「外圍管壁長太快撐開 commissure」這種失效模式，搬到兒童身上時間軸會拉長許多，未必那麼快出問題，但也代表 Gen 2 的 sleeve 設計優勢可能會被縮小。

主題四：鑒於 Gen 1 中 root 在 12-20 週暴衝、撐開 commissure 造成 regurgitation 的新失效模式，作者在 tri-tube 外再縱向剖開一根同源 ECM tube 縫成 sleeve，使 Gen 2 在 2/3 隻羊撐到 52 週時 valve diameter 穩定在 ~25 mm、ΔP<5 mmHg、僅 trivial-to-mild regurgitation，且 leaflet free-edge 長度增加 17-37% 補償了 coaptation 縮短。

最佳版本10 票

前一代 (Gen 1) 解決了前作縫線單點承重的問題，但又冒出新的失效模式：根部 (root，包住三片葉片的外圍管壁) 在植入後 12-20 週裡長得比葉片快很多。根部變寬時整顆瓣膜的外圍跟著被撐大，三片葉片相接處 (commissure) 也跟著被拉開、葉片在血流中央碰不到一起、血就從中間縫隙倒流回心室。換句話說：前作敗在縫線、Gen 1 敗在根部組織自己長太快。

Gen 2 設計怎麼做：作者把另一根同製程的膠原蛋白小管縱向劃開成一張長方形片，再包在已縫好的三管狀瓣膜外面、沿一條軸向縫合線縫起來合攏。縱向剖開是為了讓這層片狀的鞘 (sleeve) 能夠攤平貼合外圍，不會跟內層三管打架 (Fig. 1G)。整顆變成「裡面三管狀 + 外面一層袖套」的雙層瓣膜。植入前還用 22 號針 (22-gauge) 在 sleeve 上戳 6 個小針孔，讓兩層之間可能卡的組織滲液 (exudate) 能流出，避免局部腫脹或感染。為什麼這樣做能拖慢根部？作者推測兩個機制：第一是「物理綁帶」效應——外層多了一層膠原蛋白，根部要往外擴張就得連帶把 sleeve 一起撐大、阻力變大；第二是「延後重塑」——宿主細胞要先把外層長滿才能進到內層，內層 tri-tube 本體完全細胞化的時間被往後延，等於減慢細胞驅動的根部重塑速度。在細胞還沒長滿前，純膠原蛋白骨架要變大只能靠三條慢路：被血壓反覆拉到彈性極限 (stretching)、超過彈性極限的永久變形 (plastic deformation)、或殘留的酵素 (protease) 把膠原蛋白慢慢拆掉再重接 (protease-mediated remodeling)，三條都比細胞主動生長慢。

三隻 Gen 2 (PACV 5、6、7) 全部撐到 52 週才取出。PACV 5 與 6 整個追蹤期間只有極輕微到輕度倒流、瓣膜內徑穩定在約 25 mm 的成年正常值、收縮期壓力差降到 5 mmHg 以下。PACV 7 撐到 44 週都還很好，但到 52 週時內徑突然漲到 27 mm、超過預期值，倒流也跟著升到中度——和 Gen 1 後期出問題的那兩顆失效模式類似，只是被 sleeve 拖到比較晚才發生。取出瓣膜的照片 (Fig. 4G-L) 顯示三顆都還有三片完整葉片：PACV 5、6 只在三片葉片相接處有「小幅」分離 (對應極輕微到輕度倒流)；PACV 7 整顆變成橢圓形，剛好對應它過度擴張的數據。組織學上：包在外面的 sleeve 被宿主細胞長滿，但內層 tri-tube 本體的根部外流端 (outflow root) 仍未完全細胞化——這跟「sleeve 延後內層重塑」的推測一致，宿主細胞優先填外層、內層被拖慢。

Gen 2 還展現一個有趣的代償現象。葉片關閉時三片彼此重疊的高度叫 coaptation length——重疊越多瓣膜越關得緊、就算瓣膜外圍稍微撐大也能繼續密合，植入時設計成 3-5 mm 的重疊給後續直徑變大留緩衝。52 週時這個重疊從 5 mm 縮到 1.7 mm (Fig. 8)，因為瓣膜外圍變大、葉片寬度沒同步等比變大。直觀上這應該漏更多，但實際上 Gen 2 還是只有輕微倒流——意味著有另一個東西在補償。作者用從上往下看的超音波視角 (short-axis view) 量葉片靠血流中央那條邊的弧長 (free-edge length)，分別在第 1 週和第 52 週各測一次。Gen 2 三隻的葉片這條邊都變長了，三隻各自增加 17-19%、22-37%、25-34% (Data file S2)。葉片這條邊變長等於葉片整體多了一截料，就算重疊區變窄、瓣膜中央仍有足夠葉片伸出來把開口封住。Gen 2 用「葉片自己長長」補了「重疊區縮短」的洞。

PACV 7 為什麼還是失敗？它是 Gen 2 三隻裡 20 週時就最早出現極輕微到輕度倒流的那隻 (Fig. 3G)。作者推測它走了跟 Gen 1 後期那兩顆類似的惡性循環：倒流 → 心臟為了補償加大輸出 → 增加的血流又把根部和遠端肺動脈進一步撐大 → 倒流更嚴重。sleeve 在這條路徑上沒能完全壓住、只能延後而非避免失效。另外三隻 Gen 2 取出時都在同一個三片葉片相接處出現小縫，原因是作者一開始把 sleeve 封口的縫線剛好對齊一條 tri-tube 的縱向縫線、這個位置縫線總量最多、刺激出最強的局部生長反應、把這個接合處撐開。對策很簡單：下一版把 sleeve 縫線挪到兩個接合處中間就好、不要再跟內層縫線重疊。

這套 sleeve 策略仍有四個不確定性：第一，Gen 2 只有 3 隻、根本沒辦法做統計；第二，作者也明說 sleeve 究竟是靠「物理綁帶」還是「延後重塑」目前無法確定 (mechanism cannot be concluded)；第三，一年內 sleeve 會不會被宿主完全吸收進而失去拖慢效果還沒測；第四，即使 sleeve 有效，PACV 7 仍然走入惡性循環，顯示 sleeve 不是萬靈丹、無法救回早就走偏的瓣膜。

次佳版本6 票

為什麼非要做 Gen 2？因為 Gen 1 出現了一個前作沒有的新失效模式：外圈管壁 (root) 在 12-20 週暴衝。Gen 1 兩隻羊在 4-12 週之間瓣膜外圈直徑突然從 19 mm 衝到 24-25 mm 以上 (有些到 28-30 mm)，但中央三片葉片本身大小幾乎沒變；外圈被撐大後，三片葉片在中央碰不到一起、commissure 被拉開，心臟放鬆時血液就從中間縫隙漏回心室——葉片沒長壞，是外圈長太快「拉開了」葉片。為什麼 root 會長太快？兩個原因疊加。第一，外圈管壁是純 collagen，植入初期還沒長進細胞，靠 collagen 自身的彈性承受血壓；如果初期那幾天血液被瓣膜擋住產生的壓力差偏高 (這兩隻就是 11-12 mmHg)，血壓把管壁繃得更開，反覆的伸展又會刺激後來進來的細胞分泌更多 collagen、進一步把直徑撐大。第二，作者用 Ki67 (一種會標記正在分裂中細胞的染色) 看切片，12-20 週時外圈細胞分裂率是 36-62 週的 6 倍，正處於快速擴張期。那葉片為什麼跟不上？葉片這段組織沒有兩端的吻合口可以接到宿主血管，宿主細胞要先佔據 root、再從 root 沿著葉片表面慢慢爬上去；緻密的 collagen 又擋住細胞從葉片內部直接鑽入，所以葉片細胞填回比 root 慢了一兩個級數，葉片大小也就跟著長不上去。

Gen 2 的解法是在三管狀瓣膜外面多包一層袖套 (sleeve)：作者多拿一根同一批的脫細胞膠原蛋白管，沿軸向剖開、攤平成一片長方形膠原蛋白片，再把它包覆在三管狀瓣膜外面、用可降解縫線縫合一條軸向接縫；同時用 22 號針在 sleeve 上戳 6 個洞，避免 sleeve 跟瓣膜本體之間積液 (exudate)。為什麼用同源材料、不用其他？因為同源 collagen 的機械特性跟下面的三管狀完全相容，不會在交界處出現硬度突變、不會多冒一個應力集中點；且 sleeve 本身也會被宿主細胞長滿、漸漸跟下面瓣膜融成一體；如果改用人工纖維布之類的異物，永遠不會融合、反而引起慢性發炎。sleeve 怎麼「拖慢 root 長太快」？兩個機制疊加。第一，整段管壁厚度加倍 (從 0.66 mm 加到約 1.3 mm)，物理上提供更多機械約束，初期血壓沒辦法把管壁繃那麼開。第二，多一層空白的純 collagen 要等細胞填滿，整體 recellularization 速度被分散，root 達到「細胞滿員」的時間被推遲——等於把那波快速擴張的觸發點延後到葉片有機會跟上的時間點。

Gen 2 的結果：撐到 52 週的兩隻瓣膜直徑都穩在約 25 mm (跟正常成年羊肺動脈尺寸吻合)、52 週時血液被瓣膜擋住的壓力差小於 5 mmHg、回流只有 trivial-to-mild。第三隻在 44 週前都很好，但 52 週直徑長到 27 mm、出現 moderate 回流。整體比 Gen 1 明顯改善。但 sleeve 不是萬靈丹：第三隻早在 20 週就出現輕度回流、比另外兩隻提前；作者推測這顆瓣膜可能有額外的局部缺陷讓外圈仍快速擴張、sleeve 沒擋住。sleeve 不是把問題消除、只是把它推遲；個別案例仍可能漏網。

瓣膜直徑大了 6 mm、葉片怎麼還繼續閉合得起來？作者用超音波在 1 週和 52 週、從瓣膜正面看的視角下量葉片自由邊長度 (free-edge length)。三隻 Gen 2 分別增加 17-19%、22-37%、25-34%。為什麼這個增加重要？因為三片葉片中央重疊的長度 (coaptation length) 從 5 mm 降到 1.7 mm、縮短了 66%；葉片自由邊變長剛好補償了葉片邊緣可用的閉合面積，三片葉片才繼續能在中央碰齊。葉片自由邊為什麼會變長？兩個可能：第一，宿主細胞填回後分泌新 collagen 把葉片往外擴 (somatic growth)；第二，葉片在每次心跳承受拉力、慢慢被永久變形，搭配蛋白酶 (protease) 把舊組織重新塑形。作者沒有直接量化哪條佔多大比例，只說兩者皆有貢獻。

Histology 直接印證了「sleeve 拖慢 root」的機制：作者用 Trichrome 染色看 Gen 2 在 52 週時的瓣膜切片 (Fig. 5 I-K)，root 的出口端 (outflow region) 仍有大段「沒被細胞填回」的空白 collagen；對比 Gen 1 同期則 root 完全填滿細胞。入口端 (inflow region) 兩代都完整填滿。為什麼 outflow 沒填滿、inflow 反而填滿？因為 inflow 是接近右心室那一端，血流方向先到 inflow 再到 outflow；inflow 附近的吻合口提供宿主細胞遷移入口的機會更直接，outflow 端被 sleeve 多包一層、進入路徑被拉長，所以細胞填回較慢——這正是 sleeve 拖慢的物理畫面：擋住血流方向上游的細胞抵達 outflow 的速度。另外 Gen 2 三隻都在一個 commissure 處出現小縫；作者發現這個 commissure 剛好是 sleeve 的縱向接縫跟瓣膜本體軸向縫線重疊的位置，兩條縫線堆在一起、降解時反應更強，把那個 commissure 撐開。這個工程缺陷容易解決：下次把 sleeve 接縫挪到 commissure 之間就行。

這套 sleeve 設計的證據鏈要先承認四件事。第一，只有 3 隻，第三隻仍出現直徑暴衝。第二，sleeve 拖慢 root 填回也是事實——長期會不會變成「永遠填不滿」的死區還沒答案。如果 sleeve 那一層始終沒被細胞填回，那段組織就維持成純 collagen 狀態；它不像化學定型瓣膜會吸鈣，但也沒辦法跟著羊長大、不會自我修復，長期看來可能成為「靜止的厚層」，影響整體重塑的均勻性，甚至成為晚期破損點。本研究 52 週還看不到這個問題、但無法外推。第三，加 sleeve 後 in vitro 的血流功能測試 (pulse duplicator) 沒重做，hydrodynamic 性質只是假設沒變、沒實際驗證。第四，sleeve 縫線位置造成 commissure gap，下版要再修一次才能定案。

主題五：取出 7 顆 valve 做 trichrome、von Kossa、SMA、vWF 與 DNA 定量，發現 root 細胞密度已達 native 等級而 leaflet 仍只有 native 的 33%、endothelium 從 leaflet 基部與 commissure 區往中段延伸、leaflet 厚度與 tensile modulus 未隨植入時間漂移，證實宿主細胞確實爬進骨架、且未出現 TEHV 常見的 leaflet thickening。

瓣膜取出後作者做了一整套染色，看細胞怎麼填回、是哪種細胞、有沒有鈣化。為什麼非要這麼多種染色？因為每種回答不同問題：Trichrome 看 collagen 與細胞核分布、von Kossa 看鈣化、α-SMA 跟 vWF 看細胞身分、DAPI 補上細胞核底色、DNA 定量算總細胞密度——把這些堆起來才看得到「細胞在哪、是哪種、有沒有鈣化、有多少」的完整畫面。Trichrome 把 collagen 染成藍綠色、其他蛋白染紅色、細胞核染黑色，切片上一眼就能分辨 collagen 骨架 (藍綠) 跟細胞 (黑點) 的相對位置——「藍綠多、黑點少」就是有骨架沒細胞，「藍綠跟黑點交織」就是細胞已經填回。要看細胞身分作者再用兩種抗體：α-SMA 標記會收縮的細胞，主要是撐住葉片內層的支撐細胞 (interstitial cells，血管平滑肌也會被標到)；vWF 標記血管內皮細胞 (endothelium，包在血流接觸面那層)。瓣膜要恢復功能剛好需要這兩種：interstitial cells 把葉片撐起來、endothelium 把表面包起來不讓血直接碰到 collagen 骨架。但要注意原文寫的是「presumably interstitial cells」——α-SMA 不專屬於 interstitial cells，血管平滑肌也會被標到，所以光看 α-SMA 陽性不能 100% 確認身分；作者是依據「細胞長在葉片內」這個位置線索推測，這就是 presumably 這個字的來源。

作者怎麼把「細胞填回多少」量化？用 DNA quantitation：把切片組織切下，用會跟 DNA 結合發螢光的染料 (Hoechst 33258，標準流程見 Kim et al. 1988) 測 DNA 總量；假設每顆細胞含固定量 DNA，反推每立方公分有多少細胞。Gen 1 的 root 算出來是 123 ± 68 million cells/cm³，原生 root 是 155 ± 10 million cells/cm³，差異沒達顯著——填回程度跟原生差不多。Gen 1 葉片則是 29 ± 20 million cells/cm³，原生葉片是 96 ± 8 million cells/cm³，差距 70%——葉片只有原生的 30%；Gen 2 葉片也大致相同 (約 33%)。葉片只有 33% 算成功嗎？葉片細胞數確實沒追上，但葉片厚度、硬度 (tensile modulus)、collagen 含量都沒掉，瓣膜功能也維持。作者認為葉片不必追到 100% 細胞才算功能正常——瓣膜整體靠 root 跟著長大、搭配葉片邊緣多餘的閉合面積就維持密合。

細胞具體怎麼進入葉片？作者用 vWF 染色看 62 週 Gen 1 那顆瓣膜 (Fig. 6C)：vWF 陽性的內皮層細胞從葉片根部與三片葉片相接處 (commissure) 開始覆蓋，不是葉片中段隨機長出；這推論出血管內皮細胞是「從吻合口沿表面往中間爬」這條路徑進入的。為什麼不從葉片切面中央往內鑽？因為葉片本體的基質是脫細胞後留下來的純 collagen、纖維排得又緊又密，細胞要從側面切口擠進去等於硬撐開一片繃緊的布；葉片表面則直接泡在血流裡，宿主血管壁細胞從吻合口可以順著表面一路延伸過來、這條路寬鬆多了。「表面爬入」對瓣膜功能有什麼意義？表面爬入只把葉片表面包一層 endothelium、葉片厚度不變、還是薄而能彎；如果改成中央鑽入，葉片可能被細胞拉短或拉硬 (這正是 L1 葉片出現的失效)。表面長 endothelium 還能逐步把血流接觸面包起來、未來可能讓抗凝血藥可以停掉。

「葉片厚度與硬度未隨植入時間漂移」這件事為什麼是大新聞？作者把 Gen 1 與 Gen 2 各時間點 (12、20、36、52、62 週) 的葉片厚度、能撐多大拉力才斷掉 (UTS)、硬度 (modulus) 跟植入前的管材比 (Fig. 4E, Fig. 7A, B)：沒有顯著時間趨勢、也跟植入前數值沒差；圓周方向跟軸向方向的硬度比 (anisotropy) 也維持在 1.8 ± 0.5、跟植入前 2.0 ± 0.4 與原生瓣葉 2.3 ± 0.6 都很接近。其他組織工程心臟瓣膜 (tissue-engineered heart valve, TEHV) 常出現一個失效路徑：葉片被細胞侵入後越長越厚、最後變硬卡住、無法閉合。本篇葉片厚度不變等於避開這個常見失效模式；硬度不變也代表材料沒在退化——即使細胞填回不完全，葉片也沒有機械性鬆軟。

62 週 Gen 1 那顆瓣膜出現一個有趣的個案：三片葉片有差異。L3 維持原本高度、還跟著心跳開合，α-SMA 陽性細胞稀稀疏疏散布在葉片內層；L1 變短、完全不會動了，α-SMA 染色顯示一群陽性細胞擠在葉片某個區域、形態跟 L3 散布型不同 (Fig. 6E vs 6F)——同樣亮 α-SMA 背後可能不是同一種行為的細胞。L1 變短是不是這群「擠成一坨」的細胞造成的？不能確定。作者只在 52 週和 62 週兩個時間點取切片；52 週時 L1 仍有活動度 (mild-to-moderate)、62 週才完全失去活動度。「細胞侵入造成葉片變短」這個因果方向沒辦法直接釘住——細胞群是 52 週前就在悄悄堆積、還是 52 週後才湧進來，看不出來。所以這只是一個有趣的個案警示，不是已驗證的失效路徑。

這套組織學 + 免疫染色證據鏈最先要承認四件事。第一，cohort 太小：Gen 1 共 4 隻、Gen 2 共 3 隻，L1 葉片變短的異常更只在 1 隻 1 片葉子上看到。第二，免疫染色 (IHC) 只做了 Gen 1，Gen 2 的細胞身分是「推測跟 Gen 1 差不多」沒有直接證據。第三，α-SMA 陽性 = interstitial cells 這個對應只有 presumably、沒有同切片雙標記排除平滑肌；這是基於「位置 + α-SMA 陽性」的合理推論，不是直接證據。第四，染色拍的是 explant 那一刻的定格畫面，細胞走哪條路、以什麼速度進來，是靠不同時間點的定格拼出來的推論，不是真的看著細胞爬。最後染色還有一個根本上限：它只能告訴你「身上帶這個標記的細胞長在哪裡」，不能告訴你它會不會做正事——vWF 陽性內皮能不能擋住血直接碰 collagen、α-SMA 陽性細胞會不會像正常 interstitial cells 一樣維持葉片基質，這些功能性問題染色都答不了。要 follow-up 的活組織培養或機械刺激實驗才能補上。

作者用兩種染色看不同東西。第一種是 Trichrome 三色染料：膠原蛋白染藍綠色、其他蛋白染紅色、細胞核染黑色，一張切片就能看到「支架在哪 (藍綠) + 細胞核在哪 (黑)」。第二種是會跟組織裡鈣鹽反應形成黑點的銀染色 (von Kossa)，專門偵測鈣化。兩種都做在 9 µm 厚的冷凍切片上。光看「有細胞」還不知道是哪一種，作者再用三種抗體 (immunohistochemistry, IHC，標準流程見 Methods) 分別「點名」瓣膜裡的不同細胞：α-SMA 抗體染撐住瓣膜內層的 interstitial cells、vWF 抗體染瓣膜表面的 endothelium、Ki67 抗體染正在分裂中的細胞；DAPI 抗體把所有細胞核染藍當底色。前兩種是瓣膜要恢復功能最重要的細胞組成 (一種把葉片撐起來、一種把表面包起來不讓血直接碰支架)，Ki67 則是用來看「細胞還在不在快速繁殖」。

細胞密度怎麼量？作者把切片裡的 DNA 全部抽出來、用一種會跟 DNA 結合就發藍光的染料量總 DNA 量 (modified Hoechst assay)，再把總 DNA 除以一顆細胞的標準 DNA 量 (7.6 pg) 反推每立方公分有多少細胞。結果：根部細胞密度 1.23 億顆/cm³，跟原生肺動脈瓣膜根部 1.55 億顆/cm³ 沒差異；葉片只有 0.29 億顆/cm³，是原生葉片 0.96 億顆/cm³ 的 30%。也就是根部已追上原生水準、葉片仍落後約 70%。為什麼根部追得上、葉片追不上？瓣膜根部兩端是和宿主肺動脈縫合的接口 (anastomoses)，宿主自己的血管壁細胞與血管表面內襯細胞 (endothelium) 直接從這兩個接口往中間爬過來，路很短。但葉片沒有自己的入口，必須等細胞先佔據根部、再爬到葉片底部表面，再從葉片底部慢慢往中段延伸——多了好幾段路、所以慢得多。

細胞身分長在哪一段？作者拿 62 週 Gen 1 那顆瓣膜的葉片 L3 切片：α-SMA 染色 (Fig. 6A) 顯示葉片內層散布著 SMA 陽性細胞、作者推測是 interstitial cells；同一片葉子相距 500 µm 的相鄰切片用 vWF 染色 (Fig. 6C)，可以看到 vWF 陽性的內襯細胞從葉片底部和三片葉片相接處 (commissure) 開始往葉片中段一路延伸——表示葉片表面是從這兩個入口逐漸蓋滿、不是整片同時長出。要注意作者寫的是「presumably interstitial cells」：α-SMA 不只貼 interstitial cells、血管平滑肌也會被貼到，光看亮不亮不能 100% 確定身分；作者是靠「這群細胞長在葉片內」這個位置線索做推測，原文才寫成「presumably」。而且 α-SMA 與 vWF 不是在同一張切片同時染、是兩張相鄰切片各染一種再對比，所以「葉片內層是 interstitial cells」這個結論其實是兩張畫面拼起來推出的。

時間軸怎麼選？12 與 20 週是 Gen 1 那兩顆提早因根部暴衝而被迫取出的瓣膜；36 與 62 週是另兩顆撐比較久才取出的 Gen 1，把四個時間點排在一起就能看細胞從植入到長期住下來的時間軸。根部追上原生「是長完了還是停下來了」？作者用 Ki67 抗體在根部數每張切片有多少比例細胞在分裂：早期 (12-20 週) 每 100 顆有 1.2 顆在分裂、晚期 (36-62 週) 降到 0.2 顆，少了約 6 倍。也就是說早期根部正在快速重塑、瓣膜外圈跟著長大；晚期分裂率掉下來等於衝刺期結束、進入穩定期，不是停滯。

葉片厚度不變、軟硬度也不變是這個故事的另一個關鍵。把四個時間點的葉片切片量厚度 (Fig. 4E)，看不到「越久越厚」這條趨勢線；把葉片切細條拉到斷 (Fig. 7A,B)，量到的最大應力 (UTS) 和硬度 (modulus) 也跟植入前、原生葉片沒差異。這件事為什麼值得寫成主線？因為過去組織工程瓣膜 (tissue-engineered heart valve, TEHV) 常見的一種失效模式是葉片變厚——細胞過度增生、過度產生膠原蛋白讓葉片變厚變硬、卡住開合。作者強調 7 顆瓣膜全程都沒看到葉片變厚，所以是有意義的「沒發生」陰性證據：表示這套膠原蛋白支架沒走過去 TEHV 走過的歪路。

Gen 2 比 Gen 1 多一層 sleeve，組織學上有什麼不同？外層 sleeve 已被宿主細胞填滿、跟宿主血管融合得不錯；但內層 tri-tube 本體的根部「外流端」(離開心臟那側) 仍未完全細胞化，「入流端」(靠近右心室那側) 倒是已完全細胞化。可能是入流端離心臟近、血流方向把細胞和氧氣先帶到入流端，外面又多包一層 sleeve 把外流端供應壓低一點。順便交代 62 週 Gen 1 那顆瓣膜的怪葉片 L1：同一顆瓣膜裡另一片葉子 L3 還維持原本高度、會跟著心跳開合，L1 變短、完全不會動了。染色看下去 L3 的 α-SMA 陽性細胞稀稀疏疏均勻散布、L1 則是一坨擠成一團硬卡在葉片中間 (Fig. 6E vs 6F insets)——同樣亮 α-SMA、行為可能完全不同。作者只在 52 與 62 週兩個時間點取切片，看不出這群細胞是 52 週前就在堆積、還是之後才湧進來，因此「細胞侵入造成葉片變短」這個因果方向沒辦法直接釘住。

這套組織學 + 免疫染色證據鏈的限制有五：第一，樣本太少 (Gen 1 共 4 隻、Gen 2 共 3 隻)，L1 異常還只在 1 隻 1 片葉子上看到；第二，免疫染色只做了 Gen 1，Gen 2 的細胞身分是「推測跟 Gen 1 差不多」、沒有直接證據；第三，染色拍的是取出那一刻的定格畫面，細胞走哪條路、以什麼速度進來只能靠不同時間點的定格反推；第四，「α-SMA 陽性 = interstitial cells」是基於位置 + α-SMA 陽性的合理推論、沒做同切片雙標記；第五，染色只能告訴你身上帶這個標記的細胞長在哪、不能告訴你它會不會做正事 (例如 vWF 陽性內襯能不能擋住血直接碰支架)。

主題六：用 HCl 水解搭配 colorimetric calcium kit 定量取出瓣膜的 Ca²⁺ 含量，發現 Gen 2 leaflet 中位數 0.54 μg Ca/mg dry tissue、Gen 1+Gen 2 outflow root 中位數 0.78 μg/mg，比同模型的 bovine Contegra valve 分別低 2.6 倍與 33 倍，而對照組 bovine valve 在 11 週時即因鈣化造成 stenotic occlusion 與 right heart failure 而死亡。

現有動物組織瓣膜植入前都要先用化學藥劑泡過，把組織「定型」、避免被宿主免疫系統當外來物攻擊；但被泡過的組織等於是死的，死細胞膜上帶負電的磷脂質會慢慢吸住血中的鈣鹽，越積越多，瓣膜因此變硬、變脆、失去彈性，這是現有瓣膜最常見的壞掉方式。所以驗一顆「活材料瓣膜」抗不抗鈣化，是必查的關卡。新瓣膜的玩法是植入前先用清潔劑把所有細胞洗光，剩下的純膠原蛋白骨架沒有那些磷脂質吸鈣位置、也沒有化學定型過；植入後骨架又被宿主細胞長滿、整段恢復成活組織，活細胞會主動把鈣離子收進細胞代謝、不讓鈣留在細胞外結晶。即使葉片填回只到原生的三成、中央仍有大段純膠原沒細胞，也不會變成鈣化熱點——這是它跟化學定型的動物瓣膜根本上不同的地方。

作者用兩條互補的方法看鈣化：一條是化學定量數字、一條是染色畫面。化學定量這邊，作者先把組織冷凍乾燥、秤好乾重，再泡進強酸 (HCl) 裡 110°C 加熱 20 小時把組織燒成一鍋液體，倒進等量強鹼把酸中和掉，最後用一種商用顏色變色試劑盒 (Calcium Detection Assay Kit，abcam ab102505；標準流程見本論文 Methods) 跑出每毫克乾組織裡有幾微克鈣 (μg Ca / mg dry tissue)。為什麼要先燒乾、強酸水解？因為鈣鹽埋在緻密的膠原蛋白裡，必須先把組織燒爛把鈣離子放出來試劑盒才測得到；冷凍乾燥則把組織裡的水抽乾，分母「每毫克乾組織」才不會被水重量稀釋。染色畫面這邊，作者用會把鈣顯成黑點的銀染 (von Kossa) 看切片：Gen 1 只在縫合線旁有零星黑點，Gen 2 葉片只看到極稀疏的小點。所謂 sparse, punctate microcalcification 意思是只看到一顆顆孤立、小於 100 微米的小點，不是連成一片的鈣化層，也沒有把葉片厚度撐起來；縫合線旁那些小點則是身體分解中的縫線留下的小破口殘餘物吸住鈣離子的暫時現象，不算失效徵兆。

數字上的差距相當清楚：Gen 2 葉片中位數 0.54 μg Ca/mg 乾組織，新瓣膜外圍管壁出口端 (outflow root) 中位數 0.78 μg/mg。同一個羔羊模型上的牛瓣膜葉片是 1.42 μg/mg、管壁是 25.8 μg/mg，差距分別 2.6 倍與 33 倍。脫細胞後、還沒植入的管材本身只帶 0.10-0.12 μg/mg 的鈣，跟葉片的 0.59、管壁的 0.84 比差一個數量級，這個底數可以忽略。葉片與管壁差距為什麼不一樣大？因為管壁本身是純膠原蛋白、植入後又被宿主細胞長滿、整段變成活組織，鈣化抑制最徹底；葉片填回比較慢、表面長期泡在血流裡，沒辦法把鈣壓得這麼低，因此兩處差距才會差到一個數量級。要注意這兩個牛瓣膜數字 (1.42 與 25.8) 不是本研究自己量的，而是同一個羔羊肺動脈模型裡，另一組人 (Flameng 2011, J Thorac Cardiovasc Surg) 已經發表的數據；作者用報告中的下限值 1.11 μg/mg 當基準跑單樣本 t 檢定。為什麼借別人的數字而不自己再放一批牛瓣膜對照羊？作者明說是為了少用實驗動物。outflow root 那個 n=6 不是 4+3=7，因為 Gen 1 那顆 12 週取出的瓣膜，管壁有一塊獨立的鈣化小結節，作者在事後把它從平均裡剔除；同一批 tube 做、植 20 週才取出的另一顆完全沒鈣化，所以作者把這個結節當成個案，這個排除作者寫在 Discussion 裡、沒有藏起來。

對照組那邊的失效則更具體：作者把一顆 20 mm 商用牛瓣膜 (Contegra) 縫進一隻 15 週齡、29 kg 的羔羊；植入後 11 週這隻羊就因右心衰竭死亡。解剖時看到葉片明顯增厚、整片散布鈣化，血流被堵住 (stenotic occlusion)，研究被迫提前終止。鈣化跟右心衰竭怎麼牽上的？瓣膜變硬、開口縮小後血液推不過去，右心室得用更高壓力才推得動，撐久了就垮掉，所以「鈣化堵塞 → 右心衰竭」是一條直接的因果鏈。為什麼 Flameng 2011 那批牛瓣膜要 5-6 個月才取出、本研究這顆 11 週就掛？因為本研究這隻羊只有 15 週齡，比 Flameng 那批 8-11 月齡的青春期羊年輕得多，已有文獻 (Flameng 2006) 指出年紀越小、動物瓣膜鈣化速度越快。新瓣膜本身就是植到 4 月齡（約 17 週）的幼羔羊，跟對照組的 15 週齡幾乎相同——同一研究裡的牛瓣膜 vs 新瓣膜這條比較其實是公平的。引用 Flameng 2011 在 8-11 月齡羊上的牛瓣膜數據，那批羊年齡比較大、鈣化速率比較慢，作者拿來當「對照組最低限」用，意思是真實差距只會更大。

這套抗鈣化證據的限制要先攤開：第一，樣本量很小，Gen 2 葉片只測 3 顆、管壁只測 6 顆，無法做大規模統計推論。第二，那個 1.42 與 25.8 μg/mg 的牛瓣膜對照值不是本研究自己量的，是借用同模型上 Flameng 2011 的數據，跨研究多少有偏差。第三，追蹤只到 52 週（約 1 年），鈣化是慢慢累積的過程，再久之後會不會悄悄堆起來這篇答不了。方法本身也有上限——強酸水解會把所有形式的鈣都釋放出來、總量抓得到，但分不出鈣是均勻散開還是集中在一小塊，所以前面那組 von Kossa 染色畫面是必要的補強。再來，羊的鈣化速度比人類快非常多，人類心臟瓣膜要 5-10 年才看到的鈣化程度，在羊體內幾個月就會出現，所以一年羊資料壓力上接近人類 5-10 年的等級，但這只是「鈣化壓力等效」、不是「時間等效」，超長期會發生什麼還是要看更久的後續研究。最後那顆 12 週 root 的鈣化結節，作者當成個案排除，因為同批管子做的 20 週瓣膜完全沒鈣化；但作者也誠實承認個別事件無法完全排除，將來樣本量大一點還是要持續盯著。

在進入數字之前，得先講清楚為什麼鈣化值得單拉出來量。市面上的動物組織瓣膜 (豬、牛) 為了避免被免疫系統攻擊，都會泡一種交聯劑 (glutaraldehyde) 把組織「定型」、再植進去。但這層定型讓組織變死的，沒辦法清除每天血流帶來的微量鈣磷；鈣鹽 (主要是 hydroxyapatite) 慢慢沉積在膠原與彈性纖維上、組織變硬、葉片開不開、瓣膜就失效。在兒童身上更快，因為兒童鈣磷代謝活躍。新瓣膜如果只勝在「會跟著長大」但鈣化跟商用瓣膜一樣快，那也撐不久。

鈣化量怎麼量？作者把取出的組織烘乾秤「乾重」，在 6 M 鹽酸 (HCl) 裡 110°C 燒 20 小時，把組織的有機部分整個拆散成液體、鈣離子游離出來，再加 6 M 鹼 (NaOH) 中和。把這液體灌進一種會跟鈣離子顯色的商用套組 (colorimetric calcium kit，abcam ab102505)、按廠商指引讀顏色深淺，反推鈣含量；再除以乾重就得到「每毫克乾組織含多少微克鈣」。為什麼除乾重而不是濕重？濕重會受組織含水量左右、不同樣本含水量差很多；乾重相對穩定，跨樣本才能比；同時這個領域的鈣化文獻 (例如 Flameng 2011) 都用 µg/mg dry tissue 當單位，作者跟著用才能直接跟對照組比。

量出來的數字怎麼讀？三隻 Gen 2 各取一塊葉片做鈣定量 (n=3)，平均 0.59±0.24 μg Ca/mg 乾組織、中位數 0.54。對照組 Contegra 牛瓣膜葉片在同型實驗 (Flameng 2011) 的數字是 1.42 μg/mg，Gen 2 葉片只有牛瓣膜的 38%、低了 2.6 倍 (Fig. 7H)。再把 Gen 1 四顆加 Gen 2 三顆共 7 顆瓣膜的 root 出口端各取一塊，量得 0.84±0.42 μg/mg、中位數 0.78（作者另外扣掉一個 12 週瓣膜 root 上的 focal calcific nodule，因為那個明顯是局部異常）。對照 Contegra 牛瓣膜出口端 25.8 μg/mg，本研究瓣膜的 root 只有牛瓣膜的 1/33、低了 33 倍。為什麼葉片差 2.6 倍、外圈差 33 倍——兩邊差這麼多？因為 Contegra 是牛頸靜脈做的，葉片以外的 conduit 部分本來就是厚血管壁、化學交聯區域更多、鈣化更嚴重，對照組數字本來就高，整除下來倍數大。同時本研究的 root 細胞密度已追上 native、活細胞清除效率高，所以根部端鈣量更低、倍數更大。葉片那邊細胞填回慢、清除力沒那麼強，倍數因此小一些。

光看倍數還不夠，作者在同一個羔羊模型裡直接同步植入了一顆 20 mm Contegra 牛瓣膜當對照。結果這隻羊植入 11 週就死了：葉片快速鈣化、變厚、瓣膜開口被擋住 (stenotic occlusion)，血流通不過、阻力急升，右心要把血擠過去擠不動、就衰竭了 (right heart failure)。解剖切片證實葉片明顯鈣化變厚 (Fig. 4M、5O、5P)，是典型的鈣化型失效。為什麼這個對照組重要？因為它把「同型動物、同型手術、同段時間」下現行最好臨床選項的失效時間直接擺出來：本研究瓣膜撐到 52 週還幾乎沒鈣化、對照組 11 週就因鈣化堵死。倍數比較不必依賴跨文獻、跨動物模型的間接推算，是「相對於現行臨床標準，這個瓣膜抗鈣化幾倍」最強的單一證據。

為什麼這個瓣膜不會走商用瓣膜的鈣化老路？三個可能機制疊在一起：第一，材料沒泡交聯劑、沒有那些會「招呼」鈣離子停下來的化學殘基 (Ca²⁺ nucleation site)；第二，ECM 直接由 fibroblast 織出來、組成接近 native，免疫系統不視為異物、不啟動慢性發炎這條導致鈣化的路徑；第三，宿主細胞已填回 root、活細胞可以主動把微量鈣清掉。三個因素合起來抑制了鈣化，也呼應前面看到的「root 鈣化倍數比 leaflet 更低」——細胞填越滿的位置、抗鈣化越強。

Pediatric tri-tube valved conduits made from fibroblast-produced extracellular matrix evaluated over 52 weeks in growing lambs

There is a need for replacement heart valves that can grow with children. We fabricated tubes of fibroblast-derived collagenous matrix that have been shown to regenerate and grow as a pulmonary artery replacement in lambs and implemented a design for a valved conduit consisting of three tubes sewn together. Seven lambs were implanted with tri-tube valved conduits in sequential cohorts and compared to bioprosthetic conduits. Valves implanted into the pulmonary artery of two lambs of the first cohort of four animals functioned with mild regurgitation and systolic pressure drops <10 mmHg up to 52 weeks after implantation, during which the valve diameter increased from 19 mm to a physiologically normal ~25 mm. In a second cohort, the valve design was modified to include an additional tube, creating a sleeve around the tri-tube valve to counteract faster root growth relative to the leaflets. Two valves exhibited trivial-to-mild regurgitation at 52 weeks with similar diameter increases to ~25 mm and systolic pressure drops of <5 mmHg, whereas the third valve showed similar findings until moderate regurgitation was observed at 52 weeks, correlating to hyperincrease in the valve diameter. In all explanted valves, the leaflets contained interstitial cells and an endothelium progressing from the base of the leaflets and remained thin and pliable with sparse, punctate microcalcifications. The tri-tube valves demonstrated reduced calcification and improved hemodynamic function compared to clinically used pediatric bioprosthetic valves tested in the same model. This tri-tube valved conduit has potential for long-term valve growth in children.

INTRODUCTION

Unlike a wide variety of mechanical and bioprosthetic heart valves routinely implanted and often now delivered via catheter into adult patients, a heart valve that can grow and maintain function for pediatric patients has not yet been demonstrated. According to the Society of Thoracic Surgeons Congenital Heart Surgery National Database, over a 4-year period ending in June 2016, there were 985 pulmonary valve replacements and 1111 right ventricle to pulmonary artery replacements (typically valved conduits) or about 500 pulmonary valve replacements per year (1). At least 230 and up to 350 aortic valve replacements were performed per year because of aortic insufficiency and/or aortic stenosis (1). The only accepted options for these children are valves made from chemically fixed tissues that often become dysfunctional due to calcification and require replacement because they lack growth capacity due to the chemical fixation. These children will often need to endure at least one and up to five (or more) open heart surgeries (2) until a mechanical valve is implanted in adulthood, requiring lifelong anticoagulation therapy (3). A valved conduit that could be implanted during the initial surgery and then grows somatically, including development of a functional endothelium, would alleviate surgical burden for these children and their families and reduce health care costs.

Here, we proposed a solution using a hybrid of tissue engineering and regenerative medicine. Using classical tissue engineering, we grew tissues in vitro to generate biologically engineered bioactive material [extracellular matrix (ECM)] upon decellularization. Upon implantation, the matrix scaffold relies upon intrinsic host processes to become recellularized and to regenerate into the target functional tissue, which is the principle of regenerative medicine. Specifically, we have grown biological tubes in vitro from human dermal fibroblasts entrapped in sacrificial fibrin gel tubes (4). Over a period of about 8 weeks, the fibroblasts converted the fibrin gel tube into a tube of circumferentially aligned dense collagenous matrix, which undergoes detergent decellularization to create an allograft that can be stored for at least 6 months without loss of mechanical properties (5). The tube exhibited a nonlinear stress-strain curve typical of native valve roots and leaflets (6), and the resulting tensile mechanical properties were comparable to ovine pulmonary valve leaflets in terms of modulus and ultimate tensile stress (UTS) measured in strain-to-failure testing (6). Collagen contents were also comparable (7). Preclinical studies in sheep up to 12 months duration revealed that decellularized grafts grown from ovine dermal fibroblasts (ODFs) become populated by appropriate host cells (including endothelium formation) without a sustained inflammatory response, overt immune response, or visible calcification upon implantation as arterial grafts and tubular heart valves (5, 7–9) and grow as a pulmonary artery replacement with young lambs into adulthood (10). Similar results have been observed using vascular grafts grown from human dermal fibroblasts implanted into baboons as arteriovenous grafts for up to 6 months (11), further indicating the clinical potential of these biological tubes of allogeneic fibroblast-produced collagenous matrix with somatic growth potential (10).

In a previous study, we developed and tested a pediatric valved conduit design based on these tubes and degradable suture. One tube was placed inside a second tube, and the tubes were sutured together at the base with three equally spaced vertical suture lines, creating a tubular heart valve with three leaflets because the inner tube was constrained from collapsing inward at those three commissures (7). Commissure stability relies on host cells to recellularize the tubes that lead to their cohesion via deposition of ECM before suture degradation. This tube-in-tube valve exhibited short-term function in a growing lamb model, but it manifested a failure mode of leaflet height reduction after 12 to 22 weeks of implantation (7). The tubes had fused together along the degradable suture line, essentially forming leaflets in situ; however, the initial locations of the commissures were several millimeters above the points of closest approach of the ultimate leaflets; this occurred in the absence of any leaflet thickening, suggesting that the suture points defining the commissures at implantation were not stable in long-term exposure to cyclic diastolic (downward) force on the inner tube (7). We speculate that this caused a migration of the inner tube through the suture line at the commissures over time and led to an effective decrease in leaflet height with attendant central valvular regurgitation before the possibility of valve growth. Although recellularization occurred, it was apparently insufficient to cause or maintain tube cohesion along the entire suture line, specifically, the commissures. Cells were present on most of the leaflet surfaces as early as 12 weeks after implantation, but they had not extensively recellularized the interior at 22 weeks as would be needed for an increase in coaptation area by leaflet growth to occur; in contrast, the valve root was extensively recellularized by 12 weeks (7).

To prevent the failure mode of unstable commissures observed in the original tube-in-tube design, we developed a tri-tube valved conduit design in which three tubes were sutured together in a closed ring, with the bottom of each tube sutured closed to form its own leaflet (Fig. 1, A and B). The crucial difference in the commissures with this new tri-tube valve design is evident when comparing the connection of the three tubes (Fig. 1C) with the connection of the two tubes in the original tube-in-tube design (Fig. 1D). In the new tri-tube design, the downward diastolic force on the leaflets is going to be predominately carried by the bulk matrix as opposed to being carried by the suture in the original tube-in-tube design.

Here, we present computational mechanical analysis supporting the improved commissure stability of the tri-tube valve design, hereafter referred to as Gen 1. Pulse duplicator testing showed acceptable valve function under pulmonary valve flow conditions relevant for a child. Ultrasonography was performed 1 week, 1 month, and then bimonthly after implantation of the Gen 1 valve into the pulmonary artery of four lambs in one cohort for up to 62 weeks. A second cohort was followed for up to 52 weeks after implantation wherein an additional tube was used to create a sleeve around the tri-tube valve, hereafter referred to as Gen 2; the Gen 2 design yielded an improved outcome over Gen 1 valves. Mechanical and biomechanical properties, dimensions, and histology of the preimplant and explanted valves were used to characterize regeneration of the matrix. Results for calcification and function of the tri-tube valved conduit (simply “valve” hereafter) are compared to reported studies for clinically used pediatric bioprosthetic valved conduits tested in this growing lamb model.

RESULTS

Valve fabrication and characterization

Gen 1 valves of 19 mm diameter were fabricated using three 16-mm-diameter biologically engineered tubes derived from ovine fibroblasts embedded in fibrin gel. After decellularization, each tube was trimmed to remove a rectangular section to define the leaflet resulting from subsequent suturing (Fig. 1A). The tubes were sutured together into a closed ring using degradable sutures with three suture lines spanning the length along their point of contact, and the bottom of each tube was sutured closed to create a leaflet, yielding a tri-leaflet valve (Fig. 1B). The top view of a tube-in-tube valve and a tri-tube valve (Gen 1) with the tubes color coded (Fig. 1, C and D) facilitates comparison of commissure construction. The leaflet shape of the tri-tube valve before implantation is seen in a valve slit open and laid flat (Fig. 1E). A representative surgical field shows the suture lines of the valve under pressure (Fig. 1F). The Gen 2 valve was created by slitting an additional tube longitudinally and suturing it around the Gen 1 tri-tube valve to create a sleeved tri-tube valve, with the added sleeve over the tri-tube valve apparent from its increased opacity in the surgical field (Fig. 1G).

Computational analysis of the solid stress generated during valve closure was performed using ANSYS for both the tube-in-tube valve and Gen 1 tri-tube valve. The stress concentration that occurred at the commissure sutures for the tube-in-tube valve did not occur in the Gen 1 tri-tube valve (Fig. 1, H and I). Stress concentration still occurred at the Gen 1 valve commissures but was continuous within the matrix because the valve lacks commissure sutures, suggesting improved commissure stability.

The decellularized tissue tubes from a representative batch were compared to ovine pulmonary valve leaflets and to the standard-of-care for pediatric pulmonary valve replacement, the chemically cross-linked bovine jugular vein valve (Contegra 200 valved conduit, simply “bovine valve” hereafter) and porcine aortic valve (Hancock 150 valved conduit, simply “porcine valve” hereafter). Tube thickness was 0.66 ± 0.15 mm compared to 0.32 ± 0.09 mm for native ovine pulmonary valve leaflets (n = 3) (Fig. 2A). The tensile strength (circumferential) was similar in magnitude and anisotropy among the bovine, native pulmonary leaflets, and tube groups but appeared substantially higher for the porcine valve leaflets (UTS ~2 MPa versus 14 MPa; Fig. 2B). The tensile stiffness (circumferential) of the tube was comparable to the native leaflet and lower than the bovine leaflets and appeared lower than the porcine leaflets (modulus ~5 MPa versus 10 and 21 MPa, respectively; Fig. 2C). The tube suture pullout force was 92 ± 13 gram-force (n = 9) as compared to 244 ± 31 gram-force (n = 3) for the native pulmonary artery of a sheep (data file S1). Decellularized tissue histology shows collagen bands (stained blue green) aligned in the circumferential direction with remnant fibrin (stained red) on the luminal surface and no intact cell nuclei in the tube matrix (Fig. 2D). Proteomics directed at ~250 distinct ECM proteins or protein isoforms was used to assess differences in their density for the four batches of biologically engineered tubes used to construct the seven valves implanted. Total fibrillar collagen showed differences between batches, whereas no difference existed for specific collagen types among the batches (Fig. 2F). Valve durability and hydrodynamics

Durability of Gen 1 valves (n = 2) was assessed in accelerated fatigue testing at 10 Hz with 10 mmHg end-diastolic pressure drop for at least 95% of all cycles conforming with ISO 5840-2015 Part 1, Table E.7 for pediatric right side valves (fig. S2A) (12). Periodic high-speed video revealed that valves fully opened but did not fully close under these conditions (fig. S2B). Periodic inspection of the valves during the testing period revealed that suture pullout occurred along the suture line made at the bottom of the tube (to form a leaflet) in at least one of the tubes between 9.2M and 13.5M cycles for both valves (fig. S2C). No damage was evident at any of the commissures of either valve (fig. S2D). Substantial fungal contamination in the test fluid was evident despite the use of antimicrobial and antifungal agents and periodic fluid change and system cleaning.

Given the acceptable performance of the Gen 1 valve under pulmonary valve flow conditions for a young lamb, four lambs (average of 4 months old, 28 kg) were each implanted with a Gen 1 valve interpositionally in the pulmonary artery after removing the leaflets of the native pulmonary valve. The lamb model was chosen to assess the ability of the valve to grow and maintain function in the same ovine species that is the standard for preclinical testing, because its heart is comparable in size to the human and bioprosthetic valves that will eventually calcify in the human will typically calcify within months in a sheep (13). Three-dimensional epicardial echocardiography was performed at implantation. Ultrasound images for the Gen 1 tri-tube valve immediately (day 0) after implantation are shown in fig. S4. The measured coaptation length of the leaflets taken from the long-axis view was 3 to 5 mm (data file S2). Valve performance metrics from transthoracic echocardiography conducted 1-week post-operation are reported in table S3. All valve leaflets had normal leaflet motion with full coaptation (overlap of leaflets when bending inward, resulting in valve closure) during diastole and nil regurgitation (movie S4). Systolic pressure drop across the valves was <5 mmHg except in one animal where, due to suturing, a narrow distal anastomosis created a systolic pressure drop of 15 mmHg (table S3). Another animal had a high heart rate [189 beats per minute (bpm)] during echocardiography, although typical stroke volume, that led to high cardiac output.

The progression of this Gen 1 cohort in terms of animal weight, valve size, and valve function until explantation is shown in Fig. 3. A 52-week implantation was planned if valve regurgitation remained less than moderate. The animals were all gaining weight before explantation (Fig. 3A), with increased valve diameter and increased EOA (Fig. 3, C and D). Valve function degraded and valve diameter increased markedly between weeks 4 and 12 in two animals, leading to explantation after just 12 and 20 weeks, respectively (Fig. 3C). Two valves implanted functioned acceptably for 28 and 52 weeks (movies S5 and S6), although their explantation at 36 and 62 weeks, respectively, was also dictated by a valve diameter increase coinciding with degraded valve function. In all four animals, degraded valve function was due to increased regurgitation associated with a valve diameter greater than an expected ultimate diameter of 24 to 25 mm based on our prior pulmonary artery replacement study (10). The progressive regurgitation is reflected in the increased cardiac output to abnormally high values (Fig. 3B). For comparison, data are also shown for a 20-week study of a porcine valve (Hancock 150 bioprosthetic valved conduit) in the same growing lamb model previously reported in aggregate along with adult sheep outcomes (14) and a bovine valve (Contegra 200 valved conduit) implanted as part of this study for 11 weeks until the animal died from right heart failure (Fig. 3, A, B, D, and E).

Gen 2 valve

Three additional lambs (average of 3.5 months old, 32 kg) were implanted interpositionally with a Gen 2 valve in the pulmonary artery after removing the leaflets of the native pulmonary valve, as for the Gen 1 valves. The ECM proteins in the tubes used to construct the Gen 2 valves were similar to the tubes for the Gen 1 valves, as previously noted. Ultrasound images for the Gen 2 valve 1-week after implantation are shown in fig. S4. The measured coaptation length of the leaflets taken from the long axis view was 3 to 5 mm as for the Gen 1 valves (data file S2). Valve performance metrics from transthoracic echocardiography conducted 1-week post-operation are reported in table S3. All valve leaflets had normal leaflet motion with full coaptation during diastole and nil regurgitation (movie S7), and systolic pressure drop across the valves was <5 mmHg in two animals but elevated in one animal (17 mmHg).

Figure 3 also shows the progression of the Gen 2 cohort. As in the Gen 1 cohort, a 52-week implantation was planned if valve function remained better than moderate regurgitation. The first two animals in the Gen 2 cohort both exhibited only trivial-to-mild regurgitation over the 52-week duration (movies S8 and S9) with systolic pressure drops <5 mm Hg at the end point (Fig. 3E), and the same was true for the third animal through 44 weeks until development of moderate regurgitation at 52 weeks (movie S10) coinciding with a valve diameter increase to 27 mm (Fig. 3C).

Although there was no difference (P > 0.05) between the Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves based on statistical analysis of Kaplan-Meier survival curves for freedom from moderate regurgitation (Fig. 3F), attributable to the small group sizes and large variation in the Gen 1 survival times, the valve performance was different based on the insufficiency index at explantation (Fig. 3G) as a time-independent comparative measure (P = 0.015; Gen 1 = 3.5 ± 0.4 and Gen 2 = 2.2 ± 0.8). Lastly, the Kaplan-Meier survival curves for the new tri-tube design (Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves combined) were different from the prior tube-in-tube design (P = 0.0024, log-rank Mantel-Cox test; assumed, actual hazard ratio = 0.125, 0.123) (fig. S5), consistent with expectation based on its new commissure design.

Valve morphology at explantation

Gen 1 valve

Gross imaging upon explantation showed that all four Gen 1 valves had three intact leaflets each (Fig. 4, A to D), with no degradable suture evident, and that they were shaped much differently than the leaflets of the tri-tube valves imaged before implantation. Figure 4 (A to D) also shows separation that developed between the commissures and that the leaflets approximately maintained their initial dimensions as the valve diameter increased. There was no temporal trend in thickness of the leaflets or root with implant duration, but the explanted root was thicker than the implanted root (Fig. 4E); both were thinner than the native root (Fig. 4E). There was no difference in thickness between native leaflets and the implanted or explanted leaflets (Fig. 4E). There was a weak linear trend of decreasing leaflet height with time (R2 = 0.33; Fig. 4F); the explanted leaflets were collectively shorter than the preimplant leaflets (Fig. 4F).

Gen 2 valve

Figure 4 (G to I) shows gross images of the three explanted valves, which had three intact leaflets each that were reshaped during implantation. Small leaflet separation developed at one commissure in two animals, consistent with their trivial-to-mild regurgitation and stable ~25 mm diameter, whereas an oval valve shape also developed in the third animal, consistent with its moderate regurgitation and excessive 27-mm valve diameter. Figure 4 (J to L) conveys the native valve appearance of these three 52-week valves in situ at explantation, in stark contrast to the 11-week bovine valve (Fig. 4M) and 20-week porcine valves (Fig. 4N). The thicknesses of the leaflets and root of the Gen 2 cohort were comparable to the first cohort (Fig. 4E). All three animals in the Gen 2 cohort had explanted leaflets that were essentially the same 11-mm height compared to the preimplant leaflets (Fig. 4F) and had not thickened (Fig. 4E).

Valve histology at explantation

Gen 1 valve

The histology for these valves (Fig. 5, A to D) is consistent with the apparent growth of the root but not obviously of the leaflets, with many more cells across the thickness of the root versus the leaflets. Sparse, punctate microcalcification was evident from von Kossa staining in some leaflet regions of the valve explanted at 62 weeks and occasional deposits in association with the degradable suture lines at the anastomoses (Fig. 5, E to H). In addition, there was one focal calcification present in the root in the 12-week valve (Fig. 5E) that was not observed in any other valves, including the 20-week valve that was constructed from the same batch of tubes.

Gen 2 valve

The trichrome histology shown in Fig. 5 (I to K) for these three explanted valves is similar to the Gen 1 cohort, with higher cellularity in the root versus the leaflets. Again, leaflet recellularization appeared to progress from cells migrating primarily over the surfaces from the root. However, a key difference in this Gen 2 cohort is that although recellularization of the sleeve was extensive, recellularization of the root of the tri-tube valve was incomplete in the outflow region, in contrast to the explanted Gen 1 valves that did not include the sleeve. Recellularization of the root of the inflow region was complete in the Gen 2 valves, as for the Gen 1 valves. Another notable difference in the Gen 2 valves was an apparent increase in the leaflet free-edge length at 52 weeks, being longer by 17 to 19%, 22 to 37%, and 25 to 34% as measured from ultrasound images at sacrifice (data file S2). Von Kossa staining showed no evidence of any gross calcification in the Gen 2 valves explanted at 52 weeks, with only sparse, punctate microcalcification evident in some leaflet regions (Fig. 5, L to N). In contrast, there was substantial calcification in grossly thickened leaflets of the bovine valve that failed at 11 weeks without any evidence of infection (Fig. 5, O and P).

Valve immunohistochemistry

Focusing on the Gen 1 valve implanted for 62 weeks as compared to the one implanted 36-weeks, leaflet recellularization appeared to progress from cells migrating primarily over the surfaces from the root as seen in the histology. Several recellularization patterns occurred within and between leaflets, which are elucidated using immunohistochemistry for the 62-week valve in Fig. 6 (A to C). In a leaflet that retained nearly full height for this valve as seen in its gross image (L3), a section shows a substantial number of α-smooth muscle actin (SMA)–positive cells, presumably interstitial cells (Fig. 6A). Von Willebrand factor (vWF) staining of another section of L3 located ~500 µm away indicated formation of an endothelium emanating from both the base of the leaflet and the commissure region (Fig. 6C). A comparison of the nuclear staining in these two sections indicated a substantial difference in the number of cells that had invaded into the leaflet interstitium; however, a large portion of the leaflet surfaces were covered with cells in both cases. The one leaflet that was shorter in the 62-week valve (L1) exhibited a focal collective infiltration of SMA-positive cells with distinctive morphology (Fig. 6B, compare insets of magnified fields in E and F). The two valves were explanted at 12 and 20 weeks due to their rapid increase in diameter and resultant regurgitation motivated their characterization as well. After 20 weeks of implantation, the leaflet surfaces were covered with cells, although there were fewer cells that had invaded into the leaflet interstitium and less endothelium formation as compared to the 62-week valve (Fig. 6, C and D), as might be expected. Given the similarity between the histology of the Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves, immunohistochemistry was not performed for Gen 2 valves.

Valve composition and mechanical properties at explantation

Gen 1 valve

Despite the substantial histological differences between the high-cellularity growing root and the low-cellularity nongrowing leaflets at these extended time points, the modulus and UTS of the root and leaflets did not exhibit a temporal trend and were not different from preimplant values or native values (Fig. 7, A to D). Similarly, the explanted collagen and total protein contents exhibited no temporal trend and were not different from preimplant tube values (Fig. 7, E and F). The explanted leaflet modulus anisotropy \((E_{\mathrm{circ}}/E_{\mathrm{rad}})\) did not exhibit a temporal trend and with value 1.8 ± 0.5 (i.e., stiffer in the circumferential direction, the direction of alignment of the preimplant tube) was comparable to the preimplant tube (2.0 ± 0.4) and native leaflet (2.3 ± 0.6) (Fig. 7B). However, the explanted roots did not have a modulus anisotropy, in contrast to the preimplant tube (2.0 ± 0.4) and native root (2.9 ± 0.22) (Fig. 7D). The collagen contents of the explanted roots and leaflets were not different from the preimplant values or the native root (Fig. 7E), although the protein content of the explanted roots was greater than the explanted leaflets (Fig. 7F). Consistent with that difference and the histology, the roots had higher cellularity than the leaflets (Fig. 7G). The mean cell concentration in the explant roots for the four valves (123 ± 68 million cells/cm³) was not different from native root (155 ± 10 million cells/cm³); however, the cell concentration of the explanted leaflets (29 ± 20 million cells/cm³) was 70% lower compared to native leaflets (96 ± 8 million cells/cm³).

The two valves were explanted at 12 and 20 weeks due to their rapid increase in diameter, and resultant regurgitation motivated additional characterization of these two valves in addition to histological assessment. Ki67 immunostaining revealed a higher frequency of dividing cells in the root (1.23 ± 0.59% for the 12- and 20-week valves as compared to 0.20 ± 0.29% for the 36- and 62-week valves, n = 15 fields totaling >6000 cells) (data file S3), and DNA quantitation revealed a higher density of cells in the root of the 20-week valve (227 cells/cm³) as compared to the 36- and 62-week valves (140 and 77 cells/cm³, respectively); however, cell density was not higher in the 12-week valve (66 cells/cm³) (data file S3). These findings are consistent overall with the greatly increased valve diameter from 4 weeks to 12 and 20 weeks being due to cellular remodeling of root tissue.

Gen 2 valve

The modulus and UTS of the root for the explanted Gen 2 valves were similar to the Gen 1 valves (Fig. 7, C and D). Gen 2 leaflets had higher values; however, they were not different from the preimplant values for the batch of tubes used to fabricate the Gen 2 valves, as was also true for the Gen 1 valves. Unlike the Gen 1 valves, the collagen density of the Gen 2 valve roots was increased over the preimplant value (Fig. 7E). Similar to the Gen 1 valves, the protein density of the Gen 2 valve roots was increased over the preimplant value (Fig. 7F). Cellularity of the Gen 2 valve root increased to the value of the ovine pulmonary artery as did the Gen 1 valve root, and the Gen 2 leaflet cellularity was comparable to the Gen 1 leaflets (both ~33% of native leaflets) (Fig. 7G). Quantitative measurement of calcification from randomly selected biopsies yielded 0.59 ± 0.24 µg/mg dry weight (n = 3) in the leaflets for the Gen 2 valves (Fig. 7H). The outflow segment of the valve roots measured 0.84 ± 0.42 µg/mg dry weight (n = 6) for the three Gen 2 valves averaged with the four Gen 1 valves, which retrospectively excluded the aforementioned calcific nodule noted for the 12-week explanted valve. Median values are shown in Fig. 7H. Preimplant matrix contained 0.10 to 0.12 µg/mg dry weight (n = 2) (data file S4). Collectively, these properties imparted valve function and maintenance of large orifice area with only trace-to-mild regurgitation observed in all three Gen 2 valves for 44 weeks and two of the valves for 52 weeks despite the reduction in coaptation length evident in diastole compared to 1 week (Fig. 8), presumably offset by the increase in free-edge length.

DISCUSSION

Here, we report the results of in vitro testing and in vivo assessment of a tri-tube valve design where diastolic forces are carried predominantly by the collagenous matrix comprising the tubes and not the suture that initially attaches them together. Improved commissure stability of the tri-tube design relative to our previous tube-in-tube design (7) is suggested by the occurrence of stress concentration at the commissure sutures in the tube-in-tube design, which is absent in the tri-tube design, as shown using computational mechanical analysis. The tri-tube valve was shown here to exhibit acceptable hydrodynamic performance in a pulse duplicator. Markedly improved function of the tri-tube valve over the tube-in-tube valve was demonstrated in a growing lamb model.

Pronounced commissure separation in the Gen 1 (tri-tube) valves after implantation resulted from rapid growth of the root at the commissures relative to the leaflets, which led to increasing regurgitation. This motivated the Gen 2 (sleeved tri-tube) valve, the sleeve being an additional tube of the biologically engineered matrix placed around the tri-tube valve. The improvement in long-term performance in the Gen 2 valve was notable. Two animals that reached the 52-week end point both exhibited a stable valve diameter after growth from 19 to ~25 mm, an increase in EOA from ~1.8 to ~3.2 cm², only trivial-to-mild regurgitation, and a systolic pressure gradient less than 10 mmHg at all times (and decreasing by 52 weeks to less than 5 mmHg). The third animal developed moderate regurgitation at 52 weeks coinciding with hyperincreased valve diameter, although it had thin, pliable, leaflets of full height and with systolic gradient <10 mmHg at all times. These results were compared to a bioprosthetic valve widely used in pediatric right ventricular outflow tract (RVOT) reconstruction (15, 16), the chemically processed porcine aortic valve mounted within a woven fabric sleeve (marketed as the Hancock 150 valve). The porcine valve was evaluated in the same growing lamb model. There was an obvious deterioration in the performance of the porcine valve evident from decreasing EOA and increasing systolic pressure gradient to nearly 20 mmHg (Fig. 3). The same was true for the animal implanted with a chemically processed bovine jugular vein valve (marketed as the Contegra valve), which died after 11 weeks due to calcification and thrombus resulting in stenotic occlusion and thus heart failure, the study being discontinued.

In one retrospective analysis of 63 pediatric patients implanted with a porcine valve between 2000 and 2010, freedom from failure, defined as severe conduit regurgitation or stenosis with a main pulmonary artery systolic gradient over 60 mmHg, was 100, 96, and 83% after 1, 3, and 5 years, respectively (16). The mean systolic gradient, found to be exclusively at the valvular level across the stenotic valved conduit was 87 ± 11 mmHg, consistent with porcine valve results in the growing lamb model and in stark contrast to our Gen 2 valve. The improved performance of the Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves at 20 weeks based on echocardiography (Table 1) was not unexpected when comparing the images of the Gen 2 valves explanted at 52 weeks with the porcine valves explanted at 20 weeks or the bovine valve explanted postmortem at 11 weeks, which revealed pronounced calcification and distortion of the leaflets (Fig. 4).

We made comparison of our Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves to performance data on the porcine valve in the same growing lamb model previously reported in aggregate along with adult sheep outcomes (14). In addition to improved EOA and systolic gradient of the Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves over the first 20 weeks of implantation as compared to the porcine valve in the same growing lamb model, these valves constructed from the biologically engineered matrix also exhibited stronger calcification resistance. The newer bovine valve was shown to resist calcification in a similar growing lamb model among six stentless bioprosthetic valves tested (17), and we thus made comparison to these results to minimize animal usage when comparison control data in the same model are available, as for the porcine valve performance comparison. A median of 0.78 µg calcium/mg dry weight in the outflow of the root was measured for the seven Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves implanted for an average time of 9.4 ± 4.3 months as compared to more than a 33-fold greater 25.8 µg/mg dry weight reported for the bovine valve implanted for 5 to 6 months in older adolescent sheep (age 8 to 11 months); a median of 0.54 µg calcium/mg dry weight in the leaflets was measured for the three Gen 2 valves implanted for 12 months compared to the 2.6-fold greater 1.42 µg/mg dry weight reported for the bovine valve, also statistically different (17). The extensive calcification that occurred in leaflets of the bovine valve implanted in our study likely reflects the younger lamb used in our study, wherein acceleration of calcification has been reported (18).

The leaflets of our 19-mm-diameter valve were designed to have 3 to 5 mm of coaptation length (as measured in the long-axis view) to accommodate an anticipated rapid increase in valve diameter before leaflet growth due to their slower recellularization, without losing coaptation. It has been reported that the pulmonary artery is ~19-mm internal diameter (ID) in lambs of age 4 months and ~23-mm ID at age 12 months (19), consistent with our previously reported increase to 24 to 25 mm of a 16-mm-ID tube of the same matrix into the pulmonary artery of young lambs growing to 12 months (10). On the basis of in vitro hydrodynamic testing, the regurgitant fraction, EOA, and systolic pressure gradient of the Gen 1 valve were comparable to the bovine and porcine valves. In addition, ultrasound analysis at 1 week showed no evidence of regurgitation or abnormal closing volume in all seven animals implanted with tri-tube valves. This was true even though the cardiac flow rate was ~25% higher than the value used for in vitro testing, indicating robust valve performance. Five of the seven implanted valves had a systolic pressure drop <5 mmHg 1 week after implantation. In two other cases, the pressure drops were elevated (15 and 17 mmHg) because of a narrow distal anastomosis, inadvertently due to suturing. With our use of degradable suture, this narrow anastomosis and associated gradient were expected to resolve as previous work has shown the growth of our biologically engineered material and increase in diameter at anastomoses in this model (7). Resolution occurred in these cases, with gradients <5 mmHg by 12 weeks in both cases. Excluding these two cases, the other Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves exhibited a 2.8-fold lower systolic gradient 1 week after implantation than the porcine valve, possibly reflecting the higher stiffness of the porcine tissue, which had a ~2- and 3-fold higher circumferential modulus compared to the Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves, respectively.

The failure mode of commissure instability after 12- to 16-week implantation in our previous tube-in-tube valve design (7) was resolved with this tri-tube design, consistent with the diastolic forces now being predominately carried by the bulk matrix of the tubes as opposed to being carried by the suture in the original tube-in-tube design and consistent with the computational model and durability testing. None of the explanted Gen 1 valves exhibited extreme (~50%) leaflet shortening that was previously observed in the tube-in-tube valves due to a hypothesized commissure instability (7). Two of the four Gen 1 valves functioned longer (28 and 52 weeks) and had leaflet heights at explantation comparable to the preimplant value of 11 mm; however, new failure modes occurred.

The early, marked increase in the diameters of the two Gen 1 valves explanted at 12 and 20 weeks was unexpected. This diameter increase did not appear to be aneurysmal as the collagen density in the root and its thickness were similar to preimplantation values. The comparable or higher cell density and higher frequency of dividing cells as compared to the valves explanted at 36 and 62 weeks are also consistent with the occurrence of root growth rather than root thinning. These two valves exhibited a much higher systolic pressure gradient at week 4 (11 to 12 mm Hg), which matches the subsequent rapid diameter increase with a lag time of 8 to 16 weeks. The increased gradient implies that a higher luminal pressure was present and greater valve distension occurred. Many cell types are known to be stimulated by cyclic stretching, which has been used as a strategy for inducing tissue growth in vitro (20–23), so the subsequent rapid diameter increase could have been triggered by cells repopulating the matrix and subjected to abnormal stretching 8 to 16 weeks earlier. If this was the causal factor, it could be mitigated or preempted by using stiffer tubes to construct the valves (e.g., cultured to produce more collagen and/or more highly cross-linked collagen). However, these two valves were constructed from the same batch of tubes, whereas the other two Gen 1 valves that maintained the expected diameter and functioned much longer were constructed from two different batches. Proteomics was conducted to ascertain whether there were any differences in the starting matrix composition that might explain the outcomes. The proportions of matrix proteins among seven functional classes were not different among the three batches used to construct the Gen 1 valves except for fibrillar collagen, although there were no differences by collagen type among the three batches. The ECM proteomics suggests that differences in the matrix composition among the batches are unlikely to explain the observed differences in Gen 1 outcomes. However, because the proteomics library does not probe for all possible ECM constituents nor any ECM-associated growth factors produced by the fibroblasts, this is not definitive.

The failure mode of the Gen 1 valves that were functional at 28 and 52 weeks appeared to be root growth between the commissures leading to separation of the leaflets. We hypothesize that the increasing regurgitation occurring over many months led to increasingly abnormal RVOT hemodynamics and, eventually, a maladaptive growth of the valve root. This failure mode may be moot for children, who grow at a much slower rate than sheep, with a pulmonary artery increasing from a diameter of 12 mm at birth to a diameter of 24 mm at age 16 years or 12 mm change in diameter over 16 years (24). This is in sharp contrast to the much faster growth of the sheep pulmonary artery, 4 mm in diameter over 8 months (10, 19), a rate consistent with the valve diameter results in this study.

We nonetheless conceived the Gen 2 design, placing a sleeve comprising the same biologically engineered matrix tube around the tri-tube valve, as a possible means to slow the growth rate of the valve diameter relative to the leaflets. The sleeve also provides mechanical support to the commissures and a layer of safety to possible failure of suture lines. For example, the doubling of initial root thickness might present an increased mechanical constraint to growth and increase the time for complete recellularization to occur in the root or both. The sleeve delayed the time to complete recellularization of the root, which was necessarily accommodating increasing valve diameter in the acellular region by some combination of stretching, plastic deformation, and/or protease-mediated remodeling of the implanted matrix. Although the mechanism cannot be concluded, we achieved our objective of reducing the commissure separation observed in the Gen 1 valves and preserved good valve function—and stable valve diameter—through the target 52-week implant duration in two of three animals implanted with the Gen 2 valve. The third animal in the cohort that worsened from mild to moderate regurgitation between 44 and 52 weeks is the animal that exhibited the earliest regurgitation increase (from trivial to trivial-mild at 20 weeks), consistent with progressive regurgitation, increased cardiac output, and supraphysiological increase in diameter of the valve and distal pulmonary artery that occurred in the Gen 1 valves. That root growth occurred in both the Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves is supported by the increase in collagen density of the valve roots that increased in diameter without a change of thickness, consistent with our reported results for a pulmonary artery replacement using a single tube in this growing lamb model (10). A gap developed between the leaflets at one commissure in all three explanted Gen 2 valves. This was the commissure where the suture line of the sleeve overlapped with the axial suture line of the tri-tube valve forming the commissure. This suggests that a more robust growth response was induced by the degrading suture, which is easily remedied by relocating the suture line for the sleeve between commissures.

The Gen 2 valve leaflets were comparably recellularized to the extent observed in the long-term (36 and 62 weeks) explanted Gen 1 valves based on histology and DNA quantitation. However, the combination of somatic growth of the root and growth accommodation by the leaflets due to excess coaptation area at implantation and reported increased free-edge length (presumably by the same combination stated above) yielded a valve that retained function during growth of the lamb into adulthood, with the potential for eventual complete leaflet recellularization. This potential exists because of the remarkable durability of the biologically engineered collagenous matrix comprising the slowly recellularizing leaflets.

The recellularization pattern of the leaflets was similar in the both the Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves, consistent with migration from the root primarily on the leaflet surfaces, with migration from the base through the interstitial matrix apparently being inhibited. In this respect, our findings are consistent with study based on implantation of decellularized pulmonary and aortic valve allografts similarly implanted into the ovine pulmonary artery of lambs (25), although the resulting histology differs substantially. Our findings also appear consistent with our prior tube-in-tube valve design study that used younger lambs (5.5 weeks old), where the entire leaflet surface appeared to be covered by cells after 12-week implantation. The one severely shortened leaflet in the 62-week explanted valve appeared to have a focal collective invasion of SMA-positive cells. There was no evidence of this leaflet being shortened in the 52-week echocardiography, as this leaflet could be visualized on the basis of reference suture clips and exhibited high mobility during the cardiac cycle; the valve had only mild-to-moderate insufficiency at 52 weeks. However, this same leaflet exhibited no mobility when visualized at 62 weeks, when the valve insufficiency was moderate to severe. Whether this invasion was occurring but not yet manifested as leaflet contraction at 52 weeks or began when the leaflet lost mobility at some point after 52 weeks is unknown.

All three 52-week explanted Gen 2 valves maintained their preimplantation leaflet height. The slight reduction in height of leaflets in the long-term (36 and 62 weeks) explanted Gen 1 valves relative to the preimplantation value can be attributed, at least in part, to the circumferential stretching of the leaflets that occurred when the valve diameter increased beyond the expected value of 24 to 25 mm. This is supported by the maintenance of leaflet height in the two Gen 2 valves that maintained the expected valve diameter. It is notable that a common failure mode of tissue-engineered heart valves (TEHVs)—leaflet thickening—was never observed, with all leaflets having similar thickness compared to preimplantation values. The maintenance of tensile stiffness and strength of the implanted matrix whether it became highly recellularized (root) or modestly recellularized (leaflet) even after 52- to 62-week implantation is a favorable outcome, indicating the potential stability of the matrix over longer periods to time that are apparently required for complete leaflet recellularization.

There are several limitations to this study and its findings. Although the functional improvement of the tri-tube design over the tube-in-tube design is well supported from the Kaplan-Meier survival curves, the cohort sizes for the Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves were too small to conclude a difference between them on this basis. The difference based on reduced insufficiency index at explantation is promising, however. Potential outcome variability due to biological heterogeneity in the recipient population would only be assessable in larger cohorts. Complete leaflet recellularization with cell types and phenotypes consistent with homeostasis after regeneration of the implanted matrix will be required for longer-term durability of the valve, and this remains to be shown in a cohort with a longer end point. Heparin was administered twice daily for the duration in this study to obviate clotting as a failure mode while ascertaining valve growth potential; the time when anticoagulation can be discontinued with minimal risk, either because of spontaneous endothelium formation and/or passivation of any exposed matrix, remains to be determined. Regulatory approval will also require production of the Gen 2 valve according to current Good Manufacturing Practice. Expanded in vitro hydrodynamic characterization of the Gen 2 valve would also be required for its regulatory approval. Although there are ISO standards for testing of pediatric heart valves (ISO 5840-2015 Part 1, Annex E) it remains to be seen whether they are applied without modification to TEHVs that include living cells at implantation or, as in this approach, are designed for recellularization and growth after implantation. Hydrodynamic testing that is based on short-term function (e.g., EOA and regurgitant fraction over a range of flow rates) should apply to TEHV, and only a single flow rate was investigated using n = 2 Gen 1 valves in this study.

Note that durability testing according to ISO standards, which is justified by the need to demonstrate an inert valve that cannot self-repair in adults or grow with children will function for a clinically relevant period (200M cycles for biologic valves and 600M cycles for mechanical valves), does not clearly apply, as demonstrated by no case of suture line failure for any of the tri-tube valves (n = 7), up to 62 weeks in vivo (38M cycles at the 72 bpm average for sheep) as compared to 9.2M to 13.5M cycles in the durability test. The shorter durability life in vitro likely reflects nonsterile conditions and resultant matrix damage (absent a sterile durability test system and despite use of antimicrobial agents); however, it also reflects the impossibility of an in vitro test reflecting the durability associated with tissue growth along the suture lines that occurs in vivo. Durability testing demonstrated that the suture lines are sufficiently durable during the initial implantation period before matrix recellularization and tissue growth along the suture lines, which occurred as early as 12 weeks (8.8M cycles at 72 bpm) in the tube-in-tube valve (7).

The tri-tube design that we used in both the Gen 1 and Gen 2 valve designs, where three tubes are sutured together in a closed ring and the bottom of each tube is then sutured closed to form its own leaflet, yields a wide range of possible leaflet sizes/coaptation areas and leaflet angulations for a target valve diameter because three tubes that have a diameter within a wide range can be sewn together into a valve of target diameter with this design (e.g., tubes of smaller diameter yielding less coaptation area and vice versa). This leads to an interesting design optimization problem that dictates initial valve performance in a reasonably predictive manner given measurable constitutive mechanical properties of the collagenous matrix. It also determines long-term growth of the valve through as-yet poorly understood cell-mediated regenerative processes that can depend on the initial and evolving valve stress and strain/flexion distributions (26).

METHODS

Study design

The study was designed to ascertain whether the new tri-tube design (“group 1”) resulted in longer time of freedom from valve failure (“survival time”) than the previous tube-in-tube design. In vitro characterization and testing were first performed to ensure valve function at the time of implantation and durability until expected recellularization. These in vitro methods are all found in Materials and Methods of the Supplementary Materials and include: durability testing, pulse duplicator testing, tensile strain-to-failure testing, suture pullout force testing, tissue/matrix composition analysis, histology, proteomics, and computational model of valve diastolic forces (with stress strain curves and model geometry shown in figs. S6 and S7, respectively).

The statistical design of the implantation study, conducted under approval by the Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC), is as follows: The median survival time for our tube-in-tube study in the lamb model was 14 weeks (n = 8, “group 0”) (7). A sample size (n) for group 1 was determined from a proportional-hazards regression model (27) using the University of California, San Francisco Clinical and Translational Science Institute web-based software. For a planned average survival time of 52 weeks, a hazard ratio (group 1/group 0) value = 0.125, α (two-tailed) value = 0.05, censoring rate (censor/time) value = 0, and n = 6 is required for power = 0.8 (i.e., probability of failure, β = 0.2). The modification of the tri-tube valve during the study by adding the sleeve to the Gen 1 valve to create the Gen 2 valve precluded a powered design for their survival comparison.

Tissue tube production

Tubular, cell-seeded fibrin gels were fabricated by mixing aqueous solutions of ODFs (Coriell), bovine fibrinogen (Sigma-Aldrich), thrombin (Sigma-Aldrich), and calcium chloride (Sigma-Aldrich). The final component concentrations were as follows: 1 million ODFs/ml, fibrinogen (4 mg/ml), thrombin (0.38 U/ml), and 5.0 mM calcium chloride. The mixed solution was injected into tubular glass molds, which had a 16-mm-ID mandrel, a 4-mm annulus, and were 15 cm in total length.

After gelation, the tubular fibrin gels on glass mandrels were cultured in Dulbecco's modified Eagle medium + 10% fetal bovine serum (Hyclone), penicillin (100 U/ml) (Gibco), streptomycin (100 µg/ml) (Gibco), amphotericin B (0.25 µg/ml) (Gibco), insulin (2 µg/ml) (Sigma-Aldrich), and ascorbic acid (50 µg/ml) (Sigma-Aldrich). Culture medium was changed three times per week for 2 weeks while allowing the longitudinal shortening of the gels. The tissue tubes were then matured on the mandrel for 7 weeks.

After maturation, the biologically engineered tubes were decellularized by immersion in 1% SDS (Sigma-Aldrich) and 1% Triton X-100 (Sigma-Aldrich) for 6 hours and 30 min, respectively, at room temperature with continuous shaking. The tubes were then extensively rinsed in 1× phosphate-buffered saline before and after overnight incubation in culture medium plus deoxyribonuclease (2 U/ml) (Worthington Biochemical).

Valve fabrication

Valves of 19 mm diameter were fabricated using three of the 16-mm-diameter decellularized biologically engineered tubes. Each tube was cut to a length of 3 cm and further trimmed to define a leaflet while providing for an outflow tract (Fig. 1A). The tubes were sutured together into a closed ring using degradable sutures (Covidien Maxon CV) with three suture lines spanning the length along their point of contact (white dashed lines in Fig. 1A). The bottom of each tube was sutured together to create a leaflet (yellow dashed lines Fig. 1A) with 11 mm height, yielding a tri-leaflet valve (Fig. 1B). A sleeved tri-tube valve (Gen 2) was created by opening an additional tube longitudinally and suturing it around the tri-tube valve shown in Fig. 1B. This suture line coincided with one of the axial suture lines of the tri-tube valve in all three implanted Gen 2 valves. Six punctures were first made in the sleeve of Gen 2 using a 22-gauge needle to mitigate the possible accumulation of exudate between the sleeve and tri-tube valve after implantation.

Valve implantation procedure

Implantations were performed at the University of Minnesota Experimental Surgical Services (ESS). The fabricated Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves were implanted as pulmonary valve replacements in n = 7 Dorset lambs (average age = 15.5 ± 3.1 weeks and average weight = 29.5 ± 2.9 kg). All protocols were approved by the IACUC. Briefly, animals were anesthetized using ketamine (10 mg/kg) and propofol (2 to 6 mg/kg) and maintained on 2 to 4% isoflurane for the duration of the procedure. The heart was exposed via a left lateral thoracotomy with dissection through the intercostal space. Native leaflets of the pulmonary valve were removed, and a segment of the pulmonary artery was resected and replaced with the tri-tube valve and sutured into place using 5-0 running degradable (Maxon) suture. The distal end was first sutured using running suture followed by the proximal end. Before closing the anastomosis, the tri-tube valve was allowed to fill with blood to displace air. Once off bypass, epicardial echocardiography was performed with images acquired. After surgery, animals received subcutaneous injections of 1500 IU of heparin twice daily for the duration of the study. Valve function was measured with transthoracic echocardiography under manual restraint at 1 week, 1 month, and then bimonthly after implantation (whenever regurgitation became moderate). End-on (short-axis) ultrasound images were used to measure the free-edge length of the leaflets at 1 and 52 weeks, with percentage increase in the length reported. The same protocol was followed for a 20-mm bovine valve (Contegra 200 valved conduit, expired and thus available for research use) implanted into a lamb aged 15 weeks weighing 29 kg.

Porcine valves (Hancock 150 valved conduits) of 22 mm diameter were implanted into n = 6 juvenile sheep (age, <6 months; weight, 48 to 54 kg) at ESS under IACUC approval. The surgical procedure was similar except that the RVOT extraction method was used for implantation, where the pulmonary artery is excised from the myocardium and not incorporated into the proximal anastomosis of the test valve, which is trimmed into a “cobra-head” shape to place it on the myocardial surface as close as possible to the native position of the pulmonary valve (14). A full-thickness pledgeted polypropylene suture was placed into the most distal end of the right ventriculotomy, and the conduit was sewn to the right ventricle in a single continuous running stitch. After surgery, animals received subcutaneous injections of 2000 IU of heparin twice daily for 3 days. Valve function was measured with transthoracic echocardiography under manual restraint at days 14 and 30 and then 30-day intervals until euthanasia at day 140.

Statistical analysis

Post hoc analysis of Kaplan-Meier survival curves of valve function (freedom from moderate regurgitation) for group 0 (tube-in-tube) and group 1 (tri-tube) designs using the log-rank (Mantel-Cox) test was performed using GraphPad Prism with an assumed hazard ratio (group 1/group 0) of 0.125. The same survival curve analysis was performed for the Gen 1 and Gen 2 valves as well as a t test based on the measured insufficiency index at explantation as another comparative measure of valve performance. Tensile properties were evaluated using one-way analysis of variance (ANOVA) with Tukey post hoc test for differences at P < 0.05. Paired symbols are used to show difference between groups. Explanted valve leaflet and root tissue were evaluated for trends over the implant duration (i.e., a linear regression slope based on preimplant and explanted values not different from zero at P < 0.05) to evaluate any trend in thickness and length of leaflet and root tissues, tensile properties, cellularity, and collagen or total protein concentrations. Absent any temporal trend, measurements from the four explanted valves in Gen 1 were averaged. Tissue anisotropy was measured using t test at P < 0.05. One-way ANOVA with Tukey post hoc test was used to evaluate difference in explanted valve properties compared to the preimplant tubes and native pulmonary valves. Calcium measurements for Gen 2 leaflets were compared with the reported lower limit for explanted bovine valve leaflets [dry tissue (1.11 µg/mg) (17)] using a one-sample t test. Similarly, the outflow root tissues of Gen 1 and Gen 2 were compared with the reported lower limit for explanted bovine valve roots [dry tissue (1.11 µg/mg) (17)]. Any usage of “difference” implies a statistically significant difference at P < 0.05. Proteomic differences between functional classifications were evaluated by two-way ANOVA with Bonferroni with no correction for multiple comparisons.

Evaluating engineered valves

Particularly for pediatric patients, replacement heart valves that can grow with the patient would substantially reduce surgical burden. Syedain et al. designed acellular conduits for pulmonary valve replacement by suturing together three tubes of cell-derived extracellular matrix. The valves maintained their function for 52 weeks when implanted in the pulmonary arteries of lambs, and adding an additional tubular sleeve around the tri-tube valve improved the valve design and reduced regurgitation at explantation. Valves became recellularized and increased in diameter over time while showing reduced calcification compared to clinically used bioprosthetic valves, supporting the potential utility of these conduits in pediatric populations.

Materials and Methods

Durability testing:

Valves were mounted on a custom molded 19-mm ID silicone tube and placed in Bose Durapulse System. Valves were tested at 10 Hz frequency with end diastolic pressure of 10 mmHg (to mimic pulmonary diastolic pressure gradients) for at least 95% of the cycles, in accordance with ISO 5840 part 1/AnnexE, Table E-7 for pediatric right-side valves. The test fluid was comprised of phosphate buffer saline with antimicrobial-antimycotic (Gibco Anti-Anti) added, maintained at 37 °C, and replaced every 4-7 days. Additional, antifungal fungizone (250 µg/ml Gibco) was added on day 12. Valves were removed for visual inspection every 4-7 days. High-speed video end-on of the outflow side of the valves were periodically acquired at 240 fps using a Pixel 4 camera.

Pulse duplicator testing:

A customized pulse duplicator system previously described (28) was used. It consists of a commercial wave generator and pump (ViVitro Systems), reservoir, valve mounting chamber, variable compliance chamber, and mechanical bi-leaflet valve to ensure one-directional fluid movement. The system has pressure transducers (ViVitro Systems) immediately upstream and downstream of the valve to allow for accurate pressure measurements. Additionally, there is an electromagnetic flow meter (Carolina Medical, 500 series flow-meter) upstream of the valve to measure the flow rate in both directions. A custom Labview program is used to record flowrates and pressures. For testing, the valve is sewn into two custom silicone sleeves that are then mounted in a custom chamber that allows for applying a transmural pressure gradient, which was set to 16±1 mmHg and generating cyclic root distention (28). The pulse duplicator loop is run at room temperature with phosphate buffered saline as the test fluid. Each valve is tested at a prescribed flow rate (set by pump stroke volume and frequency) with measurement of pressure proximal and distal to the valve. An average flow rate of 4.0 liters per minute (LPM) was used to mimic pediatric cardiac output (29). Pressure is controlled by changing the down-stream flow resistance, stroke volume, and up-stream hydraulic pressure head. During valve testing, end-on camera (Canon EOS T3i) images are obtained at 30 fps for video capture. At least 10 cycles were recorded during continuous testing and analyzed for EOA, valvular pressure drop, and regurgitation fraction (closing volume plus leakage volume).

Tensile strain-to-failure testing:

Strips with dimensions ~2 mm x ~10 mm were cut from the biologically engineered tube, Contegra valve, ovine pulmonary valve, and explanted tri-tube valve as specified in the circumferential and axial directions. Each strip was tested in tensile strain-to failure as previously described (9, 28). Sample dimensions are measured prior to testing using a digital caliper. The strips are mounted in custom grips attached to the actuator arms of an Instron tensile testing system (Instron Systems) and straightened with a 0.05 N tensile load. Strain is calculated by taking the natural logarithm of the sample's deformed length over its initial length. Stress is defined as the force divided by the undeformed, cross-sectional area of the strip. Modulus and ultimate tensile strength (UTS) are taken as the slope of the linear region of the stress-strain curve and the maximum stress recorded, respectively, as shown for representative stress-strain curves in fig. S6

Suture pull-out force testing:

As previously described (11), suture pull-out force was measured by placing a loop of 7-0 prolene suture 2 mm from the edge of a strip that is cut 1x1cm. One end of the loop is clamped to a stationary arm and the other end is connected to the actuator arm of an Instron tensile testing system (Instron Systems). The loop is pulled to failure at a rate of 50 mm/min based on ISO 7198 for vascular grafts.

Tissue/Matrix composition analysis:

The collagen mass content was quantified using a hydroxyproline assay previously described (30) assuming 7.46 mg of collagen per 1 mg of hydroxyproline. The total protein content was measured using the ninhydrin assay (31). The tissue volume calculated using the measured length, width, and thickness of the samples (in units of ml) was then used to calculate the sample mass concentration (mg protein/ml tissue) of collagen and total protein. The DNA content was quantified with a modified Hoechst assay for total DNA (32) using 7.6 pg of DNA per cell (33). For calcium content, lyophilized tissue was hydrolyzed in 6 M HCl for 20 hrs at 110°C, the hydrolysate volume measured and 0.8 volumes of 6 M NaOH added. Samples were then used in a colorimetric Calcium Detection Assay Kit (abcam ab102505) according to the manufacturer’s protocol.

Histology:

Preimplant tube and explanted tritube valve tissue samples were fixed in cold 4% paraformaldehyde, infiltrated with 30% sucrose and 5% DMSO, embedded in O.C.T. Compound (Tissue-Tek) and frozen in liquid N2. Sections 9 µm thick were adhered to slides and stained using standard procedures for Lillie’s Trichrome and Von Kossa. Contegra valve explant tissue was fixed in 10% formalin followed by paraffin embedding, sectioning, and staining with Mason’s trichrome and Von Kossa. Immunostaining was performed on tissue pre-blocked with 5% normal donkey serum in PBS. Primary antibodies (VWF, abcam ab6994, 1:200; SMA, Sigma A-5338, 1:200; Ki67, abcam ab15580, 1:100) were diluted into fresh blocking buffer for overnight incubation. Detection was with species matched Alexa Fluor 647 (Jackson Immunoresearch). Nuclei were stained with Hoechst 33342 (Invitrogen H3570). Images were acquired with a Leica DMi8 widefield microscope equipped with Leica DFC 7000T camera and Leica Application Suite X software.

Proteomics:

Approximately 3 mgs of tissue samples were weighed and chemically digested with 1 M hydroxylamine at a concentration of 5 mg/mL for 3 hr as described previously (34). Equal volumes of each sample representing ~25 µg of protein was then combined with 500 fmols of 13C6 labeled QconCAT standards and proteolytically digested using filter aided sample preparation protocol (35). Resultant peptides were analyzed by Liquid Chromatography – Selected Reaction Monitoring (LC-SRM) analysis with the following modifications (36). LC-SRM analysis was performed on a QTRAP 5500 (ABSciex) in line with a Dionex Ultimate 3000 UHPLC system. Equal volumes of resultant protein digest were injected over an Acquity UPLC BEH C18 column (1.7 µm, 1 x 150 mm). Resultant spectra were manually interrogated for peak integrity in Skyline as described previously (35).

Computational model of valve diastolic forces

Finite element models of the valves were created in ANSYS Workbench: Transient Structural. To simplify, an isotropic stress-strain curve (the average of the curves for tensile testing of the tube in the axial and circumferential directions in fig. S6) was fit to the Mooney-Rivlin (hyperelastic) constitutive equation and sutures were modeled as point attachments at merged nodes (“suture points”). The models were solved for a waveform of increasing backpressure. An augmented Lagrange-based contact algorithm was utilized to prevent penetration between bodies defining the leaflets.

The tube-in-tube valve model was composed of an inner tube defining the leaflet domain (green body) and the outer tube defining the root domain (orange body) shown in fig. S7A. Axisymmetry was invoked to justify a 1/3 sub-domain. They were connected at nodes representing suture points (blue spheres) along the suture lines made in valve construction [c.f. in fig. S7B and Fig. 2 of (28)]. To constrain the degree of freedom of the model (by necessity for convergence of the numerical solution), top and bottom edges were fixed in the axial (z) and circumferential (y) directions. Consistent with axisymmetry, all nodes and the two suture points on the left and right edges and all nodes on the axial midline of the domain were constrained circumferentially. Two suture points that at the middle of model domain were constrained in the y direction and free to move in radial (x) and z directions to simulate the movement of suture points during valve diastole. All other suture points were free to move in all directions. The valve model was discretized by 24715 10-node tetrahedral elements.

The model of the tri-tube valve also invoked axisymmetry to justify a 1/3 sub-domain, defined by one-half of two adjacent tubes (cyan and green bodies in fig. S7C). They were connected via nodes defining the suture points along the axial suture line made in valve construction (fig. S7C). Leaflets were created by merging nodes so as to emulate the suture points created when suturing the bottom of each tube closed for valve construction (fig. S7D). Top and bottom edges of the valve were again fixed in the z and y directions, and all nodes and the two suture points on the left and right edges were constrained circumferentially. Consistent with axisymmetry, suture points along the mid-line of the domain were constrained in the y direction. There was no constrains for all other suture points. The valve model was discretized by 23093 10-node tetrahedral elements
