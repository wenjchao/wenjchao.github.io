最佳版本vote 10藍色發光的鈣鈦礦 LED 一直做不好，根本原因在於要讓晶體又小又乾淨幾乎不可能：作者把一種能在鈣鈦礦結晶過程中『邊長邊變成塑膠網』的小分子（OEGA）丟進前驅液裡，讓塑膠網一邊鎖住每顆鈣鈦礦小晶體不讓它長大、一邊用許多氧原子穩穩抓住鉛離子幫助晶格慢慢排整齊，最後做出 11 奈米的高品質晶體，並把藍光 LED 的外部量子效率推到 21.8%、操作壽命拉長到 69.4 分鐘。

藍色鈣鈦礦 LED 要做得亮，發光晶體必須同時做到兩件事。一是晶格內部要排得很整齊，因為晶體裡如果有位置長歪了，電子被激發後常會卡在這些歪掉的位置，把能量直接釋放成熱，而不是變成光。二是晶體本身要夠小，這樣電子和電洞被擠在一起，更容易撞在一起發光。問題在於，要把鈣鈦礦在基板上直接結晶又排得整齊，往往需要讓晶體慢慢長大，結果就一定不會小；想壓著它不長大，內部又來不及排整齊。這個矛盾正是長期以來藍光鈣鈦礦 LED (blue PeLED) 落後綠光、紅光版本的根本原因。

作者的解法是把一段中等長度的小分子（名為 OEGA）丟進鈣鈦礦的前驅液裡。這段小分子有兩個重點構造：一頭有個會跟同類連在一起的小掛鉤（乙烯基），整條身上又掛著許多氧原子。當鈣鈦礦正在結晶的同時，這些小掛鉤會在另一個扮演火種角色的分子（自由基引發劑，AIBN）引發下彼此勾住，把一個個 OEGA 連成一張塑膠網；身上的氧原子則同時穩穩抓住鈣鈦礦裡的鉛離子。這張塑膠網像許多小軟格子，每顆鈣鈦礦只能長到自己那格的大小，再長就被擋住，整片膜因此都是十幾奈米的小晶體。氧原子抓住鉛離子的功能是替剛聚在一起的鬆散鈣鈦礦小團塊踩煞車，給它們時間把原子一顆顆喬到該在的位置，再開始長大。那為什麼非要在結晶的同時讓 OEGA 連網、不能事先做好？作者刻意測試過幾個對照：直接丟一段已經做好的長鏈塑膠進前驅液，因為它跟鈣鈦礦結合得很弱、形狀又不變，根本擋不住晶體長大；用比 OEGA 短很多的小分子，也排不成連續的網。只有 OEGA 在鈣鈦礦長大的當下同步連成塑膠網，才能緊貼著正在長大的晶體建立軟格子。

這套同步策略帶來的第一個改變是尺寸。沒加 OEGA 時，鈣鈦礦顆粒平均 270 奈米；加了 OEGA 後縮到只剩 11 奈米，整整小了 25 倍。更有意思的是，晶體內部的鉛溴八面體（晶體裡最基本的小積木）排列方式也跟著改變：從原本稍微歪斜的『斜方排列』轉成方正對齊的『立方排列』。理論計算告訴我們，方正排列的小晶體表面比較『平和』，因此當晶體變小、表面占比變高時，方正排列反而更穩。對發光來說這件事非常關鍵：方正排列的小積木只會輕微抖動，不會把電子的能量偷走變成熱；而斜方排列的小積木本來就歪斜，容易把電子的能量吸走。原本被熱浪費掉的那一部分能量，現在留下來變成光。

為了直接看清楚這套『邊結晶邊塑膠化』的機制是不是真的成立，作者用一根帶有奈米針尖的玻璃管把前驅液直接放進電子顯微鏡裡觀察。沒加 OEGA 時，鈣鈦礦在電子束一打到的瞬間就聚在針尖長成大團；加了 OEGA 與火種分子後，超過十分鐘都看不到任何結晶，等到溶劑慢慢被抽乾、濃度被推上去之後才同時冒出許多 10 奈米左右的小晶體，且不再繼續長大。這直接拍下了『先把鈣鈦礦壓住，再讓它一次冒出一堆固定大小的小晶體』這件事。另一方面，質子核磁共振（一種分子身分證掃描）追蹤 OEGA 那個會連起來的小掛鉤，發現加熱完成後這些掛鉤的訊號完全消失，證實它們真的全部彼此勾住變成了塑膠網。

作者再用三組互補的量測證明發光真的變有效率。第一，用一束光打在樣品上，量被吸收的光裡有多少又變回光（這個比例稱為光致發光量子產率 PLQY），衝到 83%。第二，量電子激發後平均過多久才放出光，原本只能撐 7.5 奈秒，加了 OEGA 後拉長到 15.2 奈秒；再多加一層名為 PEA 的小分子作為接縫鈍化劑，又拉到 23.6 奈秒。時間變長代表電子比較不會在半路被缺陷拉去消耗掉，等於從動態角度也看到損失變少。第三，再從不同溫度下光的訊號變化，算出晶格振盪偷走電子能量的程度大幅降低。值得一提的是，PEA 只負責塞在晶體與晶體之間的接縫補洞，並不會跑進晶體內部破壞 OEGA 已經修好的結構，因為 OEGA 抓晶體表面抓得比 PEA 緊很多，PEA 擠不進去。

把這套晶體真正做成 LED 後，在 491 奈米的藍光下，沒處理過的元件每送進 100 顆電子大約只射出 11 顆光子（外部量子效率 EQE 10.7%）；用了 OEGA 加 PEA 之後，這個比例幾乎翻倍到 21.8%，屬於藍光鈣鈦礦 LED 裡最高的一群。最亮亮度也從 720 提升到 1,925（每平方公尺燭光）。元件能持續發光到亮度掉一半所需的時間，則從 10.3 分鐘拉長到 69.4 分鐘。壽命變長的關鍵其實還是同一張塑膠網。鈣鈦礦 LED 退化的主因之一是內部的離子被電壓推著到處跑、最後把元件破壞掉；作者量到原本只要 0.27 電子伏特就能讓離子開始移動，加了 OEGA 後變成要 0.77 電子伏特。那張塑膠網不只擋住晶體長大，也擋住離子搬家。為了不是只看到一顆運氣好的元件，作者另外做了 32 個元件，平均效率也從 9.0% 提升到 19.5%。

這套方法不挑配方：作者把同樣策略換到不同的藍光組成（從深藍到天藍）、換到另一個主流光伏材料（FAPbI3）、換到不同的下層基板，甚至搬到完全不同的『把材料先做成膠體再塗上去』的合成路線，都能得到比原來更小、更整齊的晶體。當然，即使壽命相對於原版本拉長了將近 7 倍，這個藍光 LED 持續發亮的時間還只是分鐘等級，離綠光、紅光鈣鈦礦 LED 已經達到的數百小時還很遠；效率 21.8% 雖然是藍光裡的頂尖一群，但離綠光、紅光突破 30% 的水準仍有距離。

藍光鈣鈦礦 LED 要好，發光顆粒得同時做到「很小」與「結晶很整齊」這兩件互相打架的事。作者把一種可以自己連在一起的小分子（OEGA）丟進溶液，讓它一邊抓住鈣鈦礦原料慢慢長晶，一邊串成一張高分子網把顆粒鎖在十幾奈米。最後做出的藍光 LED 達到 21.8% 外部量子效率（EQE），是這類藍光裝置目前的頂尖水準。

藍光鈣鈦礦發光二極體（blue PeLED）要做得好，發光層裡的小晶體得同時做到兩件互相打架的事。一是『夠小』：當鈣鈦礦顆粒小到十幾奈米，被光打上去的電子與電洞會被擠在一起，比較容易碰在一起發光，發光效率才高。二是『結晶夠整齊』：晶格裡的原子排得越整齊，內部破洞越少，能量就比較不會在破洞裡變成熱白白浪費掉。麻煩的是，傳統作法為了把結晶弄整齊，會故意讓晶體慢慢長大，結果顆粒往往一路長到上千奈米；反過來，硬把顆粒留在十幾奈米，表面又會破破爛爛、晶格亂排，發光反而更差。更麻煩的還在後頭：這層發光晶體必須直接在元件其他材料層上長出來——這叫『原位成長』——不能先在燒杯裡長好再貼上去，否則貼合處會留下接縫，電流跑不過去。

作者的關鍵招式是把一種叫 OEGA 的可聚合小分子，連同一個叫 AIBN 的『點火劑』，一起丟進鈣鈦礦原料溶液。OEGA 同時扮演兩個角色。第一個角色是『戴手套抓鉛』：它身上掛了一整排氧原子，每個氧都帶著一對閒置電子，可以塞給溶液裡缺電子的鉛離子；一條 OEGA 上有好幾個氧同時抓鉛，這個多點抓力比原本溶劑（DMSO）的抓力還強，於是鉛被 OEGA 接走，沒辦法亂衝去結塊，得乖乖、慢慢地排進晶格。第二個角色是『織網把晶體關起來』：OEGA 末端還留著一個可以彼此串連的反應點，加熱時 AIBN 受熱裂開、放出活潑的化學碎片，這些碎片就像火種，去戳 OEGA 末端的反應點，被戳到的 OEGA 又變成下一個火種去戳下一顆，於是 OEGA 一顆接一顆串連起來，在鈣鈦礦長晶的同時，原地織出一張高分子網，把每一顆晶體實際關進小籠子裡，讓它再也長不大，最後停在約 11 奈米。

為什麼非要用『中等大小』的可聚合分子？作者也比較了其他選項：太小的（P-MOA）身上能抓鉛的氧太少，框不住晶體；太大的（MPEG-MAA）自己先纏成一團，跟不上鈣鈦礦結晶的節奏；OEGA 剛好大小適中，串網速度與抓鉛能力都到位。

為了直接看到這個過程，作者用一台特殊的電子顯微鏡，把鈣鈦礦溶液裝進極細的玻璃管，邊照邊錄。沒加 OEGA 的對照組溶液一被電子束照到，立刻就長出失控的大塊團聚；加了 OEGA 和 AIBN 的溶液在同樣條件下十分鐘都還是清澈的，什麼都沒長；接著像噴撒一樣突然冒出一整批約 10 奈米的小晶體，而且之後就停止生長。對照組裡，如果只加不會串連的小分子（N-MOA），晶體確實長得慢一點，但最後還是會繼續長大，框不住大小；如果直接加事先做好的長鏈高分子（PAA），則幾乎沒有影響。這直接證明 OEGA 兼具『讓鈣鈦礦慢慢長』與『把大小鎖死』兩個動作，而且這兩個動作必須在原地同時發生才有效。慢慢長的意義在於：鈣鈦礦原料若太快湊在一起，會把彼此卡在錯位置；給它時間，每顆原子才能慢慢調到正確位置，最後結晶因此既小又整齊。

顆粒一旦縮到約 11 奈米，會額外帶來一個意外的好處：晶體裡的原子排列從原本歪歪扭扭的低溫結構，整顆翻成原本只有在高溫才會穩定的『方正排列』（cubic 相）。原因是顆粒越小，表面相對於整顆體積的比例就越大，表面的影響力會主導整顆晶體的排列方式。在這種方正排列裡，原子比較不會劇烈左右搖晃；這件事為什麼重要？因為當電子被光打上去後，本來應該乖乖跳回來放出光，但若晶格一直在抖，電子會把能量交給這些抖動變成熱，光就少了——這個現象叫『電子–聲子耦合』，它越弱代表越少能量被偷走變成熱、越多能變回光。OEGA 顆粒的晶格震動因此明顯比對照組弱，能量不再流失，結果光致發光量子產率（PLQY，每吸收 100 顆光子能發出幾顆光子）一路衝到 83%。

把這套鈣鈦礦做成完整的藍光 LED 之前，作者再加一道叫 PEABr 的小分子做表面補土，去填晶體之間的縫隙。妙的是，OEGA 對晶體表面抓得比 PEABr 強很多，所以 PEABr 只能乖乖待在縫隙、不會擠進晶體內部把結構搞亂。最佳的藍光 LED 元件在 491 奈米發光，外部量子效率（EQE，每打進元件的一個電子能變成多少個射出元件的光子）從對照組的 10.7% 提升到 21.8%，是目前藍光鈣鈦礦 LED 最頂尖的表現之一；最高亮度從約 720 提升到 1,925 cd/m²；工作壽命（亮度衰退到一半所需時間）從 10.3 分鐘拉長到 69.4 分鐘。32 顆元件統計平均 EQE 也從 9.0% 提升到 19.5%，代表這不是僥倖的單顆冠軍。穩定性會跟著大幅改善，是因為鈣鈦礦在運作時內部離子會被電壓推著跑、破壞晶格；作者測量讓離子跑得起來所需的能量（活化能），對照組是 0.27 電子伏特，加了 OEGA 後變成 0.77 電子伏特，等於離子被高分子網牢牢卡住、動不了，元件因此比較不會壞。

先把這篇真正的貢獻框住：它不是追求單一最高數字，而是首次用『原位聚合』這個新概念，把『顆粒小』與『結晶整齊』這個老矛盾一次解開。接著幾個重要邊界要說清楚。雖然 69 分鐘的工作壽命比對照組好六倍，但距離商用顯示器需要的數千小時還非常遠；EQE 21.8% 雖屬頂尖，同期也已有 23–26% 的競爭結果。元件級的驗證只做在 491 奈米的這個藍光鈣鈦礦系統；作者雖然展示同樣策略可延伸到其他組成、甚至應用在太陽能用的 FAPbI3，但這些延伸都只做到『顆粒層級』看 TEM 照片，沒有再回頭做完整元件，所以這個策略在其他系統上能不能跑出同樣好的元件，這個實驗回答不了。

藍光鈣鈦礦 LED 一直卡在兩個互斥需求：晶體要長得夠規整才不浪費電能、卻又要長得夠小才能高效發光。作者把一種能在加熱時自己連成網的小分子（OEGA）混進前驅液，讓它一邊抓住鉛、一邊聚合成網包住正在長大的晶粒。結果晶粒從超過 250 nm 縮到 11 nm、又因前期慢慢長而排列更整齊，做出 21.8% 外部量子效率、491 nm 波長的藍光 LED，是同類元件的世界級水準。

藍光鈣鈦礦 LED 一直卡在一個結構性矛盾。鈣鈦礦是一種會發光的離子晶體。要它把吃進去的電轉成光而不是變成熱，就得把它排得整齊，免得電子撞到缺陷把能量浪費掉；但同時又得把晶粒長得很小，帶負電的電子和帶正電的電洞才會被擠在同一個小房間裡碰面、放出光子。麻煩在於，過去要把晶體排整齊，幾乎都得讓它慢慢長大，結果一定變成大顆粒。

作者選了一支叫 OEGA 的小分子混進塗膜用的前驅液。這支分子身上有兩種功能。尾端的雙鍵在加熱並加上一支叫 AIBN 的化學引爆劑後，會跟其他 OEGA 互相連起來，在薄膜裡織成一張看不見的網。身上其他地方則有許多含氧的小爪子，會一個個抓住溶液裡的鉛離子。同一支分子先把鉛黏住、讓晶體種子比較晚才冒出來；加熱時它又當場織成網，把已經成形的小晶粒鎖在格子裡長不大。為什麼非得選『進去時是小分子、加熱時才當場連起來』的這種？因為如果只放短分子，它抓得到鉛卻沒辦法圍成網，晶體照樣長到比原本還大、平均約 500 nm；如果直接放現成的長鏈高分子，它在液體裡會自己捲成毛球，跟鈣鈦礦黏不太住，只能把晶粒壓到約 200 nm。兩端都失敗，問題出在時機。中等長度的 OEGA 剛好兩件事同步：聚合速度配合得上鈣鈦礦結晶的速度，爪子又夠多，最後把晶粒乖乖縮到 11 nm。

為了證明這張網真的有織起來，作者用核磁共振追蹤 OEGA 身上雙鍵的指紋訊號，發現加熱後這個訊號整個消失，代表 OEGA 確實彼此連起來了。電子顯微鏡則直接拍到對照：沒加 OEGA 時晶粒平均 270 nm，加了之後變 11 nm。他們還設計了兩種即時觀察。一是邊塗膜邊量光譜：沒加 OEGA 的版本一接觸反溶劑就馬上長大發光；加了 OEGA 的版本則明顯拖了一下才開始發光，強度也漸漸變強，代表晶體確實晚一步、且長得更整齊。二是直接在液體裡用電子顯微鏡拍前驅液：沒加添加劑的版本一下就長出一大團；加了 OEGA 的版本盯了 10 分鐘都還沒長出任何顆粒，等到把電子束加強、模擬旋塗時溶劑揮發的瞬間，才一次冒出一堆穩定的約 10 nm 小晶粒。

晶粒一旦被縮到 11 nm，連晶體內部的堆法都會跟著變。鈣鈦礦這種離子晶體可以想成一格一格的小盒子，盒子堆成方正立方體叫 \(\alpha\) 相、歪斜地擠成菱形叫 \(\gamma\) 相。大顆粒時，內部的堆法說了算，歪斜版比較省能量；顆粒一變小、表面占的比例變大，方正的立方體版本反而最穩。差別在哪？歪斜版裡，每一格鉛–溴小盒子都被擠得有點扁。電子在晶體內傳遞時容易碰到正在扭動的盒壁，把能量交給盒壁變成熱，本來該發出來的光就變少。方正版沒有這種扭曲，電子比較少被晃到，發光效率自然高。實驗上，他們從低溫量到室溫，發現 OEGA 樣品的發光寬度比原版穩定許多，這正是電子比較少被晶格振動干擾的訊號。直接量發光也對得起來：強度幾乎是原版的兩倍，壽命從 7.5 奈秒拉長到 15.2 奈秒；再加上一層叫 PEABr 的表面鈍化劑後拉到 23.6 奈秒。發出來的光子佔被激發激子的比例（也就是發光效率指標 PLQY）達到 83%。

他們把這層 OEGA 處理過的薄膜疊上電子和電洞傳輸層，做成完整的 LED。發出來的是波長 491 奈米的藍光：每打進 100 個電子就能轉出將近 22 個光子（也就是 21.8% 的外部量子效率 EQE），是這個藍光色域已知最高的水準之一。亮度從原版的 720 cd/m² 跳到 1,925 cd/m²；亮度衰減到一半所花的時間也從 10.3 分鐘拉長到 69.4 分鐘。作者進一步追查穩定度為什麼跟著變好，發現薄膜裡的離子要在電壓下『偷溜』所需的能量從 0.27 eV 漲到 0.77 eV——換句話說，那張聚合物網順手把會破壞元件的離子遷移也卡住了。

這個方法雖然刷新藍光鈣鈦礦 LED 的紀錄，但跟商用 OLED 或量子點 LED 相比仍差一大截：最高亮度只到約 2000 cd/m²、亮到一半的時間也只有約一小時，離真正的螢幕或照明還很遠。理論計算原本預測『晶粒得小到 2 奈米才會變方正』，實驗卻是 10 奈米就方正，作者只能用『真實條件下晶粒表面比較鬆動』和『加進去拉藍光的 EA 成分也卡進晶格幫忙』兩個原因來補釋，這個落差暫時還是靠模型補。不過同一招套到不同顏色（混了氯的更短波藍光）、不同成分比例、甚至完全不同用途的鈣鈦礦（用在太陽能電池的 FAPbI3），都能看到一樣的小顆粒方正晶體效果；連在燒杯裡先合成、再灑到基板上的傳統膠體合成法，加了 OEGA 也能讓顆粒更乾淨。

技術及材料

主題一：可聚合單體前驅液配方與 in situ 自由基聚合反應
透過將具有合適骨架長度與多配位位點的乙烯單體（OEGA）與自由基引發劑 AIBN 直接拌入鈣鈦礦前驅液，在旋塗、退火過程中就地形成 POEGA 聚合物網路，動態約束鈣鈦礦奈米晶生長並穩定其表面。

作者把整套化學反應「全部塞進同一張基板、同一次退火」。基礎前驅液先在 DMSO 裡溶入 CsBr (0.1 M)、EABr (0.044 M)、\(PbBr_2 (0.11 M)\) 攪過夜，對應鈣鈦礦組成 \(Cs_{0.7}EA_{0.3}PbBr_3\)。接著把可聚合單體 OEGA、共配體 PEABr (0.03 M) 與化學引爆劑 AIBN 一起拌進這鍋液體。OEGA 全名 oligo(ethylene glycol) methyl ether acrylate，可以拆成兩段看：一頭是丙烯酸酯雙鍵 C=C，當作日後串連的「掛鉤」；另一頭拖著一條短聚乙二醇鏈，每兩個碳之間夾一個氧 (–\(OCH_2CH_2O\)–)，整條側鏈有好幾個氧，每個氧都伸出一對閒置電子去抓鉛離子。分子量 \(M_n = 480\) 對應約 10 個重複單元，正好落在「不太長也不太短」的甜蜜點。OEGA 的加入量以「OEGA 重量／\(PbBr_2\) 重量」做掃描，從 0、0.2、0.5、1、2、4 wt% 中找到最佳值 1 wt%；AIBN 固定為 OEGA 重量的 1%，因為火種只要少少幾顆就能引發接力反應。整鍋拌好後旋塗成膜，送進 60 °C 烤箱退火 20 分鐘——這個溫度同時做兩件事。

60 °C 同時觸發兩件事的關鍵在 AIBN 的「裂解溫度門檻」剛好落在 60–65 °C：分子中間的 N=N 鍵一加熱就斷開、把中間的 \(N_2\) 釋出當氣體，兩端留下帶單電子的活潑碎片（自由基）。這些碎片像火種一樣去戳 OEGA 末端的雙鍵掛鉤；被戳到的 OEGA 自己也變成下一個火種，再去找下一個 OEGA 接力連起來。為什麼非要這套「邊長晶邊聚合」？鈣鈦礦結晶有一個非常短的時間窗——從反溶劑滴下、溶劑揮發到晶體長出來只有幾秒到幾十秒。OEGA 在結晶前已經抓住鉛延緩成核，當晶體真的開始長出來時，自由基聚合也同步啟動，把已經貼著晶體表面的 OEGA 就地連成網，網眼當場形成、剛好把晶體鎖死在約 11 奈米。配位與物理侷限兩個動作在同一個時空發生，缺一不可。

OEGA 為什麼能搶贏 DMSO 抓鉛？單一氧的結合能 (-0.67 至 -0.74 eV) 其實比 DMSO 單顆抓鉛 (-1.07 eV) 還弱，但 OEGA 是一條長鏈，鏈上多個氧能同時伸過去抓住同一顆鉛，多齒配位的平均強度拉到 -1.51 eV/位點（螯合效應）。更關鍵的是溶劑置換時會釋出好幾顆 DMSO，這些被解放的 DMSO 在溶液裡自由飛行，整體熵增，置換的 Gibbs 自由能 ΔG = -0.66 eV——OEGA 因此能擠進 \(PbBr_2\) 的第一配位殼層。這也解釋了為什麼分子長度與加入比例都有「甜蜜點」：可聚合單體階梯實驗中，最短的 \(P-MOA (M_n = 120)\) 身上只有一兩個氧、螯合力不足，晶粒落在 52±16 奈米；\(M_n = 480\) 的 OEGA 增加到約 10 個氧，晶粒縮到 11±3 奈米；再長到 \(MPEG-MAA (M_n = 5,000)\)，分子先捲成團、跟結晶速度脫節，出現尺寸不均。加入比例同理：0.2 wt% 網眼太鬆鎖不住晶體；4 wt% 又把鈣鈦礦稀釋成塑膠基質裡的散落小島，干擾後續電子電洞注入。1 wt% 是「網眼夠密 + 鈣鈦礦體積仍主導」的折衷。

這套策略的因果鏈藏在三組對照裡。第一組拔掉 AIBN，只放 OEGA：OEGA 雖然仍然抓得住鉛延緩成核，但晶體一旦冒出來，OEGA 沒被點火、彼此沒串連，形不成網眼。LP-TEM 直接看到「一群小於 100 奈米、一群大於 300 奈米」的雙峰分佈，少數運氣好被 OEGA 圍住的晶體還小，運氣不好的晶體繼續長到上百奈米。第二組換成「只會配位、絕對不聚合」的短鏈 N-MOA 與 DMM：晶粒反而長到 514±120 與 504±125 奈米，比沒加任何添加劑還大——少量短鏈分子吸附在晶體表面後，反而變成讓晶體合併聚集的「橋」。第三組換成「已經做好的長鏈聚合物」MPEG \((M_n=5,000)\) 與 PAA \((M_n=40,000)\)：它們一進液體就捲成毛球、跟鈣鈦礦黏不牢，只能鬆鬆地擠壓晶體，晶粒只縮到 202±44 奈米且整片膜出現非晶聚集。三組失敗都指向同一結論：成品聚合物或單純配體都做不到「就地、緊貼晶體、即時形成網眼」。

工具與材料
  • OEGA \((oligo(ethylene glycol) methyl ether acrylate, M_n = 480)\)：兼具丙烯酸酯雙鍵與多氧醚側鏈的可聚合單體；雙鍵負責串連、氧負責抓鉛。
  • AIBN (偶氮二異丁腈)：化學引爆劑（自由基引發劑）；60–65 °C 受熱後 N=N 鍵斷開、釋出 \(N_2\) 並留下兩個自由基去點燃 OEGA 串連反應，加入量為 OEGA 重量的 1%。
  • POEGA：OEGA 在退火當下自由基聚合形成的軟質聚合物網路，網眼把鈣鈦礦奈米晶鎖死在約 11 奈米。
  • PEABr (phenethylammonium bromide)：共配體，0.03 M 一起拌進前驅液；不參與聚合，後期負責晶界表面鈍化。
  • DMSO (dimethyl sulfoxide)：前驅液主溶劑；OEGA 多齒配位 + 熵增驅動下被擠出 Pb 的第一配位殼層。
  • \(Cs_{0.7}EA_{0.3}PbBr_3\)：靶向鈣鈦礦組成；A 位混合 Cs 與乙胺鎓 (EA) 把純藍光波長推到約 491 nm。
  • \(OEGA/PbBr_2 = 1\) wt%：經 0/0.2/0.5/1/2/4 wt% 掃描確認的最佳比例，網眼密度與鈣鈦礦體積比的折衷點。
  • N-MOA / DMM 對照：「能配位但不聚合」的短鏈分子；晶粒反而長到 ~500 奈米，證明只配位不夠。
  • MPEG / PAA 對照：「已經做好的長鏈聚合物」；在液體裡先捲成毛球、跟鈣鈦礦黏不牢，只能弱壓晶到 ~200 奈米。

主題二：鈣鈦礦薄膜旋塗與退火（反溶劑一步法）
使用標準一步反溶劑旋塗法在 ITO/m-PEDOT:PSS 上沉積 Cs0.7EA0.3PbBr3 薄膜，並利用 60 °C 低溫退火同步啟動 AIBN 熱分解、引發 OEGA 自由基聚合，達成「鈣鈦礦結晶」與「聚合物網路形成」的動力學同步。

整套薄膜工藝在 N2 手套箱裡進行。最底層是透明電極 ITO，作者先用洗滌劑、去離子水、丙酮、IPA 依序超聲清洗，再 UV-臭氧處理 20 min 把表面殘渣氧化掉、順便讓 ITO 變得親水。接著鋪上一層改質版 PEDOT:PSS（m-PEDOT:PSS）當電洞傳輸層：把商業 PEDOT:PSS 雙倍稀釋後與含 PSS-Na 與 KBr 的水溶液 1:1 混合，旋塗後 150 °C 退火 15 min 烘乾。鈣鈦礦薄膜本身由『一步反溶劑法』完成——把前驅液倒在 m-PEDOT:PSS 上、3,500 rpm 高速旋塗，旋塗開始 36 秒時快速滴下 450 µl 反溶劑（甲苯 + 0.2 mg/ml PMMA）；PMMA 用來幫助鈣鈦礦把孔洞堵掉、薄膜更緻密。隨後在 60 °C 退火 20 min 同時完成結晶與聚合，最後用 CF4 電漿表面處理 3 秒封口。完整 PeLED 結構由下到上是 ITO / m-PEDOT:PSS / 鈣鈦礦 (20 nm) / TPBi (40 nm) / LiF (1 nm) / Al (100 nm)——後三層用真空熱蒸鍍完成，因為旋塗會用溶劑、會把鈣鈦礦溶掉。

為什麼反溶劑要在 36 秒這個時機滴下、又為什麼整套退火只能用 60 °C？關鍵在『時序對齊』。甲苯本身跟 DMSO 互溶但不溶解鈣鈦礦鹽，作者一滴下，甲苯與 DMSO 立刻混在一起，原本鎖在 DMSO 裡的 CsBr、PbBr2、EABr 突然發現周圍變成『不能溶我』的環境，過飽和度瞬間衝高、整鍋一起成核 (nucleation)。這提供了大量奈米尺度種子，後面退火只負責讓種子排整齊、不再大幅成長。退火溫度選 60 °C 是因為它正好踩在兩個反應曲線的交點：AIBN 從這個溫度開始大量釋出自由基、鈣鈦礦也才剛要慢慢排整齊。兩者必須同步發生才能讓 OEGA 一邊串網、鈣鈦礦一邊長晶，網孔當場套住正在長大的晶簇、長到 11 nm 就被網擋住停止。這就是『動力學同步 (kinetic synchronization)』——這套工藝設計能讓 OEGA + AIBN 策略真正成立的根本原因。

兩個看似次要的配方細節其實對效率影響很大。原版 PEDOT:PSS 的功函數（電子躍離材料所需的最低能量）約 5.0 eV，而 Cs0.7EA0.3PbBr3 的價帶頂在 5.8 eV，兩者差 0.8 eV、電洞要翻一道高牆才能注入。作者加入 PSS-Na 與 KBr 改質後，功函數被推高到接近價帶頂、電洞翻牆容易；附帶好處是表面潤濕性改善，鈣鈦礦前驅液不會收縮成水珠、薄膜更平整。反溶劑這邊也有調味——純甲苯抽乾 DMSO 的速度快但留下零星孔洞，元件電流容易從這些洞短路。多加 0.2 mg/ml PMMA 形成薄模板層，幫助鈣鈦礦把孔洞堵掉、薄膜更緻密。這兩個小改造都是為了把後面 OEGA 帶出的高 PLQY 真的轉換到元件效率。

三件事任何一件出錯，整套策略就崩盤。第一，環境必須是 N2 手套箱：空氣中的濕度會跟 PbBr2 反應形成水合物破壞晶體；空氣中的氧分子本身是雙自由基 (·O–O·)，會搶先把 AIBN 釋出的活潑碎片吃掉、終止 OEGA 聚合鏈。第二，退火溫度不能貪快改到 100 °C：鈣鈦礦在 80 °C 以上幾秒內就衝完結晶，OEGA 串網需要 20 min 才完成，鈣鈦礦會比 OEGA 早結束、晶粒長到微米級才被網包起來——這時網只能包大塊、無法縮小。第三，反溶劑必須等旋塗 36 秒後才滴：太早滴的話前驅液層還很厚，甲苯只把表層 DMSO 洗掉、底層 DMSO 還在，上層先成核、下層後成核，薄膜出現雲紋與顆粒大小分佈不均。

工具與材料
  • 一步反溶劑法 (one-step anti-solvent approach)：旋塗中途滴入與 DMSO 互溶但不溶解鈣鈦礦的甲苯，瞬間把鹽從溶液擠出來成核。
  • 反溶劑 (anti-solvent, 甲苯 + 0.2 mg/ml PMMA)：旋塗 36 秒時滴入 450 µl，PMMA 同時幫助薄膜緻密化。
  • m-PEDOT:PSS：改質版 PEDOT:PSS 電洞傳輸層，將商業液雙倍稀釋後與 PSS-Na + KBr 水溶液 1:1 混合，調高功函數以匹配 Cs0.7EA0.3PbBr3 價帶頂。
  • UV-臭氧處理 (UV-ozone treatment)：ITO 清洗後紫外+臭氧氧化表面殘渣，提升親水性，作 20 min。
  • 60 °C 低溫退火：踩在 AIBN 熱分解與鈣鈦礦慢結晶兩條曲線交點，使兩個反應動力學同步。
  • 動力學同步 (kinetic synchronization)：鈣鈦礦結晶與 OEGA 聚合在同一退火步驟同時進行，是策略成立的根本。
  • CF4 電漿處理 (CF4 plasma)：退火後 3 秒表面處理，加一層氟封口。
  • TPBi/LiF/Al：電子傳輸層 (40 nm) / 功函數修飾層 (1 nm) / 金屬背電極 (100 nm)，全部以真空熱蒸鍍完成。
  • N2 手套箱：把水氣與氧氣降到 ppm 以下；前者保護 PbBr2 不水解，後者保護自由基不被氧消耗。

主題三：高解析 TEM、HRTEM 與電子能量損失譜 (EELS) 結構表徵
直接以原子級解析度確認 OEGA 處理後鈣鈦礦由正交相 (Pnma) 轉變為立方相 (Pm-3m)，並透過 EELS 元素剖面驗證單體確實在奈米晶表面形成薄包覆層。

TEM 樣品必須夠薄（< 100 nm）讓電子能穿透，又要保留薄膜真實的結晶狀態。作者用兩種製樣方式互相印證：第一種把鈣鈦礦前驅液直接旋塗在碳膜銅網（一面附碳膜的金屬網片，可當 TEM 樣品台）上、按薄膜製程退火；第二種先在真正的 ITO/傳輸層基板上旋塗、退火完成，再用甲苯把鈣鈦礦刮下重新分散到銅網。HRTEM 用像差校正 TEM (Cs-corrected TEM) JEOL Grand ARM 300 (300 kV)——這台機器配備能修正電子透鏡固有像差的元件，把解析度推到 0.1–0.2 nm，能直接看到單一原子柱排成的點陣。判讀晶相靠『快速傅立葉變換衍射圖 (FFT)』：把點陣影像丟進 FFT，原本連續的點陣會變成一組亮點，亮點間距反映晶面間距、亮點對稱性反映晶體對稱類型。觀察角度叫『帶軸 (zone axis, ZA)』，用三個整數標示（如 [223]）。粒徑統計用 Nano Measurer 軟體：每顆奈米晶量邊長、每樣本 ≥ 100 顆、分散在多區域多樣本上採樣、排除重疊顆粒，最後以平均值 ± 標準差報告。

鈣鈦礦是離子晶體，對電子劑量非常敏感——一般 TEM 強束流會把鹵離子撞鬆、表面非晶化，FFT 看到的會是『非晶玻璃』而不是立方相，作者的相變宣稱就會被質疑成『電子束燒出來的假象』。EELS 也會被驅趕的鹵離子擾亂、無法分辨表面包覆。所以作者特別用『Gatan K2 ultra-low-dose 相機』——這台相機把每個電子訊號數位化，極微弱的訊號就能成像；因此電子劑量比常規拍低一個數量級，樣品幾乎不被破壞，HRTEM 看到的真的是原始晶相。低劑量 + Gatan K2 不只是『更精細』，是『讓相變主張站得住腳的必要條件』。

怎麼從 HRTEM 影像判斷立方相還是正交相？正交相（晶格軸長不等）的 FFT 斑點分佈不對稱、立方相（三軸等長且互相垂直）則呈現正方形對稱。但單一帶軸不夠：從特定角度看立方相與某些四方相 (tetragonal) 的 FFT 會幾乎一樣，所以作者沿多個帶軸拍 FFT。正交相的 pristine 樣品沿 [223] 與 [332] 兩個帶軸都拍到正交相特有的低對稱斑點；OEGA 樣品則沿多個帶軸都拍到高對稱的正方形斑點，才能排除四方相、確認真的是立方相。

光看相變還不夠——作者還要直接證明 OEGA 真的形成『表面薄包覆層』。電子穿過樣品時會把部分能量交給樣品原子的內層電子，每種元素有自己的『門檻能量』（O-K edge ~532 eV、Cs-L edge ~726 eV）。作者把高角環狀暗場掃描 TEM (HAADF-STEM) 的電子束沿奈米晶剖一條線、逐點記錄能量損失，就是電子能量損失譜 (EELS)。挑兩個關鍵元素互補：O 是 OEGA 獨有（鈣鈦礦只有 Cs/Pb/Br），所以 O 訊號出現的地方就是 OEGA 所在；Cs 是鈣鈦礦的 A 位陽離子、標誌晶粒本體。把兩條剖線疊在一起，氧訊號剛好分佈在 Cs 訊號的兩側（即奈米晶邊緣），就直接證明 OEGA 包覆在奈米晶表面，而非進入體相。

作者用兩個額外設計確保結論可信。第一，TEM 製樣做兩組：一組直接旋塗在碳膜銅網、一組從真實 ITO/傳輸層刮下重分散。兩組都看到 11 nm 立方相，就排除『碳膜表面化學特殊性影響結晶』的質疑，確認真實元件中的奈米晶就是這個尺寸與相。第二，HRTEM 必須用像差校正 TEM——電子透鏡有固有的球面像差，邊緣電子聚焦點跟中心不同，導致原子點陣被『暈成一團』。Cs-corrector 由多重磁多極元件主動產生反向像差抵消、相當於把眼鏡度數校正，這樣立方相 vs 正交相的對稱差才能量得準。

主題四：液相原位 TEM (LP-TEM) 觀察結晶動力學
在液態溶液中即時觀察前驅液的成核/生長動力學，視覺化「無添加 vs. OEGA+AIBN vs. 單獨 OEGA vs. N-MOA vs. PAA」如何改變成核時序，以證明 in situ 聚合（而非單純配位）是尺寸控制的關鍵。

最佳版本vote 1

LP-TEM（Liquid-Phase TEM）是『邊長邊錄』的活體 TEM——把液態反應溶液裝進兩面是電子可穿透薄窗的小腔室，電子束穿過窗看溶液裡正在發生的事。商業 LP-TEM holder 多用氮化矽 (SiN) 薄膜窗，封口繁複；作者沿用 Zhang et al. (PNAS 2024) 開發的『奈米吸管液態池 (nanopipette liquid cell)』：用一根石英毛細管（外徑 1.0 mm、內徑 0.7 mm、長 10 cm），放到 CO2 雷射拉伸器 (P-2000) 把中段熔化、再快速拉開——熔化的玻璃像太妃糖被兩端拉長、變成一根頭尖只有幾百奈米的奈米針。兩線拉伸參數分工：第一線 (HEAT 835, FIL 5, VEL 30, DEL 128, PUL 90) 收細、第二線 (HEAT 865, FIL 4, VEL 25, DEL 132, PUL 195) 拉到亞微米尖端，避免一次過拉導致尖端閉合或斷裂。把前驅液注入毛細管、靜置 3 h 讓液體擴散到奈米尖端，剪下含液 0.5–1 mm 段，用真空油脂封住兩端組裝在碳膜 TEM 網上。成像用 JEOL JEM-2100 Plus (200 kV) 配 Gatan OneviewIS 相機；電子劑量率有兩段：標準 5 e-/Å²·s 用於追蹤慢慢長晶的靜態觀察，當要模擬旋塗時 DMSO 快速被甲苯帶走的瞬間就提到 12 e-/Å²·s——電子打到 DMSO 上的能量足以引發局部蒸發與輻射分解，過飽和度被拉高。

為什麼非要做 LP-TEM 而不只是傳統 TEM？傳統的 XRD、SEM、TEM 都是『拍照表徵』，只能看樣品最終狀態。如果 OEGA 樣品最後出現 11 nm 立方相、pristine 出現 270 nm 正交相，傳統表徵可以告訴你『差異』，但說不清『差異是怎麼發展出來的』。LP-TEM 把溶液保留液態下連續成像，能直接拍到第幾秒第一顆晶體冒出來、長多大就停。作者觀察到：pristine 一接觸電子束就立刻長出失控大團；OEGA + AIBN 系統超過 10 min 完全看不到結晶；提高電子劑量模擬溶劑萃取後才一次冒出大量 10 nm 小晶粒並停住。這個『成核延遲 (nucleation delay)』正是聚合物網路提供動態空間侷限的直接證據——其他表徵看不到的時序差異。

作者要證明的命題是『in situ 聚合（而不只是配位）是把晶粒鎖在 11 nm 的關鍵』。為了拆開兩個機制，他設計了五組 LP-TEM 對照像邏輯閘的真值表：(1) 無添加→自然成核大團；(2) OEGA + AIBN（完整策略）→成核延遲 10 min、10 nm 後停止；(3) 單獨 OEGA（無 AIBN）→雙峰分佈，<100 nm 小顆粒來自電子束偶然打出的零星自由基『偷』聚一小段、>300 nm 大顆粒則是 OEGA 維持單體、慢慢長過頭——直接證明聚合反應本身才是關鍵；(4) N-MOA（與 OEGA 骨架極相似但缺乏可聚合乙烯基，代表『有配位、無聚合』）→延緩長晶但最終仍長到 514 nm；(5) PAA（預先聚合好的高分子，代表『有聚合、無有效配位』）→對長晶幾乎沒影響。五組合在一起說明：聚合與配位兩個機制必須『同時就位、且在當下發生』才有效。

LP-TEM 一定會面對的質疑是：高電子劑量會不會把溶劑分解、把元素驅趕，看到的『結晶』只是電子束燒出來的人造產物？作者用兩個檢查回應。第一，在 12 e-/Å²·s 高劑量下用 EDS（能量散射 X 射線譜）量元素比，得到 Cs:Pb:Br = 37.4:23.6:39.0，接近化學計量比，排除電子束把元素重分配的可能。第二，FFT 分析非晶聚集體確認其為非晶中間態（amorphous intermediate）、不是電子束生成的鈣鈦礦人造產物。這兩個檢查讓 LP-TEM 觀察的『真實成核延遲與結晶停滯』站得住腳——電子劑量是『時序加速器』而不是『結晶創造者』。

主題五：1H NMR、EPR、XPS 對聚合與配位的化學驗證
從化學鍵層級驗證 (a) OEGA 在退火後確實完成自由基聚合（乙烯質子消失）；(b) 反應後無殘留自由基；(c) OEGA 透過 O–Pb 配位降低 Pb2+ 電荷密度。

最佳版本vote 1

三套化學探針分別逼近三個機制問題。質子核磁共振 (1H NMR, Bruker 400 MHz) 的原理是：氫原子核在強磁場下會以特定頻率共振，每種化學鍵環境裡的氫都有自己的『化學位移』（chemical shift），就像身分證號碼。乙烯基末端的氫（H 接在 C=C 雙鍵上）位移在 5.9–6.4 ppm 這個獨特區間；OEGA 一聚合，C=C 變飽和 C–C，這些氫就跑到 1–3 ppm 區，原本的乙烯峰會消失。樣品準備是把鈣鈦礦薄膜旋塗退火後刮下、重溶於氘代溶劑（DMSO-d6 或 CDCl3，氫被換成氘避免溶劑訊號蓋掉樣品），且 OEGA 濃度刻意放大提升訊噪比。電子順磁共振 (EPR) 是 NMR 的姐妹——只偵測『單一未成對電子』也就是自由基。為了量 AIBN 點火後產生的丙烯酸酯自由基中間態（壽命微秒級），作者用『自旋捕獲劑』DMPO (5,5-dimethyl-1-pyrroline N-oxide) 把短壽命自由基轉成壽命較長的氮氧加合物。實驗條件是 OEGA + DMPO 溶於 DMSO，加熱到 335 K，做 5/10/20 min 時間序列。

EPR 結果有點反直覺：三個時間點都沒量到 DMPO 加合物訊號。乍看可能是『反應沒發生』，但這跟 NMR 結果（乙烯訊號消失）矛盾。正確解釋是丙烯酸酯自由基反應太快——根據 Barth et al. (Macromol. Rapid Commun. 2009) 文獻，這類自由基壽命落在微秒級，AIBN 一吐出自由基就立刻撞上下一個 OEGA、繼續鏈成長，根本沒時間跟 DMPO 反應。335 K (62 °C) 比 60 °C 退火稍高，確保 AIBN 完全進入熱分解區；做 5/10/20 min 三個時間點覆蓋反應不同階段，如果自由基壽命有秒到分鐘級應該至少被捕獲一次。三個時間點都無訊號反過來坐實『微秒級壽命』假設，也排除『AIBN 沒分解』或『DMPO 失效』兩個其他可能。對元件而言，『最終無殘留自由基』是好消息：殘留自由基會繼續攻擊鄰近分子、引發長期降解。

XPS (X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy) 用 Kratos Axis Ultra \((Al K\alpha, 150 W)\) 進行：X 射線打到樣品表面，把內層電子整顆打出來（光電效應），量這些電子的動能反推它們的『束縛能 (binding energy, BE)』。把 Pb2+ 想成『缺電子』的空盒子，OEGA 的氧把孤對電子塞給它後，Pb 周圍的電子密度增加；XPS 量的內層電子相對被原子核的拉力減弱，BE 向低能方向偏移。實驗結果：Pb 4f5/2 由 pristine 138.1 eV、Pb 4f7/2 143.0 eV 在 OEGA 處理後都往低 BE 偏移；Br 3d 也同樣偏移，反映晶格整體電子密度提升、Pb–Br 鍵離子性減弱。位移幅度雖小（0.1–0.3 eV）但可重現、且與 DFT 算出的 O–Pb 配位能定性吻合。鈣鈦礦表面不導電容易累積正電（charging effect）把整個譜往高 BE 偏移幾百 meV，所以作者把表面吸附碳氫殘留物的 C 1s 峰（真實 BE 已知為 284.8 eV）當『內標』校正——否則帶電造成的整體位移會被誤判成配位偽訊號。

為什麼一個聚合反應要做三個技術？三者各自有盲區，必須拼起來才能涵蓋『機制完整鏈條』。NMR 只看終點：乙烯訊號消失等於 OEGA 已聚合，但不知過程中有沒有奇怪中間產物。EPR 看自由基中間態：但結果是『沒訊號』本身不足以證明反應發生——必須搭配 NMR 證明聚合確實發生，才能把『EPR 無訊號』反過來解讀為『反應太快、最終無殘留』。XPS 在完全另一條軸上——它證明 OEGA 跟 Pb2+ 之間有電子轉移、有配位鍵，但無法告訴你聚合有沒有完成。三者拼起來，才能同時回答：(a) 聚合完成 (NMR)；(b) 無殘留自由基 (EPR)；(c) 配位機制成立 (XPS)，剛好對應主線 (a)(b)(c) 三個機制宣稱。

  • C 1s 內標 (284.8 eV)：校正樣品表面帶電與儀器漂移的標準動作。

  • UPS (Ultraviolet Photoelectron Spectroscopy, He I)：同儀器以紫外光激發，分析 OEGA 處理前後功函數與能帶對齊。

主題六：原位 UV–Vis 吸收與 PL 演化、低頻 Raman、溫變 PL
在結晶/退火過程中即時量測光學訊號演化，捕捉成核延遲、晶簇緩慢生長與晶格弛豫；溫變 PL 與低頻 Raman 用以萃取電子-聲子耦合與相穩定性指標。

作者用兩台儀器同步追蹤結晶過程的光學訊號。原位 UV–Vis 用 Hitachi UH4150 漫反射 UV–Vis 光譜儀加 2 mm 縫的石英流通半微量比色皿——把含 OEGA 的前驅液注入比色皿、邊照不同波長的光、邊在儀器外側加熱模擬退火條件。每隔一段時間掃一張吸收譜：當鈣鈦礦剛開始形成激子吸收峰（藍光鈣鈦礦的激子吸收峰在約 490 nm 附近），峰位與強度演化就會被連續記錄。對照組（無 OEGA）的激子峰在反溶劑滴下幾秒內冒出；OEGA 組則延遲且峰位開始時偏短波長、隨時間紅移到 490 nm，反映晶體從超小簇慢慢長到 11 nm。同步的原位 PL 用 Edinburgh FLS980 配 345 nm 紫外雷射激發——這個波長能量遠高於鈣鈦礦的吸收邊，確保所有樣品都吃得到能量。穩態 PL 看持續打光時放出來的譜長什麼樣；TRPL 把雷射切成超短脈衝，每打一發量 PL 強度隨時間怎麼衰減——衰減越慢代表電子掉回基態前在發光態待越久、非輻射損耗越少。雙指數擬合 \(I(t) = I_0 + a_1 exp\)(-t/τ\(_1) + a_2 exp(-t/\)τ_2) 把衰減拆成快慢兩個過程，加權平均得 τ\(_avg：\)原版 7.5 ns；OEGA 處理 15.2 ns；OEGA + PEA 處理 23.6 ns。

PL 譜的寬度（FWHM, full width at half maximum）反映電子在發光態與基態之間能量分佈被多少東西攪亂——溫度越高、晶格振動越強、譜寬越寬。但 FWHM(T) 同時混有：低溫殘留 Γ\(_0\)（缺陷貢獻）、線性 \(\gamma_ac T\)（聲學聲子貢獻）、玻色子項 \(\gamma_LO/(e^{E_LO/k_B T}-1)\)（縱光學聲子 LO phonon 貢獻）、以及雜質激活 \(\gamma_imp e^{-E_b/k_B T}\)。只看一個溫度拆不開四項。作者用 Oxford OptistatDN-V 液氮冷凝樣品台從 100 K 量到 325 K，每 10–25 K 取一張譜，把 FWHM(T) 整條曲線丟進獨立玻色子模型擬合。為了避免過擬，作者援引 Wright et al. (Nat. Commun. 2016) 慣例把 \(\gamma_imp\) 預設為可忽略（高品質鈣鈦礦下雜質項小），主要讓 \(\gamma_LO\) 與 \(E_LO\) 兩個物理上最重要的參數浮動。pristine 萃取出 \(\gamma_LO = 228.0\) meV、\(E_LO = 39.2\) meV；OEGA 處理萃取出 \(\gamma_LO = 115.5\) meV、\(E_LO = 28.7\) meV，耦合強度幾乎減半。為什麼這代表「非輻射損耗減少」？電子被光激發後要回基態有兩條路：放出光子（輻射）或把能量交給晶格振動變熱（非輻射）。LO phonon 是鈣鈦礦中最重要的能量竊取者，特別是 \(PbBr_6\) 八面體扭曲帶來的低頻模。\(\gamma_LO\) 從 228 降到 116 meV 對應的物理畫面是：立方相 \(PbBr_6\) 八面體比正交相站得更正、扭曲模軟化、能量竊取通道被關掉一半，所以 PLQY 衝到 83%、TRPL τ\(_avg\) 從 7.5 拉長到 15.2 ns。375 nm 雷射激發是因為這個波長比 345 nm 更接近吸收邊，避免高能激發引入的熱載流子干擾。

Raman 譜量的是「雷射打進樣品後被晶格振動偏移能量再散射回來」的訊號。鈣鈦礦的 \(PbBr_6\) 八面體扭轉、旋轉這類軟模落在很低的頻率區（&\(lt; 50 cm^-1\)），緊貼著入射雷射本身的頻率位置。問題是 99.99% 以上的入射光是不偏移能量的瑞利散射（直接彈回來），這個訊號比真正的 Raman 訊號強好幾個量級，把低頻 Raman 全部蓋住。體積布拉格光柵 (VBG) 是一種把特定波長精準擋掉、其他波長通過的厚透射光柵——線寬窄到 \(5 cm^-1\)，剛好把雷射波長挖一個極窄的凹陷，瑞利散射被擋掉。作者用 Newport N-LHP-925 633 nm He–Ne 雷射，入射端串接 VBG 帶通濾光 \((Optigrate 5 cm^-1)\) 先把雷射自身的放大自發發射 (ASE) 尾部濾乾淨，再用反射 VBG + 三組凹陷 VBG（光密度 > 3）三段串聯去除瑞利散射。為什麼 Raman 要從 200 K 量起？聲子的平均熱激發數隨溫度上升，室溫下低頻軟模（&\(lt; 30 cm^-1\)）譜寬會被熱激發寬化到糊在一起；200 K 起聲子熱激發數降低、軟模譜寬窄到能分辨。pristine 在低溫看到清楚的軟模 doublet；OEGA 處理組整體強度低、模糊化——對應八面體已經不扭曲、軟模消失，是 \(\gamma_LO\) 減半的獨立佐證。

這套「原位 UV–Vis + 原位 PL + 低頻 Raman + 溫變 PL + 共聚焦 PL mapping」組合不多餘，而是把「結晶動力學 → 晶格結構 → 光物理」的因果鏈分三層分別量。原位 UV–Vis + 原位 PL 提供時間軸：什麼時候開始有激子吸收、什麼時候開始發光，跟 LP-TEM 看到的「延遲 10 分鐘」相互呼應。低頻 Raman 提供晶格振動證據：八面體扭曲模是不是真的軟化了？溫變 PL 把 \(\gamma_LO\) 量化：耦合強度減半多少。最後共聚焦螢光顯微 (CFM, Nikon 雷射掃描共聚焦顯微鏡) 對薄膜做 PL mapping 看「整片膜每個位置發光均不均勻」——確認 11 奈米晶體不是只在某個小角落形成、而是整片均勻。少了任何一個，論證鏈就缺一環。例如只做溫變 PL 不做 Raman，\(\gamma_LO\) 減少可能被解釋為缺陷下降而非結構變化；只做 Raman 不做溫變 PL，看到軟模減弱但沒辦法量化它對光物理的貢獻。另有飛秒瞬態吸收 (fs-TA, Ultrafast Systems HELIOS) 寬譜泵-探系統處理載流子超快動力學，補上 ns 以下的時間尺度。

作者把剛做好的 PeLED 元件不封蓋直接放進手套箱裡的整合球量測系統 (Enlitech LQE-100-EL)。整合球是一個內壁全反射的空心球，元件不管往哪個角度射光都會在球壁亂彈直到被探測器收到——這樣才能把「所有方向射出的光」一次收齊。儀器再從 2.8 V 慢慢掃到 6 V，每 0.1 V 停一下，同時量「流進元件的電流密度」、「射出的光亮度」與「光子能量分布算出的色座標 (CIE)」，畫成 J–V–L 三軸曲線。外部量子效率 (EQE) 就是「每打進元件一顆電子，最後能從元件正面射出來幾顆光子」；21.8% 在藍光鈣鈦礦 LED 已是頂尖水準。操作穩定性 (operational stability) 則是把元件持續通電發光、看亮度衰退到初始一半所花的時間 T50，本研究把初始亮度定在約 100 cd/m²（約等於一般室內照明亮度），OEGA/PEA 元件 T50 達 69.4 min、對照組僅 10.3 min。

光學量測之外，作者另開一條獨立的電學量測來追究「為什麼穩定性會變好」。他們另外做一片簡單的測試片：玻璃上旋塗一層鈣鈦礦，上面熱蒸鍍兩塊金電極，兩塊金電極之間留 50 微米的縫——這叫橫向電極組態 (lateral geometry)。橫向組態的好處是電流必須順著薄膜走 50 微米的長路徑（遠大於薄膜本身 20 奈米的厚度），這條長路徑上主要靠離子搬家來搬運電荷，量到的 \(\sigma\) 主要反映離子的活動程度。樣品放進液氮 Dewar 容器、用 Lake Shore 控溫器掃溫，Keithley 2400 在每個溫度下量電流算出橫向導電率 \(\sigma(T)\)。鈣鈦礦裡的離子要從自己的晶格位置搬到隔壁空位置，必須翻過一個能量山頭。Nernst–Einstein 公式把這件事寫成 \(\sigma(T) = (\sigma0/T) \cdot exp(-Ea/kBT)\)：把 \(ln(\sigma T)\) 對 1/T 畫成圖，斜率就是 −Ea/kB，這個 Ea 就是「離子搬家必須翻的山頭高度」。對照組 Ea 為 0.27 eV、OEGA/PEA 處理後拉高到 0.77 eV——山頭高了快三倍，正好對應 T50 從 10.3 min 延長到 69.4 min。

幾個設計細節讓這套量測可信。第一，作者堅持量未封裝元件：封裝膠會把元件跟外界水氣、氧氣隔開，量到的壽命變長有一部分是封裝的功勞、不是材料的功勞；不封裝才能把「材料本身能撐多久」跟「封裝有多嚴密」乾淨拆開。第二，鈣鈦礦裡的離子會跟著電壓重新分布、反應時間是秒等級，掃描太快會讓 J–V 出現「正掃跟反掃對不上」的滯後現象，量到的 EQE 不是真正穩態值；所以 dwell 100 ms、delay 100 ms 是給離子站定的時間，並請第三方實驗室交叉驗證。第三，Nernst–Einstein 擬合靠 \(ln(\sigma T)\) 對 1/T 的線性段斜率，溫度範圍太窄誤差大、橫跨相變點則上下段斜率不同會失真，必須畫圖檢查線性度才能下結論。第四，鈣鈦礦折射率 n 從 pristine 2.10 降到 OEGA 處理後 2.01，這 0.1 的差異決定了發光層發出來的光有多少比例能穿出 ITO、玻璃進入空氣；作者親自用橢偏儀重量這兩片膜的 n，其他層才沿用文獻值，避免 FDTD 光取出分析全錯。

主題八：DFT 分子簇結合能與配位幾何計算
透過分子層級 DFT 量化 OEGA 各種 O–Pb 配位構象（醚氧、羰氧、單點/多點配位）與 PbBr2 的結合能，建立 OEGA「冠醚樣多齒螯合」優勢的微觀證據，並與 DMSO 比較解釋為何在熱力學上 OEGA 能取代溶劑進入第一配位殼層。

最佳版本vote 1

作者把 PbBr2 一顆和 OEGA 一條（或多條）放進量子化學軟體 (ADF/AMS2023)，讓電腦用密度泛函理論 (DFT) 解這群原子在不同擺位下的電子分布與總能量。流程是：擺幾種可能的牽手姿勢（醚氧單點、羰氧單點、兩點/三點/四點齊抓），讓軟體把每種姿勢鬆弛到能量最低點，再算複合物總能量 \(E_complex；\)定義分子簇結合能 Eb \(= (E_complex - E_PbBr2 - n\cdot E_OEGA) / n\)，Eb 越負代表抓得越緊。OEGA 是柔性分子，隨手挑一個構象可能掉進高能量局部極值，所以多個起始構象都跑一遍幾何優化、取最穩定態才有代表性。技術細節上，泛函用 PBE（夠快又對配位鍵合理）+ D3-BJ 色散校正（補上 PBE 漏掉的范德華吸引），Pb 等重原子用 ZORA 把相對論效應塞進方程式；基組用三套價層加極化函數的 Slater-type orbital (TZP)，H/C/O/S 全電子算到底，Br/Pb 內層用凍結芯近似省計算量。

OEGA 之所以特別，在於它身上同時帶「強而少」的羰氧 (carbonyl O，丙烯酸酯端基，單點 −0.74 eV) 與「弱而多」的醚氧 (ether O，聚乙二醇尾巴上約 9 個，單點 −0.67 eV)。羰氧像錨點先把 OEGA 拉近 Pb，醚氧再順著鏈條像冠醚樣把 Pb 包圍起來形成多齒結合 (chelation)。一個磁鐵抓 Pb 是 −0.67 eV，四個磁鐵在同一條繩子上同時抓住同一顆 Pb（四齒配位），結合能會疊加並因幾何鎖定達到 −1.51 eV。這就是螯合效應的關鍵——多隻手由於已被同條鏈帶到 Pb 附近、不必再從溶液中游過來，抓上去的「機率代價」幾乎不用付。

光看單點 Eb 會得出錯誤結論：OEGA −0.67 eV 比 DMSO 單體 −1.07 eV 弱，照理 OEGA 抓不過 DMSO；但實驗 XPS/NMR 明明看到 OEGA 進入 Pb 第一配位殼層。化解矛盾的關鍵在 Gibbs 自由能 ΔG = 電子能 + 零點振動能 (ZPE) + 壓力體積項 + 0→298 K 熱能修正 − T·S。完整溶劑置換反應「OEGA + 4 DMSO·PbBr2 → OEGA·PbBr2 + 4 自由 DMSO」釋出 4 個原本被綁在 PbBr2 上、現在自由飄回溶液的 DMSO 分子，它們重新獲得平移轉動自由度，系統熵 S 大幅提升、−T·S 變得很負。算出來：OEGA 路徑 ΔG = −0.46 eV（自發進行），DMSO 路徑 ΔG = +0.20 eV（不自發），熵驅動的差距約 −0.66 eV。換句話說，OEGA 之所以贏，不是抓得更緊，而是「進入時釋放出更多自由分子」——這把表觀的能量劣勢翻成熵驅動的優勢。

主題九：VASP 表面結合能與相穩定性 DFT 計算
在週期性鈣鈦礦表面模型上計算 OEGA-like 配體吸附能、不同相 \((\alpha/\beta/\gamma)\)、不同表面端 (CsBr / PbBr2) 的表面能與形成能，據此預測立方相在小尺寸奈米晶下熱力學穩定（透過 surface/volume 比放大表面能差異）。

鈣鈦礦是無限延伸的晶體，必須用週期性 DFT 軟體 (VASP) 配合平面波 + 週期邊界條件描述。要算表面就在計算盒子上下加 25 Å 真空層，模擬出一個「上下兩面都裸露」的薄板 (slab model)。slab 設定上：厚度方向取 6 層八面體（中間數層恢復體相電子環境）、真空層 25 Å（隔絕上下表面影像互擾）、橫向 2×2 supercell（讓表面吸附配體不跟自己的鏡像太近）。表面能 Esurf = (Eslab − N·Ebulk − 求和 ni·µi) / (2A)：把薄板總能減掉等量原子在體相時的能量，再除以 2A 是因為薄板有上下兩個表面、每個面積為 A； 求和 ni·µi 補償化學計量不整時的能量差。化學勢 µ 必須滿足 µCsBr + µ\(PbBr2 = E_CsPbBr3\)（體相平衡）且 µCsBr \(\le E_CsBr\)、µ\(PbBr2 \le E_PbBr2\)（純物質不會自發析出），對應實驗的富 Cs 與富 Pb 兩個端點。

拿到表面能與體相能量之後，作者把它組合成奈米晶能量模型 ENC = Δbulk + Δsurf 推臨界尺寸。Δbulk 來自相 A vs. 相 B 的單位體積能量差（\(\alpha\) 內部比 \(\gamma\) 稍高），跟邊長 d 的三次方成正比；Δsurf 來自相 A vs. 相 B 的表面能差，跟邊長 d 反比（Δsurf \(= 2V_unit\cdot\) 求和 Esurf / d）。對一個邊長 d 的立方體，表面/體積比 = 6/d——粒子越小這個比例越大，「斷面原子」相對於「內部原子」的比例急速放大。當粒徑縮到某個臨界值 dcrit，立方相的「表面便宜」剛好抵掉「體相吃虧」，再小就翻轉變成立方相更穩定。這就是為什麼小晶粒會強迫亞穩的高溫相在室溫穩定下來。

問題是純 CsPbBr3 預測的臨界尺寸只有約 2 nm，跟實驗的 11 nm 立方相差了一個量級。作者用 PBEsol 泛函（固體優化版 GGA，晶格常數與相能差比 PBE 準）+ r²SCAN（算能隙更準）+ PBE+D3-BJ（算配體吸附帶 van der Waals 校正）分工處理三類觀測量，並引入兩個額外因素彌合落差：(a) EA+ 比 Cs+ 大，把原本歪斜的 \(\gamma\) 相 PbBr6 八面體「撐開」趨近方正的 \(\alpha\) 相結構，\(\alpha\)–\(\gamma\) 體相能差 Δbulk 從純 CsPbBr3 的 0.107 eV 急降到 Cs0.75EA0.25PbBr3 的 0.001 eV——幾乎一樣穩，dcrit 因此往大粒徑推；(b) OEGA-like 配體吸附進一步降低立方相表面能，提供額外動能屏障阻止 \(\alpha\) 變回 \(\gamma\)。兩個機制同時納入後，理論的 dcrit 才能跟實驗的 11 nm 對上——這正是這篇文章 multi-scale 整合的價值。

主題十：分子動力學 (GAFF) 聚合動力學模擬
在原子尺度模擬不同單體(P-MOA vs. OEGA vs. MPEG-MAA)於 DMSO 中的聚合動力學,比較反應終端密度、主鏈剛性、側鏈與前驅體配位的能力,解釋「為何 OEGA 是平衡點」。

作者想知道「在 DMSO 中,單體 P-MOA、OEGA、MPEG-MAA 各自怎麼動、怎麼聚合、能不能跟 \(PbBr_2\) 配位」,所以用分子動力學 (MD) 把每顆原子當牛頓力學的小球,從「力場」這張查表函數讀出原子之間的力,以 Δt = 0.5 fs 飛秒等級時間步迭代更新位置與速度,1 ns 後得到完整軌跡。工具用通用 AMBER 力場 (GAFF) 在 AMS2023 框架下執行——GAFF 是 AMBER 家族專為通用有機分子設計的版本,丙烯酸酯類單體都能直接套用;原子類型用 antechamber 自動掃描,搭配 MOPAC 半經驗計算決定電荷;初始構象用 PACKMOL 把單體與 400 顆 DMSO 隨機塞進盒子。傳統 MD 力場不會自動生成新化學鍵,所以「聚合動力學」的做法是預先建構不同鏈長 m 的鏈段 (P-MOA m = 3, 9, 15;OEGA m = 1, 3, 5) 代表聚合反應跑到不同階段的中間產物,分別跑 MD 觀察反應終端密度、主鏈剛性、側鏈與 Pb 配位能力三個指標。恆溫恆壓器分工:平衡相 200 ps 用 Berendsen (快速壓回目標 T = 330 K、p = 1 atm,但破壞統計分佈);生產相 1 ns 切換成 Nosé-Hoover Chain 恆溫器 + MTK 恆壓器,產生嚴格的 NPT 系綜軌跡;barostat 對 x/y/z 等比例縮放防止 cell 異常塌陷。最後用 OVITO 把軌跡可視化。

GAFF 套到 DMSO 上算出來的結果可信嗎?跑聚合動力學之前要先做「對照組驗證」:模擬 400 顆 DMSO 在 300/310/320/330 K 下平衡 200 ps,讀出每個溫度的密度,跟 Campbell 1979 的實驗值對。模擬給出 \(1.086 / 1.073 / 1.064 / 1.053 g cm^{-3}\),與實驗值吻合,代表 GAFF 對 DMSO 的描述夠準。如果跳過這步直接套到聚合動力學上,可能出現:DMSO 密度比實驗低 5%,單體擴散速度被高估、反應端碰撞機率被高估,聚合時間估偏快;或反過來,密度高得不合理,單體被擠在一起、側鏈構象採樣不充分。這時「OEGA 是平衡點」到底是物理事實還是力場誤差,根本分不清。先把這條對照組做了,後續結論才站得住。

MD 跑完三個單體,結果直接回答「為何 OEGA 是平衡點」這個問題。三個指標互相拉扯:(1) 反應終端密度——分子小、單體濃度高時,單位體積內未反應的乙烯端基多,聚合反應發生機率高、速度快;(2) 主鏈剛性——隨鏈長 m 增加,主鏈構象越受限,剛性越大,提供奈米晶尺寸的物理約束;(3) 側鏈配位能力——側鏈要能自由擺到 \(PbBr_2\) 附近、把多齒氧塞給 Pb。模擬結果:P-MOA (短) 反應端密度最高、聚合快,但側鏈只有兩個氧、太短伸不到 Pb,束縛奈米晶的能力差;MPEG-MAA (大) 主鏈過長過僵硬,反應端被埋在自交互作用的構象裡,聚合反而慢、來不及跟上鈣鈦礦結晶。OEGA m = 3 左右剛好:反應端密度夠高跟得上結晶節奏,主鏈剛性夠把晶粒鎖住,側鏈四個氧又能自由擺到 \(PbBr_2\) 配位——三軸都站在「夠用但不極端」的位置。這就是為什麼 OEGA 不是隨便選的,而是 in situ 聚合 + 多齒配位這個雙重需求下的局部最優解。

幾個關鍵設定值得拆開講為什麼這樣選。Δt = 0.5 fs:MD 用離散時間步近似連續演化,步長必須小於系統內最快振動 (C–H 鍵伸縮約 10 fs) 的 1/10,通常 1–2 fs 配合鍵約束就夠;0.5 fs 是更保守的設定,確保 Pb–O 距離、二面角這些細緻量都算準。模擬 T = 330 K (而非實驗的 333 K):330 K 是 MD 文獻常用整數檔,差 3 K 對丙烯酸酯體系的構象與動力學影響可忽略,而且 330 K 在 DMSO 密度驗證裡有實驗值對照,順便驗證力場。生產相 1 ns (而非 100 ps):聚合物鏈構象演化緩慢,100 ps 只走過初始構象附近,主鏈剛性與側鏈配位統計量會被採樣噪聲淹沒;1 ns 才讓鏈段在構象空間走得夠遠,P-MOA / OEGA / MPEG-MAA 之間的差異浮出來。

主題十一：溫變 PL 線寬擬合：獨立玻色子模型萃取 LO 聲子能量與電子-聲子耦合
用溫變 PL 之 FWHM(T) 擬合萃取 LO 聲子能 ELO 與耦合強度 \(\gamma LO\)，量化 OEGA 處理如何降低八面體扭曲所驅動的電子-聲子耦合，連結結構（正交 vs. 立方）與光物理（非輻射損耗）。

作者把鈣鈦礦樣品放進液氮低溫量測台 (Oxford OptistatDN-V)，這台設備能把樣品冷到 100 K 再慢慢升到 325 K，每升幾度就停下來。在每個溫度下用 375 nm 紫外雷射照射樣品激發鈣鈦礦發光，量測射出來的光譜——光譜在發光峰附近呈一個鐘形，用半高寬 (FWHM) 描述「胖瘦」。低溫時 PL 譜窄、高溫時變胖。為什麼挑這個溫度範圍？100 K 時 kBT ≈ 9 meV 遠小於 LO 聲子能量 ELO（~30 meV），LO 聲子占據數幾乎是零、能乾淨分離出與溫度無關的 Γ0 截距；325 K 是室溫稍上限，更高溫鈣鈦礦會開始熱降解、PL 反映的是不可逆破壞、不能 fit 模型；100→325 K 中間正好覆蓋 Bose-Einstein 函數轉折最劇烈的區段，曲率清楚，\(\gamma LO\) 跟 ELO 才能擬合得精準。

PL 寬度反映「電子在發光前能量分布有多散」。溫度升高、晶格振動越來越劇烈、電子能量被分散到不同的振動次態，PL 譜就被拉胖——這個現象就叫電子-聲子耦合。公式 Γ(T) = Γ\(0 + \gamma ac\cdot T + \gamma LO/(exp(ELO/kBT) - 1) + \gamma imp\cdot exp(-Eb/kBT)\) 四項各有來源：Γ0 是溫度無關的不均勻寬化與雜質貢獻；\(\gamma ac\cdot T\) 是聲學聲子（晶格像聲波一樣的低頻振動）的線性貢獻；\(\gamma LO/(exp(ELO/kBT)-1)\) 是這篇文章最關心的項——括號裡是 Bose-Einstein 統計（光學聲子是玻色子，占據數 n = 1/(exp(ELO/kBT)−1)），溫度越高、kBT 越接近 ELO，占據數急速增加；\(\gamma LO\) 是「每個 LO 聲子能把電子能量打散多少」。在離子型晶體如鈣鈦礦，縱光學 (LO) 聲子讓 Pb²⁺ 跟 Br⁻ 反向震盪、產生瞬時靜電場 (Fröhlich 耦合)，對電子能量的影響遠強於聲學聲子；高溫段 FWHM 幾乎完全由 \(\gamma LO\cdot n(ELO,T)\) 主導，所以拿實驗 FWHM(T) 去 fit 公式最重要的就是讀出 ELO 跟 \(\gamma LO\)。模型背後是獨立玻色子近似 (Independent Boson Model)，假設電子同時跟很多振動模互動但這些振動模彼此獨立。

實驗對比：pristine（正交相 \(\gamma\)）\(\gamma LO = 228\) meV、ELO = 39.2 meV；OEGA（立方相 \(\alpha\)）\(\gamma LO = 115\) meV、ELO = 28.7 meV——耦合強度直接砍半。物理直覺：\(\gamma\) 相的 PbBr6 八面體歪斜，Pb-Br 鍵長不對稱、鍵角偏離 90 度；LO 聲子讓 Pb 跟 Br 反向震盪時，因為原本就歪斜，瞬時偶極矩特別大、瞬時靜電場特別強、電子被拉扯得兇——\(\gamma LO\) 大。\(\alpha\) 相的 PbBr6 八面體方正對稱，LO 震盪產生的偶極矩較對稱、部分抵消，\(\gamma LO\) 較小。這個量測結果是「立方相→低八面體扭曲→低電子-聲子耦合→低非輻射損耗」整個因果鏈的光物理印記。前提是樣品結構乾淨——HRTEM、EELS 已確認 OEGA 處理後是均勻立方相、pristine 是均勻正交相，才適合直接 fit 單一模型；如果薄膜含多種相或不同尺寸晶粒，PL 譜會變多峰疊加、擬合的 \(\gamma LO\) 會被峰間距污染。同樣地，\(\gamma imp\cdot exp(-Eb/kBT)\) 項在這篇文章中被驗證「可忽略」，避免熱啟動雜質散射被錯歸給 \(\gamma LO\) 而過估電子-聲子耦合。

工具與材料
  • 光致發光 (PL)：用雷射激發樣品、樣品釋出光子，量測波長 vs. 強度即為 PL 譜。
  • 半高寬 (FWHM)：在峰高一半的地方量左右兩邊的波長差；本研究讀 FWHM 隨溫度的變化萃取電子-聲子耦合。
  • Oxford OptistatDN-V 液氮低溫量測台：把樣品冷到 100 K 再慢慢升到 325 K 的低溫量測台，本研究用 375 nm 雷射激發。
  • 縱光學聲子 (LO phonon)：晶格中陰陽離子反向震盪的高頻振動，能量遠高於聲學模；產生瞬時靜電場 (Fröhlich coupling) 主導電子-聲子耦合。
  • 電子-聲子耦合強度 \(\gamma LO\)：公式中每個 LO 聲子能把電子能量打散多少的耦合常數；pristine 228 meV → OEGA 115 meV，砍半。
  • LO 聲子能量 ELO：縱光學聲子的振動量子能量；pristine 39.2 meV → OEGA 28.7 meV，立方相 LO 聲子被軟化。
  • Bose-Einstein 占據統計：n = 1/(exp(ELO/kBT)−1)；玻色子的平均占據數，溫度越高 n 越大，公式中括號項即此。
  • 獨立玻色子模型 (Independent Boson Model)：假設電子同時跟多個獨立振動模互動的近似，可解析推導 FWHM(T) 公式 (Wright 2016, Lao 2019, Li 2021)。
  • 聲學聲子 \(\gamma ac\cdot T\) 項：晶格像聲波一樣的低頻振動，占據數對溫度近似線性；FWHM 中的線性貢獻。
  • 雜質散射 \(\gamma imp\cdot exp(-Eb/kBT)\)：熱啟動的雜質散射貢獻；本研究中驗證可忽略，避免污染 \(\gamma LO\) 萃取。
  • Fröhlich 耦合：離子晶體中 LO 聲子的瞬時靜電場拉扯電子能量的機制，是極性鈣鈦礦電子-聲子耦合的主導項。

主題十二：Nernst–Einstein 擬合溫變導電率→離子遷移活化能
從橫向溫變 \(\sigma(T)\) 擷取離子遷移活化能 Ea，量化 POEGA 聚合物網路對鹵離子 / Cs+ 遷移的物理阻擋作用，提供 PeLED operational stability 的微觀解釋。

最佳版本vote 1

鈣鈦礦是離子型晶體——Cs⁺、Pb²⁺、Br⁻ 各自待在晶格上規定的位置，但晶格不可能完全完美，總有一些位置是空的（叫做空位 vacancy）。當薄膜兩端加電壓，鹵離子 (Br⁻) 跟陽離子 (Cs⁺) 會被電場推著沿空位跳躍遷移。離子要搬家必須翻過一個能量山頭（活化能 Ea），所以速率很慢、溫度依賴強烈。作者在玻璃基板上旋塗一層鈣鈦礦薄膜，再用陰影遮罩熱蒸鍍兩塊 80 nm 金電極，兩塊金電極之間留 50 微米的縫——這就是橫向組態。三件儀器分工：液氮 Dewar 是裝液氮的真空保溫桶把樣品冷到 77 K 周圍；Lake Shore 335 控溫器精準把樣品從 77 K 一步步升到室溫；Keithley 2400 源量測單元在每個溫度點加電壓並量電流算出橫向導電率 \(\sigma\)。Nernst–Einstein 公式 \(\sigma(T) = (\sigma0/T) \cdot exp(-Ea/kBT)\) 把離子遷移拆成兩部分：指數項 exp(−Ea/kBT) 是熱激活機率（溫度越高越有機會翻過 Ea 山頭），1/T 是溫度對遷移率的修正，\(\sigma0\) 是包含載流子濃度、跳躍距離、嘗試頻率的前因子。兩邊乘 T 取對數 → \(ln(\sigma T) = ln(\sigma0) - Ea/(kBT)\)——這是一條直線方程，把實驗資料畫成 \(ln(\sigma T) vs. 1/T\) 散點圖，最小二乘法解斜率直接得到 Ea，誤差也是標準線性回歸輸出。

量測結果：對照組 Ea = 0.27 eV、OEGA/PEA 處理後 Ea = 0.77 eV，山頭高了近三倍。物理機制有兩個疊加：第一，POEGA 聚合物網路嵌在奈米晶之間的晶界處，物理上擋住離子從這顆晶粒跳到隔壁晶粒——晶界本來是離子長程遷移的高速公路，現在被聚合物網路堵死，離子不得不繞路或翻過更高的能量牆。第二，OEGA 用多個氧原子緊抓 Pb 第一配位殼層（前一節已討論），這個結合也讓空位形成濃度降低、可參與遷移的離子變少。室溫 kBT ≈ 25 meV，Ea 從 0.27 → 0.77 eV 使 exp(−Ea/kBT) 從 2×10⁻⁵ 降到 4×10⁻¹⁴——理論上離子搬家速率降低近十億倍。實際 T50 從 10.3 min 延長到 69.4 min（約 7 倍）「只是」7 倍而不是十億倍，是因為元件退化不只受離子遷移控制——還有界面化學反應、熱應力等其他途徑。但 Ea 大幅提升至少證明「聚合物網路擋住離子遷移」這條路徑被有效封死，這是穩定性提升的微觀基礎。

為什麼要另做一片橫向測試片而不直接用 PeLED 量？垂直 PeLED 結構（ITO/鈣鈦礦/Al）的兩個電極專門設計來「把電子和電洞打進去」，量到的 \(\sigma\) 大半都是電子和電洞貢獻的，離子那一小部分被淹沒；橫向組態的金電極與鈣鈦礦能階對齊不利於注入電子或電洞，電流主要靠離子搬家來搬運，\(\sigma\) 才能乾淨反映離子活動程度。幾個 Nernst–Einstein 擬合的「不能跳的健身操」：第一，掃描溫度不能跨過鈣鈦礦相變點，否則 \(ln(\sigma T) vs. 1/T\) 會出現轉折、單一直線 fit 出的 Ea 變成兩段的平均、沒有物理意義；作者必須畫圖檢查線性度好才能下結論。第二，每個溫度點都要等到樣品熱平衡（通常 5–10 分鐘）再量，否則樣品內部還在升溫、\(\sigma\) 偏離平衡值、\(ln(\sigma T) vs. 1/T\) 散點不線性。量測本身也要在恆定電壓下停留足夠時間取穩態電流，避免離子尚未重新分布的瞬態效應。

工具與材料
  • 離子遷移 (ion migration)：鈣鈦礦中 Br⁻、Cs⁺ 等離子在電場下沿晶格空位跳躍遷移；常溫下空位濃度足以驅動可觀測的離子電流，是元件退化主因之一。
  • 活化能 Ea：離子從晶格位置搬到鄰近空位必須翻的能量山頭高度；對照組 0.27 eV → OEGA/PEA 0.77 eV，提升近三倍。
  • Nernst–Einstein 公式：\(\sigma(T) = (\sigma0/T) \cdot exp(-Ea/kBT)；\)把離子遷移率與擴散係數連起來，指數項是熱激活機率，\(\sigma0\) 含載流子濃度與嘗試頻率。
  • \(ln(\sigma T) vs. 1/T\) 線性擬合：Arrhenius plot 標準動作；斜率 = −Ea/kB，截距 \(= ln(\sigma0)\)，最小二乘法直接解 Ea。
  • 橫向電極組態：兩塊金電極都放在薄膜頂面、間距 50 µm；電流走長橫向路徑，主要靠離子搬家貢獻，\(\sigma\) 才能乾淨反映離子活動。
  • 80 nm 金電極：陰影遮罩熱蒸鍍；金跟鈣鈦礦能階對齊不利於注入電子/電洞，正是要的效果。
  • 50 µm 電極間距：遠大於薄膜厚度 (~20 nm)，迫使電流走長橫向路徑、離子貢獻主導。
  • Cryo Industries 液氮 Dewar：真空保溫桶，把樣品冷到液氮 (77 K) 周圍提供低溫環境。
  • Lake Shore 335 控溫器：帶加熱絲與溫度感測器，從 77 K 一步步精準升到室溫，每升幾度停一下取點。
  • Keithley 2400 源量測單元 (SMU)：精準輸出電壓並同步量電流，在每個溫度點算出橫向導電率 \(\sigma\)。
  • 離子跳躍機制 (ion hopping)：離子從一個晶格位置跳到隔壁空位的微觀路徑；POEGA 聚合物網路嵌晶界把這條長程路徑堵死。

主題十三：3D-FDTD 光學耦合模擬：解析次微米粒子對波導模式的破壞
用三維 FDTD 模擬 PeLED 多層光學堆疊，量化 OEGA/PEA 薄膜表面孤立次微米顆粒（來自侷限奈米晶聚集）如何提取側向波導模式，將奈米形貌 → 光取出效率改善 → EQE 增益建立量化連結。

最佳版本vote 1

作者在電腦裡把整顆 PeLED 拆成一格一格的小立方體，每格標好它是哪一層材料，再用三維時域有限差分模擬 (3D-FDTD) 解 Maxwell 方程式，等於讓光在這個多層三明治裡一步一步『跳格子』地傳出去，最後算出有多少光逃出表面、多少被金屬電極吃掉。模擬的輸入只有兩件事：每一層的厚度與折射率。作者把實際元件依序拆解：玻璃 (n = 1.5) / ITO (n = 1.8, 110 nm) / m-PEDOT:PSS (n = 1.63, 15 nm) / 鈣鈦礦發光層 (n = 2.10 對照 / 2.01 OEGA, 20 nm) / TPBi (n = 1.7, 40 nm) / 鋁電極 (100 nm)；其中鈣鈦礦與 m-PEDOT:PSS 的折射率 n 與吸收係數 k 由橢偏儀 (ellipsometer) 量得，其他層引用 Li 等人 (Adv. Mater. 2021, ref 13)。作者刻意跑兩套模擬，一套是完全平整的三明治、一套是頂層加上實驗 SEM/AFM 量到的次微米顆粒花紋；兩套相減就乾淨切出『顆粒貢獻了多少額外光取出』。

為什麼次微米顆粒能幫忙撈光？因為鈣鈦礦折射率約 2.0、玻璃只有 1.5，平整介面之下，光只要射得比臨界角 \((\sin\theta_c = n_2/n_1 \approx 0.75)\) 更斜就會 100% 被反射回鈣鈦礦裡，沿著薄膜橫著跑，這部分叫『側向波導模式 (lateral-propagating waveguide modes)』，最終會被鋁電極吃光、變成熱損耗。SEM/AFM 看到 OEGA/PEA 薄膜表面均勻撒著一顆顆直徑幾百奈米的小丘 (isolated submicrometric particles)，能量散布光譜 (EDS) 顯示它們的 Cs:Pb:Br 比例與本體相同，作者因此推測是 11 nm 奈米晶在表面又聚集而成的鈣鈦礦小丘。這些小丘打破平整介面、把原本只能側向跑的光散射成各種方向，部分撞到小丘的角度因此能翻過臨界角、逃進玻璃並射向觀眾。FDTD 把『加顆粒 vs. 不加顆粒』兩種情境的逃出比例直接算出來 (Supplementary Fig. 30e)，量化這個光取出 (light outcoupling) 的貢獻。

要算逃出比例，第一件事是處理『發光源的方向』。鈣鈦礦裡的電子-電洞復合可看成一支發光的小天線（偶極），同一支偶極朝不同方向放出的光被多層三明治攔截的比例差很多：橫躺的偶極（x、y 方向，平行於膜面）放出的光容易直接穿出表面，立著的偶極（z 方向）放出的光大半被困住。公式 \((3) P=\Theta(P_x^s+P_y^s)+(1-\Theta)P_z^s\) 因此把總輻射功率拆成『橫躺貢獻』與『站著貢獻』，再用權重 Θ 加權合成。Θ 是橫躺偶極佔的比例，叫『水平偶極比 (horizontal dipole ratio)』；鈣鈦礦的發光偶極方向隨機（各向同性發射體, isotropic emitter），三維裡有兩維是橫的，所以 Θ = 2/3。實作上，FDTD 一次只能放一支單一方向偶極跑模擬，作者因此分別在 x、y、z 各跑一次（多偏振模擬, multiple polarization simulations），再用公式 (3) 加權合成，把『隨機方向發光』翻成可計算的非相干各向同性光源 (參考 Cui et al., Sci. Adv. 2021, ref 71)。

FDTD 雖然原理清楚，但結果好不好用，靠的是輸入是否誠實，作者在四個地方刻意把關。第一，顆粒形貌必須是真實的：作者把 SEM/AFM 量到的 OEGA/PEA 薄膜表面細節直接導入模型，不是隨便假設一個規則花紋，這樣算出來的才是『這顆元件實際多撈到多少光』，而不是『假設花紋下的理論上限』。第二，折射率必須對齊到實際薄膜：pristine 與 OEGA 兩款各跑一次橢偏儀，獨立量到 n = 2.10 與 2.01 並分別餵進 FDTD；如果隨便共用同一組 n，就會高估被困光比例、把『加顆粒能撈出的光』算得比實際大。第三，偶極比 Θ = 2/3 不能拿掉：把 Θ 設成 1 等於假設所有偶極都橫躺，會吹高取出效率；設成 0 又低估。第四，必須跑『平面』對照：EQE 從 10.7% 跳到 21.8% 同時有兩個來源——奈米晶 PLQY 提升到 83%，以及表面顆粒撈光；只算加顆粒版本就分不清這兩部分各佔多少，靠『平面 vs. 加顆粒』兩套相減，作者才能乾淨把這個 EQE 增益歸給次微米顆粒形貌。

工具與材料
  • 3D Finite-Difference Time-Domain (3D-FDTD)：三維時域有限差分模擬：把元件拆成一格一格小立方體，每隔一小段時間更新每格的電磁場，逐步算光在多層結構裡如何傳遞與逃逸。
  • Ellipsometer：橢偏儀：量測薄膜折射率實部 n 與虛部 k 的標準工具，作者用它取得 m-PEDOT:PSS 與鈣鈦礦層的光學常數（pristine n = 2.10、OEGA 處理 n = 2.01）。
  • Lateral-propagating waveguide modes：側向波導模式：光被夾在高折射率鈣鈦礦薄膜裡橫著跑、出不來的部分，最終被鋁電極吸收成熱損耗。
  • Light outcoupling：光取出：把原本被困在元件裡的光導出表面變成可見發光的物理過程，是 EQE 的乘法因子之一。
  • Isolated submicrometric particles：次微米顆粒：OEGA/PEA 薄膜表面均勻撒佈、直徑幾百奈米的小丘，EDS 顯示為鈣鈦礦組成，推測來自 11 nm 奈米晶在表面再聚集。
  • Horizontal dipole ratio (Θ)：水平偶極比：橫躺（x、y 方向）偶極佔總發光偶極的比例。各向同性發射體 Θ = 2/3。
  • Isotropic emitter：各向同性發射體：發光偶極方向隨機、各方向機率相同的光源；鈣鈦礦屬於這類，因此用 Θ = 2/3 處理。
  • Multiple polarization simulations：多偏振模擬：分別在 x、y、z 方向各跑一次單一方向偶極，再用公式 (3) 加權合成非相干各向同性光源。
  • Equation (3)：\(P=\Theta(P_x^s+P_y^s)+(1-\Theta)P_z^s：\)把三個方向偶極的輻射功率依水平偶極比 Θ 加權成總輻射功率，方法承襲 Cui et al. Sci. Adv. 2021 (ref 71)。

作者規定的粒徑量測流程很單純但條件很硬。每張 TEM 照片載入 Nano Measurer 影像量測軟體，人工或半自動在每顆方正立方體形狀的奈米晶上拉一條線，量它的『立方奈米晶邊長 (edge length of cubic nanocrystals)』作為這顆晶的尺寸——OEGA 處理後晶粒呈立方相、外形規整，邊長是三個方向都相等的特徵尺寸，比直徑或外接圓更明確、更可重複。重疊或聚集的顆粒一律排除：TEM 是二維投影，兩顆 11 nm 奈米晶疊在一起時投影輪廓會像 ~20 nm 大粒，若保留就會把粒徑分布人為向右拉。取樣面上，每組配方必須蒐集 ≥ 100 顆，並且這些顆粒來自同一片網格的不同區域與不同樣品，避免把局部好區當成全片代表。

100 顆下限的背後是一條簡單的算式：平均尺寸的不確定度約以 \(\sigma/\sqrt{N}\) 縮小（\(\sigma\) 是粒徑分布的標準差，N 是樣本數）。若分布相對標準差約 30%（如 11 ± 3 nm），N = 100 把平均的標準誤差壓到約 3%，能可信地宣稱『OEGA 1% 與其他配方的微小差異真實存在』；若只量 20 顆，標準誤差會擴大 ~2.2 倍，OEGA 1% vs. MPEG-MAA 1k (9 nm) 這類接近的差距就會被雜訊掩蓋。鈣鈦礦 LED 領域比的是粒徑階梯，少於 100 顆基本上沒有判別力。

粒徑統計回答『奈米晶有沒有真的變小』；接下來換到元件層級，要回答的是『同樣配方做成 PeLED 有沒有重現性』。器件統計把多片獨立做出的 PeLED 視為各自獨立的樣本：作者每個配方一次做 32 片，每片獨立量它的最高 EQE，畫成『32 顆元件直方圖』——對照組平均 9.0%、OEGA/PEA 19.5%。32 片是 OLED/PeLED 領域常見的下限，能同時呈現平均效能與分布形狀，並把平均 EQE 的標準誤差壓到小於 1 個百分點。除了 EQE 直方圖，作者另外做 32 片 OEGA 薄膜的 PL 峰位直方圖，平均從 490.8 nm 微移到 490.0 nm，獨立驗證『藍光顏色批次間一致』。最後，作者把代表性元件送到獨立的第三方實驗室再量一次 EQE，排除自家儀器系統性偏高的疑慮，作為紀錄數字級別成果的清白證明。

鈣鈦礦 LED 領域歷史上多次出現『單顆冠軍刷紀錄、別人重現不出來』的案例。如果作者只報那顆 21.8% EQE 冠軍而沒有平均與直方圖，審稿人與下游讀者無法分辨『這配方真的厲害』與『32 顆裡只有那一顆碰巧好』。32 片直方圖讓 9.0% → 19.5% 的平均差距變成可重現的事實，而非僥倖；PL 峰位直方圖再補上『顏色一致性』；第三方交叉驗證再補上『儀器無偏』。三層保險缺一不可。

方法組合策略

(Synergy & Pipeline)

這篇研究的強大之處在於巧妙組合了以下步驟 (Pipeline)：

1. 「配位先卡位、聚合後固鎖」雙時序策略：

  • Step 1 – 配位卡位（旋塗前）： OEGA 的多氧醚/羰基進入 PbBr2 第一配位殼層取代部分 DMSO（熵驅動，ΔG = −0.66 eV），形成 crown ether 樣多齒中間體，延遲成核（LP-TEM 觀察 > 10 min 無核成）。
  • Step 2 – 聚合固鎖（退火期）： 60 °C 同時觸發 AIBN 熱分解（產生自由基）與鈣鈦礦結晶；OEGA 乙烯基在晶簇生長表面就地交聯為 POEGA 網路，將奈米晶物理鎖在 ~11 nm 尺寸（in situ TEM 顯示 >300 nm 巨晶生成被完全壓制）。
  • 效果： 解決傳統「非聚合配體只能配位、預形成聚合物只能侷限」的二選一問題——配位降低生長驅動力 + 動態聚合提供成長截止點，缺一不可。對照組 N-MOA（僅配位）→ 大晶；PAA（僅聚合物侷限）→ 弱配位；混合卻不聚合（OEGA 無 AIBN）→ <100 nm 與 >300 nm 雙峰分佈。

    • 2. 「小尺寸→立方相→低聲子耦合」的結構-光物理耦合鏈：

  • Step 1： 尺寸降到 11 nm → 表面/體積比放大 → 立方相表面能 (2.84 meV/Å) 顯著低於正交相 (4.61 meV/Å) → 立方相熱力學穩定（VASP 預測 + Extended Data Fig. 2）。
  • Step 2： 立方相 [PbBr6]4- 八面體扭曲度下降 → 低頻 Raman 強度降低、線寬模糊 → LO 聲子能 39.2 → 28.7 meV、耦合 γLO 228 → 116 meV → 非輻射損耗顯著抑制。
  • 效果： 將「縮小晶粒」這個尺寸控制動作，透過熱力學論證直接連到「相變」與「電子-聲子去耦」，為 PLQY 83% 提供完整的因果鏈，而非僅描述現象。

    • 3. 「PEA 表面鈍化 + OEGA 體相侷限」的層級分工：

  • Step 1： OEGA 內嵌於晶界並深入到第一配位殼（DFT 算得 9.2 eV 結合能），主導奈米晶尺寸與相。
  • Step 2： 後加的 PEABr 結合能僅 1.8 eV，無法擠走 OEGA，被熱力學「逼到」晶界處作鈍化（Supplementary Fig. 25），既避免形成 2D 阻擋相、又補充表面缺陷鈍化。
  • 效果： 解決過去 PEA 易在鈣鈦礦中自組裝成 2D 相而阻礙電荷注入的痛點，τavg 從 OEGA-only 的 15.2 ns 提升到 23.6 ns，PLQY 83%、EQE 21.8%。

    • 4. 「LP-TEM 動力學 ↔ MD 聚合動力學 ↔ DFT 熱力學」的計算-實驗三角驗證：

  • LP-TEM 提供時間序列影像 → MD 給出單體結構為何敏感於 m 與側鏈長度 → DFT 給出 ΔG 與相穩定性。三者結合論證 OEGA 是局部最優而非偶然。
  • 效果： 對「為何不是 P-MOA、不是 MPEG-MAA、不是 PAA」的問題，提供物理化學完整可信的多尺度解釋，這是這篇文章相較單純添加劑工程研究的關鍵差異。

借鑑價值

(Takeaways for your research)

如果你未來的研究遇到以下狀況，可以借鑑這篇論文的方法：

  • 【尺寸與結晶度的內在矛盾】 如果你正在合成的奈米/微米晶面臨「想小又想結晶好」的矛盾（例如：膠體量子點、奈米藥物晶體、催化劑奈米粒），不要再尋找更強的單分子配體；可以考慮加入「兼具配位基團 + 可聚合官能團（乙烯基、丙烯酸酯）」的單體 + 自由基引發劑，讓配體在生長過程中就地交聯，把奈米晶機械物理性卡住。
  • 【尋找配體相對結合能 vs. 溶劑】 如果你發現新配體和金屬中心的單點結合能比現有溶劑還弱，但實驗上仍能擠走溶劑——不要急著放棄配體；計算「整體溶劑置換的 Gibbs 自由能」並把熵效應（釋出自由溶劑分子）也納入。多齒螯合 + 熵增可以翻轉表觀的能量劣勢，這是 OEGA 啟示的標準診斷流程。
  • 【在有限晶格尺寸下穩定亞穩相】 如果你需要在室溫穩定材料的高溫相（如 CsPbI3 黑相、SnO2 立方相等），與其去改組成或施壓，不妨用 DFT 計算高對稱相 vs. 低對稱相的「表面能差」，並將該差值用「\(E_{\mathrm{NC}} = \Delta_{\mathrm{bulk}} + \dfrac{2V_{\mathrm{unit}}\sum E_{\mathrm{surf}}}{d}\)」反推臨界尺寸；再去設計合成手段將晶粒降到該尺寸以下。OEGA paper 提供完整套路。
  • 【區分「配位」vs.「聚合侷限」貢獻】 如果你的新策略同時涉及「化學配位」與「物理空間侷限」兩個機制，建議設計「拆分對照」：(a) 同分子骨架但去掉乙烯基（只剩配位）；(b) 預先聚合好的聚合物（只剩侷限）；(c) 完整 in situ 聚合系統。LP-TEM/原位光學/MD 三軸即可乾淨拆出兩貢獻。
  • 【量化離子遷移阻擋】 如果你想驗證添加劑/聚合物網路是否抑制鈣鈦礦或離子型半導體中的離子遷移，使用 Nernst–Einstein 公式擬合橫向 σ(T) 萃取 Ea，比 J–V 滯後分析更直接，且可定量比較不同配方。
  • 【藍光鈣鈦礦組成設計】 想做 ~490 nm 純藍光而不靠 Cl 摻雜（避免相分離）時，可以參考 Cs0.7EA0.3PbBr3 的 A-site 混合做法，搭配本文配體策略，避開鹵化物分離難題並保留高 PLQY。
  • 【表面波導模式提取】 如果你的 LED/OLED 元件薄膜表面自然存在 sub-µm 顆粒（曾被視為缺陷），先用 3D-FDTD 模擬該形貌對光取出的影響——可能反而是 EQE 提升的「免費光學結構」，不必硬性追求超平表面。
  • 【設計多尺度計算-實驗驗證鏈】 當你提出新機制時，建議刻意組合「DFT 熱力學 + MD 動力學 + 原位實驗」三層：DFT 算 ΔG 解釋為何能驅動；MD 跑動力學解釋反應/聚合時序；in situ 顯微/光譜捕捉實際路徑。這篇 paper 的三角驗證是值得仿效的模板。

Metal halide perovskites have emerged as promising semiconductors for light-emitting diodes (LEDs) owing to their excellent luminescence properties1. However, their performance remains limited, primarily owing to the inherent contradiction between ‘high crystallinity’ and ‘small size’ in the in situ synthesis of perovskite nanocrystals on substrates. Here we report efficient blue perovskite LEDs (PeLEDs) achieved via in situ polymerization-driven nanocrystal confinement to synthesize perovskite films composed of high-quality nanocrystals. Metal halide perovskites are promising candidates for next-generation light-emitting materials owing to their tunable bandgaps, high colour purity and low-cost solution processability1,2. Recently, perovskite light-emitting diodes (PeLEDs) have undergone rapid developments, with the external quantum efficiency (EQE) exceeding 30% for green and red emissions3–6. Nevertheless, there is still a clear gap between PeLEDs and organic LEDs as well as metal chalcogenide-based quantum-dot LEDs7–12, especially for the blue-emitting counterparts. To improve the performance of blue PeLEDs, perovskites with high crystallinity and nanoscale grain sizes are required. High crystallinity with low defect density suppresses non-radiative recombination losses and material degradation13, whereas small grains enhance radiative efficiency through charge confinement and limited diffusion14,15.

Despite extensive studies on perovskite crystallization kinetics, it is still an inherent challenge to simultaneously achieve perovskite nanocrystals with high crystallinity and nanoscale confinement through in situ synthesis on substrates. Current strategies, including solvent engineering16,17, thermal annealing18,19 and additive engineering20, primarily improve crystallinity by retarding nucleation and promoting crystal growth. These approaches contribute significantly to the device stability13, but typically yield micrometre-scale grains that limit emission efficiency. Meanwhile, perovskite nanocrystals grown in situ on substrates generally suffer from low monodispersity, defective surfaces and poor crystallinity, which collectively degrade their luminescence21. As a result, this long-standing trade-off between crystallinity and size confinement has limited the full potential of perovskite nanocrystals in PeLED applications. Therefore, it is necessary to develop a simple, controllable and universal approach that enables precise control of both crystallinity and nanoscale confinement in perovskite nanocrystals for optoelectronics.

Here we propose a facile in situ polymerization strategy to produce perovskite nanocrystals with both high crystallinity and confined sizes. The polymer network formed in situ from oligo(ethylene glycol) methyl ether acrylate (OEGA) dynamically constrains nanocrystal overgrowth during crystallization, reducing the grain size from over 250 nm to 11 nm. This facilitates carrier confinement and intergranular coupling. Owing to the strong coordination affinity, OEGA interacts effectively with perovskite precursors, moderating the initial rapid growth of perovskite clusters and thereby improving crystallinity. The fully confined nanocrystals exhibit a cubic phase at room temperature with decreased octahedral distortions, substantially mitigating non-radiative losses caused by electron–phonon coupling. Accordingly, blue PeLEDs realize a recorded EQE of 21.8% and prolonged operational stability of 69.4 min. This work demonstrates a feasible in situ nanocrystal confinement approach that advances understanding of the effect of ligand engineering on perovskite nanocrystal synthesis, benefiting the development of efficient blue PeLEDs and related optoelectronic technologies.

In situ nanocrystal confinement strategy

Inspired by colloidal perovskite nanocrystal synthesis, we find that the ligand structure has a crucial role in crystallization processes, influencing nanocrystal size, monodispersity, morphology and surface states20,22. For in situ synthesis on substrates, ideal ligands should provide continuous confinement throughout the perovskite crystallization process, and have a strong binding affinity to their framework to prevent rapid cluster aggregation. This promotes crystal rearrangement and ordered lattice alignments, yielding high-quality, small perovskite nanocrystals with reduced defects23,24. However, directly introducing conventional organic ligands can not meet both requirements, prompting our development of an in situ polymerization strategy using polymerizable monomers. Effective monomers require: (1) linear rather than cyclic configurations for minimal steric hindrance and effective nanocrystal encapsulation25; and (2) sufficient coordination sites for strong binding towards the perovskite lattice26.

Cs0.7EA0.3PbBr3 (EA, ethylamine) blue-emitting perovskites are selected as a typical example to evaluate this in situ-confinement strategy and its potential application in LEDs. The pristine film, prepared via the one-step anti-solvent approach27 (Fig. 1a), exhibits large crystal gains of 270 ± 88 nm (Fig. 1b and Supplementary Fig. 1a). Attempts are made to directly introduce ligands into precursor solutions, including non-polymerizable passivating molecules and polymers (with carbonyl or ether groups as electron donors) (Extended Data Fig. 1 and Supplementary Figs. 2 and 3). Non-polymerizable ligands such as 2-methoxyethyl acetate (N-MOA) and dimethyl malonate fail to reduce the grain size and instead yield larger perovskite grains (514 ± 120 nm and 504 ± 125 nm), probably owing to their short-chain structures limiting spatial confinement. Moreover, when high-molecular-weight polymers (methoxy polyethylene glycol (MPEG) and poly(acrylamide) (PAA) are incorporated directly, they tend to form amorphous aggregates with poor perovskite lattice affinity, yielding grains averaging 202 ± 44 nm. Impressively, polymerizable monomers such as 2-methoxyethyl acrylate (P-MOA) significantly reduce the perovskite grain size to 52 ± 16 nm (Fig. 1c and Supplementary Fig. 1b). This trend extends to other monomers such as 2-aminoethyl methacrylate (AEM), N,N′-methylene bis(acrylamide) and trimethylolpropane triacrylate, resulting in average nanocrystal sizes of 40–60 nm (Supplementary Fig. 4). These monomers with unsaturated vinyl groups enable easy polymerization with free radical initiators at low temperatures28,29, ensuring in situ polymerization during nanocrystal growth. The consistent size reduction across diverse monomer structures highlights the central role of the in situ polymerization reaction in modulating perovskite crystallization.

Mechanistic studies further reveal that as the number-average molecular weight (Mn) of the monomer increases from 120 (P-MOA) to 480 (OEGA) and 1,000 (poly(ethylene glycol) methyl ether acrylate, MPEG-MAA), the monomers increasingly constrain the nanocrystals to 9 ± 1 nm (Fig. 1c–e and Supplementary Fig. 1b–d). In contrast, an excessive molecular weight of 5,000 induces ligand self-aggregation, causing nanocrystal size heterogeneity (Fig. 1f and Supplementary Fig. 1e). Furthermore, controlled increases in ligand concentration can also effectively reduce nanocrystal sizes (Supplementary Figs. 5 and 6). This implies that the in situ synthesis of controllable nanocrystal sizes can be achieved by adjusting both the chain length and content of polymerizable monomers.

Among the tested ligands, OEGA provides an optimal balance between confinement and charge transport in perovskite nanocrystals. OEGA (1%) reduces the grain size to 11 ± 3 nm (Fig. 1d) and forms a thin coating layer via strong coordination, as evidenced by electron energy loss spectra (Fig. 1g). Low-dose high-resolution transmission electron microscopy analysis reveals a ligand-induced structural phase transition. Fast Fourier transform analysis of pristine perovskites (Fig. 1h) shows diffraction spots indexed to the orthorhombic (\(\gamma\)) phase along the [223] and [332] zone axis. In contrast, OEGA nanocrystals exhibit cubic (\(\alpha\)) symmetry across multiple crystallographic orientations (Fig. 1i and Extended Data Fig. 2c–f), indicating an orthorhombic-to-cubic phase transition. The transition is also verified by X-ray diffraction measurements, showing that peak evolves from orthorhombic (Pnma) to cubic symmetry (Pm\(\bar{3}\)m) with increasing OEGA content (Fig. 1j and Extended Data Fig. 2a,b). Notably, the OEGA-treated films show significantly enhanced diffraction intensity, reflecting their improved crystallinity.

To elucidate the impact of polymerizable monomers, we investigated their evolution and interaction with perovskites. Proton nuclear magnetic resonance (1H NMR) confirms the thermal polymerization of OEGA (Fig. 2a). After annealing, vinyl proton signals at 5.9–6.4 ppm disappear completely, signifying polymerization of vinyl groups and formation of a poly(oligo (ethylene glycol) acrylate) (POEGA) network. Electron paramagnetic resonance further verifies the absence of residual radicals in the final film (Supplementary Fig. 7 and Supplementary Table 1). X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (Fig. 2b) reveals that both Pb 4f5/2 (138.1 eV) and Pb 4f7/2 (143.0 eV) peaks shift towards lower binding energies after OEGA incorporation, demonstrating reduced charge on Pb2+ ions through OEGA coordination. A similar negative shift is also observed for the Br 3d3/2 and Br 3d5/2 peaks (Supplementary Fig. 8a). This interaction promotes the formation of OEGA coating layers throughout the crystallization process, as evidenced by electron energy loss spectroscopy analysis (Fig. 1g), facilitating defect passivation and grain stabilization.

To investigate the effects of in situ polymerization on the entire crystallization process of perovskites, we conducted in situ ultraviolet–visible absorption and photoluminescence analyses (Fig. 2c,d). Pristine perovskites show simultaneous emergence of absorption and photoluminescence peaks upon anti-solvent treatment, indicating rapid cluster aggregation and growth. In contrast, OEGA-modified samples show delayed emission onset with gradual redshift and intensity enhancement, suggesting suppressed initial cluster aggregation and improved lattice relaxation. During subsequent annealing, pristine films show pronounced thermal photoluminescence quenching (Supplementary Fig. 8b), whereas OEGA perovskites achieve enhanced final absorption and photoluminescence intensity, consistent with improved crystallinity and confined nanocrystal sizes.

The growth dynamics are further visualized by in situ liquid-phase electron microscopy imaging of precursor solutions (Extended Data Fig. 3). Pristine precursors form sol–gel intermediates instantaneously under low-dose electron irradiation (5 e− (Å2 s)−1), rapidly grow along the nanopipette tip, and ultimately grow into uncontrollable large aggregates. In contrast, OEGA- and 2,2′-azobis(2-methylpropionitrile) (AIBN)-modified precursors show no detectable nucleation for over 10 min under identical conditions. Increasing the electron dose rate to 12 e− (Å2 s−1) induces solvent extraction and local supersaturation30, mimicking the perovskite spin-coating process. This triggers the formation of numerous nanoscale particles (approximately 10 nm) that remain stable over time, contrasting with pristine systems. These observations align with the in situ optical measurements (Fig. 2c,d), demonstrating that OEGA delays nucleation of perovskite precursors and subsequently stabilizes nanocrystal size through polymer-network confinement. Comparatively, non-polymerizable ligand N-MOA slows nanocrystal growth but fails to confine the size owing to the absence of a rigid network, yielding larger particle sizes. Preformed polymer PAA shows negligible nanocrystal growth restriction, reflecting its static and weak interactions with precursors. These contrasts demonstrate that the in situ polymerization process is critical for size regulation, providing key insights into ligand-mediated growth control for nanocrystal engineering.

To clarify the mechanism, density functional theory calculations (Supplementary Note 1) are performed to compare the thermodynamics of OEGA and solvent (dimethyl sulfoxide (DMSO)) coordination with lead bromide (PbBr2). The carbonyl (0.74 eV) and ether (0.67 eV) groups of OEGA show slightly weaker binding energies with PbBr2 (Supplementary Fig. 9 and Supplementary Table 2) than DMSO (Supplementary Fig. 10 and Supplementary Table 3), yielding a minimal binding-energy difference (\(\Delta E_b\approx 0.1\,\text{eV}\)) for one-site OEGA adsorption to replace DMSO (Supplementary Fig. 11). However, such an exchange increases entropy owing to the release of free DMSO molecules, yielding a favourable Gibbs free energy (\(\Delta G=-0.66\,\text{eV}\); Supplementary Tables 4 and 5), which is expected to further decrease upon polymerization. The thermodynamic stabilization arises from multiple O–Pb coordination of OEGA, adopting a crown ether-like configuration (Supplementary Fig. 9) known to slow crystallization and improve crystal quality31–33. OEGA also binds nanocrystal surfaces at multiple sites (Supplementary Fig. 12), while its in situ polymerization provides crystallization modulation, as discussed below.

Molecular dynamics simulations of polymerization reactions (Supplementary Note 2, Supplementary Fig. 13 and Supplementary Table 6) reveal that monomer structure critically influences polymerization efficiency and growth modulation. Small monomers such as P-MOA polymerize rapidly owing to high densities of reactive termini, whereas their short side chains lack sufficient coordination capacity to restrict crystal evolution (Supplementary Figs. 14 and 15). Medium-length OEGA achieves optimal kinetic synchronization with perovskite crystallization through balanced reactive group densities, whereas its extended side chains offer structural flexibility and considerable sites that facilitate polymer–perovskite interactions for controlled growth (Supplementary Figs. 16 and 17). Large monomers such as MPEG-MAA (Mn 1,000 and 5,000) show suppressed polymerization rates owing to self-interactions, limiting their utility in perovskite crystallization. These findings highlight the importance of balancing polymerization dynamics and coordination affinity for crystal engineering.

On the basis of these findings, Figs. 1a and 2e schematically illustrate the crystallization pathway enabled by the in situ polymerization strategy. OEGA incorporation enables an in situ-formed polymer network, which effectively constrains crystal overgrowth during crystallization. The multiple coordination bonds thermodynamically stabilize intermediates, and further suppress rapid perovskite cluster growth while facilitating intergranular coupling. The prolonged relaxation of small clusters promotes ordered lattice formation with reduced defects, leading to enhanced crystallinity23. In contrast, pristine films show accelerated phase transformation from intermediates to perovskite during spin-coating, where unregulated cluster aggregation disrupts optimal stacking and generates lattice imperfections34. Subsequent annealing further promotes grain coarsening, leading to increased non-radiative recombination losses.

These results establish in situ polymerization as a distinct strategy for confined nanocrystal growth. Although conventional non-polymerizable passivators can improve crystallinity by slowing growth, they often induce excessive grain coarsening. In contrast, the dynamic confinement environment generated by polymerization simultaneously reinforces crystallinity and restricts grain growth, providing a synergistic pathway to enhance radiative recombination.

We further demonstrate the generality of this strategy across diverse perovskite systems, spanning deep-blue to sky-blue emissions (Supplementary Fig. 18a–d). Notably, applying this approach to the predominant photovoltaic material formamidinium lead iodide (FAPbI3) also yields smaller, cubic-phase nanocrystals (Fig. 2f,g and Supplementary Fig. 18e,f). The resulting perovskite nanocrystals show largely substrate-independent properties when deposited on various surfaces (Supplementary Fig. 19). Beyond in situ nanocrystal synthesis, this polymerization-assisted approach is also applicable to colloidal nanocrystal synthesis, where it unexpectedly improves phase purity, photoluminescence efficiency and colloidal stability (Supplementary Figs. 20 and 21).

The phase of lead halide perovskites is governed by [PbBr6]4− octahedral distortions, which critically determine optical and electronic properties. Whereas bulk pristine grains adopt the orthorhombic phase, the in situ-confined nanocrystals maintain the high-temperature cubic phase at room temperature. It is demonstrated that, in addition to pressure, temperature and compositions35, the physical size critically modulates phase stability. Density functional theory calculations36 reveal that the cubic phase has significantly lower surface energy than the tetragonal and orthorhombic phases (Supplementary Note 3 and Supplementary Tables 7 and 8). Therefore, size confinement stabilizes the perovskite cubic phase by increasing the surface-to-volume ratio (Extended Data Fig. 2g,h and Supplementary Table 9), which leads to a reduced critical temperature for the orthorhombic-to-cubic phase transition. Moreover, ligand-induced surface interactions further reinforce this stability37,38, enabling the confined nanocrystals in our experiments to maintain the cubic phase as thermodynamically favourable at room temperature.

The in situ-confinement effect drives a phase transition of perovskite nanocrystals from the original orthorhombic to cubic structure, with direct implications for luminescence properties. Temperature-dependent photoluminescence measurements show gradual intensity attenuation and full-width at half-maximum (FWHM) broadening from 100 K to 325 K (Supplementary Fig. 22). Notably, pristine nanocrystals exhibit more severe thermal quenching than their cubic counterparts, even at low temperatures (Fig. 3a), which could be partially attributed to stronger electron–phonon coupling associated with octahedral distortions in the orthorhombic lattice. Quantitative analysis based on the temperature-dependent linewidth broadening39–41 reveals that the in situ-confined nanocrystals show markedly reduced longitudinal-optical (LO) phonon energy and electron–phonon coupling strength (\(\gamma_{\mathrm{LO}}=115.5\,\text{meV}\), \(E_{\mathrm{LO}}=28.7\,\text{meV}\)) compared with pristine nanocrystals (\(\gamma_{\mathrm{LO}}=228.0\,\text{meV}\), \(E_{\mathrm{LO}}=39.2\,\text{meV}\); Fig. 3b). This result indicates suppressed lattice vibrational energies in the in situ-confined nanocrystals, consequently reducing non-radiative recombination pathways and enhancing the photoluminescence quantum yield (PLQY).

Low-frequency Raman spectra further elucidate the lattice vibrations in pristine and OEGA perovskites (Fig. 3c). Pristine perovskites exhibit five Raman peaks at 200 K, assigning to octahedral distortions42, which broaden and redshift with increasing temperature owing to enhanced thermal vibrations and electron–phonon interactions42. In contrast, OEGA perovskites exhibit a broad spectral band with a central peak, reflecting inherent vibrational instabilities from highly anharmonic double-well potentials in the cubic phase43. Crucially, OEGA samples maintain spectral stability at 200 K, confirming robust low-temperature phase stability. The significantly reduced Raman intensity in OEGA perovskites indicates suppressed lattice vibrational modes, critically mitigating electron–phonon coupling44.

On the basis of the optimized OEGA/PEA perovskite films with enhanced stability and PLQYs, PeLEDs were fabricated with the architecture of indium tin oxide/m-PEDOT:PSS/perovskite/TPBi/LiF/Al (Supplementary Fig. 27). The corresponding energy-level alignment is shown in Fig. 4a, where modified-poly(3,4-ethylenedioxythiophene): poly(styrene-sulfonate) (m-PEDOT:PSS) and 1,3,5-tris(1-phenyl-1H-benzimidazol-2-yl)benzene (TPBi) act as effective hole and electron transport layers to facilitate carrier injection. PeLEDs incorporating confined perovskite nanocrystals show narrow blue emission at 491 nm with CIE (International Commission on Illumination) coordinates of (0.07, 0.29) and a FWHM of 23 nm (Fig. 4b,g). Incorporation of OEGA/PEA induces a modest increase in current density under high voltage compared with pristine devices (Fig. 4c), accompanied by a substantial enhancement in maximum luminance from approximately 720 cd m−2 to 1,925 cd m−2 (Fig. 4d). As shown in Fig. 4e, the optimized devices achieve a peak EQE of 21.8%, nearly doubling that of pristine devices (10.7%), ranking among the highest EQEs reported for blue PeLEDs (Supplementary Table 11). A comparison study of devices modified with PEA, OEGA and OEGA/PEA (Supplementary Fig. 28), respectively, reveals that OEGA has the dominant role in performance enhancement. Statistical analysis of 32 devices further confirms good reproducibility, with the average EQE increasing from 9.0% for pristine devices to 19.5% for OEGA/PEA devices (Fig. 4f).

Apart from the high-quality confined nanocrystals, device efficiency also benefits from the optimized surface morphology of OEGA/PEA perovskite films. As evidenced by scanning electron microscopy and atomic force microscopy images (Supplementary Fig. 29), isolated submicrometric particles are uniformly distributed across the surface of OEGA/PEA perovskite films, which probably arise from the aggregation of confined nanocrystals (Extended Data Fig. 4). This morphology facilitates the extraction of lateral-propagating waveguide modes in planar devices, thereby reducing optical losses45 and providing a complementary contribution to the device performance (Supplementary Fig. 30).

The OEGA/PEA PeLEDs also show improved spectral and operational stability. As shown in Fig. 4g, both the electroluminescence peak position and FWHM of the OEGA/PEA PeLED remain constant under a bias voltage up to 8 V, in contrast to pristine devices (Supplementary Fig. 31). The operational half lifetime (T50) is markedly increased from 10.3 min of the pristine device to 69.4 min of the OEGA/PEA PeLED. To understand this stability enhancement, we tested the thermal conductance (\(\sigma(T)\), where \(\sigma\) is the conductivity and \(T\) is the temperature) of perovskite films to estimate ion-migration activation energies (\(E_a\))46,47. Fitting results (Fig. 4h) yield an \(E_a\) of 0.27 eV for pristine films and 0.77 eV for OEGA/PEA films, confirming that the in situ-formed polymer network effectively suppresses ion migration.

Conclusion

We have developed an in situ-confinement strategy that enables the simultaneous realization of high crystallinity and nanoscale size control in perovskite nanocrystals grown directly on substrates. The dynamically formed polymer network restricts nanocrystal overgrowth, while strong coordination between polymerizable ligands and perovskite clusters prolongs lattice rearrangement, yielding highly crystalline, low-defect nanocrystals without sacrificing charge transport. This study enriches the scope of ligand categories and advances understanding of the effect of ligand engineering on nanocrystal synthesis. By simultaneously controlling the thermal dynamics of perovskite crystallization and organic ligand reactions, it solves the contradiction between the ‘high crystallinity’ and ‘small size’ in the in situ synthesis of perovskite nanocrystals on substrates. This method is both simple and universal, with potential for extension to diverse quantum dots owing to similar interactions, advancing high-performance PeLEDs and next-generation optoelectronics.

Methods

Materials and reagents

Lead bromide (PbBr2), caesium bromide (CsBr, 99.999%), DMSO and poly(sodium 4-styrene-sulfonate) (PSS-Na, Mw (weight-average molecular weight) ≈ 70,000) were purchased from Sigma-Aldrich. Trimethylolpropane triacrylate and MPEG (average Mn = 5,000) were purchased from Macklin. Poly(acrylamide) (average Mn = 40,000) and 2-methoxyethyl acetate (M-MOA) were purchased from Adamas. Dimethyl malonate was purchased from Bide Pharmatech. 2-Aminoethyl methacrylate was purchased from Konoscience. N,N′-methylene bis(acrylamide) and potassium bromide (KBr) were purchased from Aladdin. TPBi was purchased from Xi’an Polymer Light Technology. Lithium fluoride (LiF, 99.99%) was purchased from Alfa Aesar. P-MOA (Sigma-Aldrich), OEGA (average Mn = 480, Sigma-Aldrich) and MPEG-MAA (average Mn = 1,000 and 5,000, Macklin) were purified by passing through basic alumina to remove the radical inhibitor before use. AIBN (Sigma-Aldrich, 98%) was recrystallized from methanol. Further adjustments are made to the purchased PEDOT:PSS (AI 4083) aqueous solution to fine-tune its work function and improve infiltration. The modified-PEDOT:PSS solution was a 1:1 volume mixture of a double-diluted PEDOT:PSS aqueous solution and an aqueous solution containing 15 mg ml−1 PSS-Na and 3 mg ml−1 KBr.

Materials synthesis

PEABr was synthesized by slowly adding 0.12 mol hydrobromic acid to a solution of 0.1 mol phenethylamine in ethanol with vigorously stirring at 0 °C for 2 h. Subsequently, the solution was concentrated by evaporating the solution at 50 °C, which was washed with ethanol 3 times and then dried under vacuum at 40 °C for 24 h. The final products were sealed in a nitrogen-filled glovebox for future use.

Perovskite precursor solution preparation

The perovskite precursor solution was prepared by dissolving CsBr (0.1 M), EABr (0.044 M) and PbBr2 (0.11 M) into DMSO under continuous stirring overnight. For OEGA/PEA perovskites, OEGA, PEABr (0.03 M) and AIBN (1% of OEGA) were directly added to the precursor solution before use, and the ratio of OEGA refers to the mass ratio between OEGA and PbBr2. Before use, the mixture was filtered through a 0.45 μm polytetrafluoroethylene membrane.

Device fabrication

The indium tin oxide (ITO)-coated glass substrates were sequentially cleaned by sonication in detergent, deionized water, acetone and isopropyl alcohol, and then further treated with ultraviolet-ozone cleaner for 20 min. The m-PEDOT:PSS aqueous solution was spin-coated onto the ITO substrates and annealed at 150 °C for 15 min in ambient air. Then the perovskite films were prepared in a nitrogen-filled glovebox by spin-coating at 3,500 rpm. After spin-coating for 36 s, 450 μl toluene anti-solvent containing 0.2 mg ml−1 poly(methyl methacrylate) was dropped onto the film, followed by annealing at 60 °C for 20 min and a subsequent 3 s CF4 plasma treatment. Finally, TPBi (40 nm), LiF (1 nm) and Al electrode (100 nm) were deposited sequentially through a thermal evaporator.

Characterization

The steady-state photoluminescence spectra and time-resolved photoluminescence spectra were recorded on the FLS980 (Edinburgh Instruments) with an excitation at 345 nm. The ultraviolet–visible absorption spectra of the samples were obtained by a ultraviolet–visible diffuse reflectance spectrophotometer (UV–vis DRS, Japan Hitachi UH4150). The broadband femtosecond transient absorption spectra were taken using the Ultrafast System HELIOS TA spectrometer. All the samples were encapsulated with quartz flakes during these optical measurements. The in situ absorbance measurements were tested on a Quartz Flow-Through Semi-Micro Cuvette with a 2 mm slit. The fluorescence microscopy images were tested under a laser scanning confocal microscope (Nikon). The temperature-dependent low-frequency Raman spectra were collected using a home-built microscope. A 633-nm helium–neon laser (N-LHP-925, Newport) was filtered from undesirable amplified spontaneous emission using a volume Bragg grating (VBG) bandpass filter (Optigrate, linewidth 5 cm−1). To minimize the Rayleigh scattered light, the collected signal was sent through the VBG reflecting filter and passed through three VBG notch filters (Optigrate, optical density > 3) before being dispersed in the spectrograph. The X-ray diffraction patterns were collected by using a PANalytical X’Pert Pro X-ray powder diffractometer with Cu Kα radiation (wavelength λ = 1.54050 Å). To improve the signal-to-noise ratio, the precursor solution concentration was magnified. The 1H NMR measurements were recorded on a Bruker-400 MHz NMR. The samples were scraped off after spin-coating and redissolved in the solvent for testing. To improve the signal-to-noise ratio, the OEGA concentration was magnified. The morphology of perovskite films was measured using a scanning electron microscope (S4800) and an atomic force microscope (Bruker). The X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy (XPS) data were obtained by an Axis Ultra XPS spectrometer (Kratos) with Kα radiation of Al, and operated at 150 W. XPS and ultraviolet photoelectron spectroscopy were obtained by an Axis Ultra XPS spectrometer (Kratos) with Al Kα radiation and ionizing helium I, respectively. The current density–voltage–luminance curve, EQE and operational stability measurement of our perovskite LEDs and the PLQY measurement were conducted using a commercialized system (LQE-100-EL, Enlitech) equipped with an integrated sphere and a photomultiplier tube, in which the photomultiplier tube was used to measure the low luminance up to 10−4 cd m−2, ensuring accurate measurement of luminance. The start and stop voltages of the EQE measurement were set at 2.8 V and 6 V, respectively, with a voltage step of 0.1 V, a dwell time of 100 ms and a delay of 100 ms. All the device characterization tests of perovskite LEDs were recorded at room temperature in the nitrogen-filled glovebox for the unencapsulated devices. To confirm the measurement results, third-party laboratory cross-checks were performed.

Transmission electron microscopy (TEM) samples were prepared by spin-coating precursor solutions onto carbon-coated copper grids, followed by thermal annealing under conditions consistent with those used for thin-film fabrication. To evaluate the potential influence of the underlying layers, additional TEM samples were prepared by spin-coating perovskite layers on ITO substrates pre-coated with the respective transport layers, followed by gentle scraping and redispersion in toluene before drop-casting onto copper grids. TEM characterization was carried out using an aberration-corrected microscope (JEOL Grand ARM 300) operated at 300 kV. To minimize beam-induced damage of perovskite nanocrystals, the samples were tested with a pixel detector-based ultralow-dose electron imaging camera (Gatan, K2). Particle size analysis was conducted using Nano Measurer software. Multiple TEM images collected from different regions of the grid and from different samples were used for statistical analysis. The particle size was defined based on the edge length of the cubic nanocrystals, and overlapping or aggregated particles were excluded from the analysis. In total, more than 100 particles were analysed for each sample.

Liquid-phase TEM images were collected with a JEM-2100 Plus (HC) microscope with a Gatan OneviewIS camera operated at 200 kV. Quartz capillaries (outer diameter 1.0 mm, inner diameter 0.7 mm, length 10 cm, Sutter Instrument) of liquid-phase electron microscopy were positioned on a CO2-laser-based puller (P-2000, Sutter Instrument). A typical two-line protocol was used to generate nanopipette liquid cells with conical shape: (1) HEAT: 835; FIL: 5; VEL: 30; DEL: 128; PUL: 90, (2) HEAT: 865; FIL: 4; VEL: 25; DEL: 132; PUL: 195. The precursor solution was injected into a quartz capillary with a nanosized conical tip from the open end and left undisturbed for 3 h to allow precursor diffusion to the nano-tip. The tip (0.5–1 mm in length) was cut and sealed with vacuum grease at the open end, then loaded onto a carbon-coated TEM grid to create a nanopipette liquid cell.

The temperature-dependent photoluminescence spectra were recorded on an FLS980 (Edinburgh Instruments) equipped with a liquid-nitrogen-cooled cryostat (Oxford Instruments, OptistatDN-V); a 375-nm laser was used as the excitation source. The extracted FWHM of the photoluminescence spectra are plotted as a function of temperature and fitted with the independent Boson model39–41:

$$\Gamma(T)=\Gamma_0+\Gamma_{\mathrm{ac}}+\Gamma_{\mathrm{LO}}+\Gamma_{\mathrm{imp}}=\Gamma_0+\gamma_{\mathrm{ac}}T+\frac{\gamma_{\mathrm{LO}}}{e^{E_{\mathrm{LO}}/k_{\mathrm{B}}T}-1}+\gamma_{\mathrm{imp}}e^{-E_b/k_{\mathrm{B}}T} \quad (1)$$

Here \(\Gamma_0\) is a temperature-independent inhomogeneous broadening coefficient. \(\Gamma_{\mathrm{ac}}\) and \(\Gamma_{\mathrm{LO}}\) are electron–phonon coupling coefficients arising from acoustic (ac) and longitudinal-optical (LO) phonon scattering, with the corresponding coupling strengths of \(\gamma_{\mathrm{ac}}\) and \(\gamma_{\mathrm{LO}}\). \(E_{\mathrm{LO}}\) represents the LO phonon energy. \(\Gamma_{\mathrm{imp}}\) accounts for scattering from ionized impurities with the average binding energy \(E_b\) and the coefficient \(\gamma_{\mathrm{imp}}\), which is exceedingly small and can be neglected in the fitting. \(k_{\mathrm{B}}\) is the Boltzmann constant. As the electron-carrier interaction is dominated by high-energy LO phonons in the high-temperature regime41,48, we fit the FWHM data based on LO phonon modes.

Temperature-dependent conductivity measurements were conducted using a Keithley 2400 and the temperature was controlled by a Cryo Industries Liquid Nitrogen Dewars with Lake Shore model 335 cryogenic temperature controller. Perovskite films were fabricated on glass substrates and 80-nm gold electrodes with spacing of 50 μm were subsequently deposited. The ion activation energy (\(E_a\)) can be derived by fitting the conductivity curve with Nernst–Einstein equation:

$$\sigma(T)=\frac{\sigma_0}{T}\exp\!\left(-\frac{E_a}{k_{\mathrm{B}}T}\right) \quad (2)$$

where \(\sigma\) is the conductivity, \(T\) is the temperature, \(E_a\) is the activation energy and \(k_{\mathrm{B}}\) is the Boltzmann constant.

Computational section

Density functional theory calculations. The molecular clusters were investigated using density functional theory (DFT) calculations with ADF49,50 using AMS202351. Slater Type Orbitals with TZP basis functions were used for all atoms52, treating the lighter atoms (H, C, O and S) with an all-electron basis set, whereas the cores for the heavier atoms were kept frozen (Br 1s–3p and Pb 1s–4d). The Zero Order Regular Approximated (ZORA) Hamiltonian was used to include spin–orbit coupling53. Exchange-correlation interactions between the electrons were modelled with the Perdew, Burke and Ernzerhof (PBE) functional54 to which we explicitly added dispersive interactions through the Becke-Johnson (BJ) damped DFT-D3 scheme55. Structures are visualized using OVITO56.

The binding energy of molecules on a perovskite surface and phase-stability calculations were investigated using DFT calculations with the projector-augmented wave method as implemented in the Vienna Ab-initio Simulation Package (VASP)57–59. The outermost electrons of H (1s1), C (2s22p2), N (2s22p3), O (2s22p4), Br (4s24p5), Cs (5s25p66s1) and Pb (6s26p2) were treated as valence electrons. In all calculations, the plane-wave basis set was expanded to 500 eV with Γ-centred k-meshes60. For the binding-energy calculations, we modelled the exchange-correlation interactions using the PBE + D3(BJ) functional54,55. The phase stability calculation used the PBEsol functional61, and the changes in the bandgap were calculated with the r2SCAN meta-GGA functional62. The Gibbs free energy was calculated from \(G=E_{\mathrm{ele}}+ZPE+pV+\Delta U_{(0\to T)}-TS\) by VASPKIT63, here \(E_{\mathrm{ele}}\) is the electronic energy, ZPE is the zero-point energy, p is the pressure, V is the volume, \(\Delta U_{(0\to T)}\) is the thermal correction to the internal energy from 0 K to temperature T, and S is the entropy.

The structural models for the binding-energy calculations were created from a bulk structure of the cubic phase of CsPbBr3, which was optimized with a 6 × 6 × 6 k-mesh. Slabs with a thickness of 6 octahedral layers and with a PbBr2-termination were made from 2 × 2 supercells in the lateral dimension with 25 Å of vacuum. The perovskite slabs with the adsorbed molecule were optimized using a 3 × 3 × 1 k-mesh by allowing the ionic positions to change. Models were visualized using OVITO56.

To assess the phase stability, we optimized the structures of CsBr and PbBr2, the precursors of CsPbBr3, as well as the different phases of the perovskite, that is, cubic, tetragonal and orthorhombic. The reciprocal space of the precursors was sampled with an 8 × 8 × 8 and 4 × 7 × 3 k-mesh for CsBr and PbBr2, and a 6 × 6 × 6, 4 × 4 × 6 and 4 × 4 × 3 k-mesh was used for the cubic, tetragonal and orthorhombic phase of CsPbBr3, respectively. The various compounds were optimized by allowing the ionic positions, cell shape and cell volume to change. The optimized perovskite geometries were then used to create slabs with CsBr or PbBr2 termination and a thickness of 8 octahedral layers with 25 Å of vacuum. The reciprocal space of these slabs was sampled using a 6 × 6 × 1, 4 × 4 × 1 and 4 × 4 × 1 k-mesh for the cubic, tetragonal and orthorhombic phases of CsPbBr3, respectively. Mixed cation systems were created from the optimized unit cells of the cubic and orthorhombic phases of CsPbBr3, replacing Cs+ with EA+ in a model structure with eight octahedral units. These systems were optimized using a 3 × 3 × 3 and 2 × 4 × 3 k-mesh for the cubic and orthorhombic phases, respectively. During optimization of the ionic positions, cell shape and cell volume were allowed to change.

Molecular dynamics simulations

Molecular dynamics simulations were done using the General Amber Force Field (GAFF)64 as implemented in AMS202351. Model systems were created using PACKMOL65. The atom types and bond types in the model systems were determined using the antechamber module66 based on data obtained from MOPAC calculations67. In the simulations, a time step of \(\Delta t=0.5\,\text{fs}\) was used. The damping constants for the thermostat and barostat were \(\tau_T=100\,\text{fs}\) and \(\tau_p=2500\,\text{fs}\), respectively. The systems were equilibrated with a Berendsen thermostat and Berendsen barostat68, whereas the production runs made use of an NHC thermostat69 and an MTK barostat70. For the barostats, an equal scaling for all dimensions (x, y and z) was used to prevent a collapse of the simulation cell in one of the periodic directions. For both pure solvent systems and polymer–solvent systems and equilibration time of \(t_{eq}=200\,\text{ps}\) was found to be enough to achieve system equilibration. The production runs were carried out for \(t_{prod}=200\,\text{ps}\) and \(t_{prod}=1\,\text{ns}\) for the pure solvent and polymer–solvent systems, respectively. Simulation snapshots are visualized using OVITO56.

Optical simulation

Three-dimensional finite-difference-time-domain simulations were performed to calculate the theoretical outcoupling in planar and patterned PeLEDs. We established a structure of glass (n = 1.5)/ITO (n = 1.8, 110 nm)/m-PEDOT:PSS (n = 1.63, 15 nm)/perovskite (n = 2.10/2.01, 20 nm)/TPBi (n = 1.7, 40 nm)/Al (100 nm). The real part (n) and imaginary part (k) of the refractive index of the m-PEDOT:PSS and perovskite layers were obtained from an ellipsometer and others were from the literature13. The nanostructure of the patterned OEGA/PEA films was modelled with the detailed surface morphologies from the scanning electron microscope and atomic force microscope images. Multiple simulations with different polarizations were carried out to simulate the incoherent isotropic light source. The total radiated power (P) was obtained according to the following equation71:

$$P=\Theta P_{\parallel}+(1-\Theta)P_{\perp}=\Theta(P_x^s+P_y^s)+(1-\Theta)P_z^s \quad (3)$$

where \(\Theta\) is the horizontal dipole ratio defining the orientation of transition dipole moments, and \(P^s\) represents the radiated power contributed by dipoles polarized along the corresponding spatial direction. For isotropic emitters that possess randomly oriented dipoles with equal probability for all directions, \(\Theta\) equals 2/3.

Supplementary Note 1: Interaction energies

Molecular binding energies

To assess the binding strength of a various molecules including the polymers and solvents to PbBr2, we calculate the binding energy \(E_b\) as

$$E_b=\frac{E_{\mathrm{complex}}-E_{\mathrm{PbBr}_2}-n_{\mathrm{molecule}}\cdot E_{\mathrm{molecule}}}{n_{\mathrm{molecule}}}$$

where \(E_{\mathrm{complex}}\) is the total energy of the complex in which the molecules are bonded, \(E_{\mathrm{PbBr}_2}\) is the energy of an isolated PbBr2 molecule, \(E_{\mathrm{molecule}}\) is the energy of an isolated molecule and \(n_{\mathrm{molecule}}\) is the number of molecules coordinating PbBr2 in the complex. For all complexes the geometry was optimized from several starting configurations, the most stable complexes were used in the determination of the binding energies.

Organic ligands

To probe the interaction strength of the organic ligands with the perovskite precursor, we compute the binding energies for various ligand conformations with PbBr2. Comparing the interaction energies of OEGA (n = 9) and a shorter model system (n = 5), we find minimal differences in the binding strength between PbBr2 and the organic ligands, highlighting the local nature of the O-Pb bonds. As a result of this, we use the shorter model system to compute the binding energies associated with the various binding modes of the organic ligands. Supplementary Fig. 9 provides an overview of the different binding modes between organic ligand and PbBr2, their respective binding energies can be found in Supplementary Table 2. The binding energies indicate that the formation of additional O-Pb bonds between the organic ligands and inorganic PbBr2 provide additional stabilization for the molecular complexes that is facilitated by the flexibility of the organic backbone of the ligands.

Solvent molecules

To investigate the binding energies of DMSO molecules to PbBr2, we progressively add more DMSO molecules until the lead atom in PbBr2 is fully coordinated (\(n_{\mathrm{DMSO}}\)). In this fully coordinated geometry Pb forms six bonds arranged in an octahedral shell around the Pb in the center: two Br-Pb bonds and four O-Pb bonds. The most stable complexes are shown in Supplementary Fig. 10 with their respective binding energies and the energy difference associated with the adsorption of an additional DMSO molecule (\(\Delta E_{\mathrm{complex}}\)) reported in Supplementary Table 3. The successive adsorption of solvent molecules diminishes the energy gain per molecule, as demonstrated by \(\Delta E_{\mathrm{complex}}\), which is likely the result of repulsive interactions that arise from the packing of solvent molecules around the PbBr2 molecule.

Solvent exchange reaction

The reaction enthalpy for the solvent exchange reaction was modeled by comparing the energies of PbBr2 complexes in which PbBr2 is completely coordinated by DMSO molecules against complexes in which a part of the PbBr2 molecule is coordinated by the organic ligand. Both DMSO and the polymer can bond to PbBr2 through O-Pb bonds, as shown in Supplementary Fig. 11. We compute the reaction enthalpy \(\Delta H\) as

$$\Delta H=E_{\mathrm{complex}}^{\mathrm{p}}-E_{\mathrm{complex}}^{\mathrm{s}}$$

with \(E_{\mathrm{complex}}^{\mathrm{p}}\) and \(E_{\mathrm{complex}}^{\mathrm{s}}\) the energies of the molecular complexes in which the organic ligand and DMSO bind PbBr2, respectively. In the calculations we used shorter chain lengths (n = 3, 5) than for OEGA used in the nanocrystal synthesis (n = 9), to prevent any reorientations of the organic ligand far away from the PbBr2 from influencing the reaction enthalpy of solvent exchange. For both complexes we optimized the cluster geometry using different starting points, using the most stable configurations for both (Supplementary Fig. 11), we find an enthalpy for solvent exchange of +0.12 eV and +0.09 eV for n = 3 and n = 5, respectively.

Surface adsorption

To assess the ability of OEGA-like molecules to the surface of CsPbBr3 nanocrystals, we compute the adsorption energies shorter molecules (n = 1; i.e. P-MOA) to the surface of CsPbBr3. We compute this adsorption energy \(E_{\mathrm{ads}}\) as

$$E_{\mathrm{ads}}=E_{\mathrm{complex}}-E_{\mathrm{molecule}}-E_{\mathrm{surface}}$$

where \(E_{\mathrm{complex}}\) is the energy of the molecule bonded to the surface, \(E_{\mathrm{molecule}}\) is the energy of the isolated molecule and \(E_{\mathrm{surface}}\) is the energy of the isolated surface. For the optimized bonded geometry, we only allow the ions in the molecule to relax. The resulting geometries, shown in Supplementary Fig. 12, have adsorption energies that range from −0.53 eV to −0.71 eV. The adsorption energies of a single adsorption site are slightly smaller compared to the binding energies with PbBr2 molecules (Supplementary Table 3). However, it is interesting to note that on perovskite surfaces, multiple adsorption sites are available, and the adsorption energy per ligand increases with the number of available sites for O-Pb bonds.

Supplementary Note 3: Nanocrystal phase stability

Nanocrystal energy

To identify the most stable phase of perovskite nanocrystals of a specific size, we compare the total energy of the nanocrystals with different phases following the method proposed by Yang and Tan19. The total energy of a nanocrystal contains a bulk and a surface contribution, which we calculate per formula unit as

$$E_{\mathrm{NC}}=\Delta_{\mathrm{bulk}}+\Delta_{\mathrm{surf}}.$$

The bulk contribution \(\Delta_{\mathrm{bulk}}\) is the perovskite formation energy per formula unit, which is defined as

$$\Delta_{\mathrm{bulk}}=E_{\mathrm{f}}=E_{\mathrm{CsPbBr}_3}-E_{\mathrm{CsBr}}-E_{\mathrm{PbBr}_2},$$

where \(E_{\mathrm{CsPbBr}_3}\), \(E_{\mathrm{CsBr}}\), and \(E_{\mathrm{PbBr}_2}\) are the total enthalpies of the materials from DFT calculations. Assuming cubic nanocrystals, the energy contribution of the surface \(\Delta_{\mathrm{surf}}\) is written as

$$\Delta_{\mathrm{surf}}=\frac{2d^2\cdot\left(E_{\mathrm{surf},1}+E_{\mathrm{surf},2}+E_{\mathrm{surf},3}\right)}{d^3/V_{\mathrm{unit}}}=\frac{2V_{\mathrm{unit}}\cdot\left(E_{\mathrm{surf},1}+E_{\mathrm{surf},2}+E_{\mathrm{surf},3}\right)}{d},$$

where \(E_{\mathrm{surf},j}\) (j = 1, 2, or 3) is the surface energy, \(d\) is the diameter of the nanocrystal, and \(V_{\mathrm{unit}}\) the unit cell volume of the perovskite phase. The surface energy is calculated as20

$$E_{\mathrm{surf},j}=\frac{1}{2A_j}\left(E_{\mathrm{slab},j}-N_{\mathrm{unit}}E_{\mathrm{bulk}}-\sum_i n_i\mu_i\right),$$

where \(A\) is the surface area of the slab, \(E_{\mathrm{slab},j}\) is the total energy of the slab from DFT, \(N_{\mathrm{unit}}\) is the number of formula units in the bulk, \(E_{\mathrm{bulk}}\) is the DFT total energy of the bulk, \(n_i\) is the number of species at the surface, and \(\mu_i\) is the chemical potential of the species at the surface termination. For the cubic phase, all surfaces are identical \(\left(E_{\mathrm{surf},1}=E_{\mathrm{surf},2}=E_{\mathrm{surf},3}\right)\), because of the highly symmetric structure. This similarity is lifted in lower-symmetry phases, such as the tetragonal and orthorhombic phase, with the different surfaces having varying energies.

For stable perovskite phases, the chemical potential \(\mu_i\) must satisfy the following relations19,21

$$\mu_{\mathrm{CsBr}}+\mu_{\mathrm{PbBr}_2}=E_{\mathrm{CsPbBr}_3}$$ $$\mu_{\mathrm{CsBr}}\leq E_{\mathrm{CsBr}}$$ $$\mu_{\mathrm{PbBr}_2}\leq E_{\mathrm{PbBr}_2},$$

which prevent CsPbBr3 from decomposing into its precursors, i.e. CsBr or PbBr2. Depending on the conditions, the chemical potential of the species is determined. In a CsBr-rich environment, \(\mu_{\mathrm{CsBr}}=E_{\mathrm{CsBr}}\) and \(\mu_{\mathrm{PbBr}_2}=E_{\mathrm{CsPbBr}_3}-E_{\mathrm{CsBr}}\). For a PbBr2-rich environment, \(\mu_{\mathrm{PbBr}_2}=E_{\mathrm{PbBr}_2}\) and \(\mu_{\mathrm{CsBr}}=E_{\mathrm{CsPbBr}_3}-E_{\mathrm{PbBr}_2}\).

Phase stability

Using the above, the total energies of perovskite nanocrystals can be determined for various phases, surface terminations, and synthesis conditions. By comparison of the total energies, the most stable nanocrystals can be identified. The parameters used to determine the total energies of the nanocrystals can be found in Supplementary Table 7–8.

For very large nanocrystals (\(d\to\infty\)), the surface contribution becomes negligible, and the total energy of the nanocrystal approximates the formation energy of the bulk, thus predicting the orthorhombic (\(\gamma\)) phase to be most stable. For smaller nanocrystals, the surface contribution starts to play a role. Due to the smaller surface energies of the cubic (\(\alpha\)) phase, the total energy of the orthorhombic and cubic phase starts to compete. Suppose that we grow CsBr-terminated (PbBr2-terminated) surfaces in CsBr-rich (PbBr2-rich) conditions, we find that for nanocrystals of 1.8 nm (2.8 nm) or smaller the cubic (\(\alpha\)) phase is the most stable, as shown in Extended Data Fig. 2. The tetragonal (\(\beta\)) phase, with its higher bulk energy than the \(\gamma\)-phase and its higher surface energy than the \(\alpha\)-phase, has a higher energy than the other phases and therefore is not the most stable phase for any nanocrystal size. Our calculations predict a smaller nanocrystal size at which the cubic becomes more stable than in the experiments (10 nm in experiments). However, as shown in earlier work, the finite temperature dynamics of metal halide perovskites, specifically close to surfaces, aids the formation of cubic domains22. Thus, increasing the critical size below which the cubic phase becomes the most stable phase.

Another factor contributing to the discrepancy found above is the effect of ethylammonium (EA+) on CsPbBr3. To study this effect, we create model structures of the \(\alpha\)- and \(\gamma\)-phase with EA+ replacing Cs+. Due to the large size of the EA+ cations, the cubic phase exhibits a more significant octahedral tilt, whereas this tilt diminishes in the orthorhombic phase. As such, the energy difference of the \(\alpha\)-phase and \(\gamma\)-phase is smaller for the EA+-containing perovskite (Cs0.75EA0.25PbBr3) than for pure CsPbBr3 (Supplementary Table 9). Therefore, the energy of \(\alpha\)-Cs0.75EA0.25PbBr3 shifts down compared to that of \(\alpha\)-CsPbBr3 (Extended Data Fig. 2), remaining stable for much larger nanocrystal sizes. We propose that the combination of the surface and bulk effects discussed above may account for the relatively large sizes of the cubic phases observed experimentally.

We note that our mixed perovskite model that incorporates EA+ does not predict the \(\gamma\)-phase is most stable at any nanocrystal size. This outcome is likely due to the overstabilization of the \(\alpha\)-phase, which may stem from the simplified unit cell used in DFT calculations. Furthermore, changes in surface energies arising from more complex surface chemistry, which were not considered in this study, could further influence phase stability.
